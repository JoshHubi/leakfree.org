I’m against abortion in any case. The reason for this is because I view the thing growing inside of you will always be less blameworthy than anyone else involved in its conception, including the mother’s. In regular cases, there is a 100% sure-way to avoid having a baby, so I think by ignoring that, you accept the risk (no matter how low the risks [using a condom] might be). I’m not saying you shouldn’t fuck, I’m just saying maybe you shouldn’t if you aren’t ready to accept the consequences associated with the risks. If you’re in high school, college, ain’t go no money, then where were all those realizations before you participated in the “risky business”? Derp. Again, you took the risks knowing the possible consequences. I say that as a risk-taking college student. That covers at least 95% of the cases of abortion. I also think it’s a cop-out when people attempt to excuse all abortions by highlighting its usage in rare, extreme cases.

If the claim is that the impregnated person was too young/didn’t know any better, well… now they do.

In the extreme cases of rape/incest, the mother is ostensibly blameless, however, they still had a choice somewhere down the line (they could’ve taken another route home or ran away, even if there was no real motivation for them to do so). The baby/embryo/thing–whatever you want to call it–isn’t only less blameworthy, it is–literally–perfectly blameless. It never had a choice in the matter, or any at all. Pushing undo on life like it’s a computer seems neither fair nor humane, even if someone else seems to have caused the screw-up. If the kid grows up and chooses to abort himself, then that’s his choice (though, I also think that’s wrong, but that’s another matter).

That said, I do not agree with legislating morality, as I’m something like an anarcho-libertarian (I’m not quite sure how far I want to take the anarchy part of it, yet). I view it to be wrong, but I’m not going to force my view on anyone else, nor wish it to be forced in some other capacity. I’m also big on forgiveness, so I’m not going to hate the person for it or wish them harm. I just think the act itself is wrong. I’m also against the death penalty in all cases, if you were wondering (as many anti-abortion people are, which I find to be inconsistent). I think that abortion is merely one of the many sad aspects of our demoralized, completely irresponsible society. There really isn’t much personal responsibility anymore, and when one is confronted with it forcibly, they react as if they had no idea it even existed. There are also other societal reasons that I disagree with it, but that is also another matter.

It’s a very rare/odd position, I know.
