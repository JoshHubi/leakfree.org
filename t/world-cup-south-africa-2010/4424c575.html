Football is about scoring goals, not making shiny dribbles or long passes. They are more amusing to see, yes, but efficiency is what qualifies as “good football” to me, not the “prettiness” of their plays. Although I do enjoy watching that sort of play (I’m a big fan of Ronaldinho for his style of playing, for example), if it serves no good purpose, as in it doesn’t help your team to score/win, I find it to be pretty pointless, and doesn’t qualify as “playing good” to me at all. And to be honest, Spain’s football wasn’t even that amusing to watch to me. They had no big international star in their teams (not one like Messi/Ronaldinho/Zidane anyway, who are known for their distinct playstyles), but they still outplayed Zidane’s France (enough to tie them for penalty shootouts at least) and Ballack’s/Klose’s Germany to win it all.

Basically, if a team wins a match, and they deserved it (as in the referee didn’t “help” them in some sort of way), they are the better team to me, regardless of how many bicycle kicks and 60m passes the other team managed to pull off.
