Malidictus November 25, 2018, 2:20am #51

I honestly expected basically “Half-Life with better graphics.” That’s my own fault for no being better informed and just guessing blindly. Beyond this, I expected Black Mesa to look good, play better but be plagued with bad voice acting. I also expected it to be complete.

What actually happened:

  1. Black Mesa turned out to be a “reimagining” of Half-Life with a lot more humour (not something I appreciate) and very different location layouts in places. I am not disappointed with anything that’s been done to the levels themselves, I just wish there were more of it Specifically, more On a Rail, more Surface Tension, more Forget About Freeman, more Residue Processing. All of these feel like they were “streamlined” and had large chunks of them taken out in the process. Basically, I like it enough that I wish there were more

  2. Black Mesa does look good. VERY good, in fact. And the voice acting doesn’t suck. Quite on the contrary - with the exception of a single line, the voice acting is GREAT! Eli doesn’t sound much like himself but Cleiner is spot on, and all the other unnamed characters sound convincing and well-acted. I’m quite amazed at this. Black Mesa is the first non-commercial product I’ve seen come out with voice acting that doesn’t make me want to strangle the actors. You guys should be making money out of this.

  3. I found out that Xen wouldn’t be included pretty early on, and I found out that the tutoria wouldn’t exist. Xen, at least, we’ll eventually see, but I’m still sad to lose the tutorial. To me, Half-Life is the only game where the tutorial actually felt like part of the story. Ah, well, at least Xen, I’m sure, will be amazing, if the rest of Black Mesa is anything to go by. I just hope it doesn’t take another eight years
