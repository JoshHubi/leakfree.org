I like the idea of this thread, being a WWII history buff, and I just wanted to throw in my 2 cents. German engineering is, and was, exceptional. But there are some very blatant engineering blunders that the Germans let slip during the second world war. One of the biggest (haha) problems was with the largest tank produced by Germany during the WWII. The King Tiger, or Tiger II, was plagued by so many problems that the Russians actually couldn’t understand why the Germans would even make it. More of the tanks were lost due to mechanical problems and lack of fuel than to enemy action. The steel used in that tank’s armour was of a lack luster quality which led to catastrophic failure in some cases. It also used an engine that was used in the Tiger I, which was actually too weak for the original Tiger; this meant the King Tiger had a top speed that was incredibly low. For the price of one overly large and unreliable Konigstigre, Germany could have produced 1 1/2 much more effective Tigers.
