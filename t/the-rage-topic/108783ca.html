I had someone else, stand buy her, in case she decided to get up and walk out on the highway. I knelt down behind her, and then began to do a basic check to see if she had any head trama, neck injuries, or broken bones. As her hand had stopped bleeding, I was not as concerned for that. I then wrapped up her mangled hand, and took vitals.

Always remember this. ABC

A Airways
B Breathing
C Circulation.

Always that order of importance. ALWAYS.

If the person has an obstructed airway, they will be history rather quickly.

If their airway is open, but they are not breathing, then that is next - need the oxygen to the brain - (2 breaths into their mouth, and then 30 chest compressions - repeat until either they revive, help arrives, or you simply can’t continue) (( By the way… the beat for Saturday Night Fever is consider the perfect speed to do chest compressions - also, do not be afraid to break the breast bone when doing chest compressions - It is common that the ribs will disconect from breast bone, so not the end of the world if you break a few bones - As long as you do not punch them through a lung.))
