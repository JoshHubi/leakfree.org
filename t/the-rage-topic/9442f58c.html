(rot)(fodder:

You’ve got people fighting to destroy the idea that men and women are inherently different inside and then you’ve got these assholes running around saying “but I feel like a girl inside” or “I feel like a man inside” “don’t judge me”

This issue isn’t as black and white as that. What makes a person a man or a woman “inside?”

What you learn in biology class in high school is that sex in humans is determined by the sex chromosomes, a pair of chromosomes called X and Y that are named for their shapes. Women have the genotype XX, and males have the genotype XY. Men and women each donate half of their genome to a child when they conceive. Women will donate one of their two X chromosomes, and men will donate either their X chromosome of their Y chromosome, so it’s the man’s gamete that determines the sex of the child.

That’s if everything goes perfectly.

You might be familiar with a condition called trisomy 21, which occurs when one parent accidentally donates two copies of chromosome 21 instead of one copy. The condition is more commonly known as Down’s syndrome. And if one gamete accidentally had two copies of chromosome 21, that means that there’s another gamete that’s missing its copy of chromosome 21. If that gamete happened to be the one that was involved in conception, then that child would be inviable, and would miscarry very early in the pregnancy, probably before the woman even realized she was pregnant.

The same types of chromosomal shenanigans can happen with the sex chromosomes - and they often aren’t fatal. There are people out there whose genotypes aren’t XX or XY. They can be X (I’m remembering from high school/college genetics classes, but I believe that these people are anatomically female but then don’t develop secondary female sex characteristics at puberty), XXY (anatomically male, but develop female secondary sex characteristics at puberty, like widened hips and enlarged breasts), XXX (quote-unquote “super females”), and probably others that I don’t remember.

Are these people “men inside” or “women inside?”

You also get the cases of people born with hermaprhoditic or indeterminate physiology at birth. Often the doctor and their parents choose for them, electing for a corrective surgery that makes them more unambiguously gendered. “Men inside” or “women inside?” Could their parents have chosen incorrectly?

All that said, I’m not convinced that many or most transsexuals aren’t suffering from a gender dysphoria. Dysphorias are a class of mental illness in which the patient is made profoundly uneasy or uncomfortable about something that they perceive as a flaw. The poster children for dysphoria are anorexia and bulimia, in which the patient feels that they are too fat, whether or not they actually are, and overreact/overcompensate by starving themselves or throwing up their food. There’s another dysphoria colloquially termed “bigorexia” in which the patient feels they are too scrawny and not fit enough, and so they spend an unhealthy amount of time exercising or doing unhealthy exercises, and damage their bodies that way.

Even if you think I’m wrong, I’m sure you can forgive me for thinking that transsexuality sure looks like a lot like a dysphoria. If transsexuality is a dysphoria in these people, then I think we should be treating it, not enabling it. If it’s not, then great, live and let live. In either case, I don’t hate, nor am I made uncomfortable by, transsexuals.
