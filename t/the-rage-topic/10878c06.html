More often than not, I find poor-grammar intolerance to be elitist, for the judgement must be cast most harshly on those who speak the language natively. It is not beyond expectation that an individual should have the capacity to master one tongue.

But when it comes to foreign learners, sometimes I find the intolerance and xenophobia to be disgusting. It doesn’t have anything to do with races or racism, but it’s a simple distrust of anything not from one’s own familiar culture. One should instead be lenient and appreciative when such a stranger takes the effort to learn some of that language.

While I admit it seems that large portion of Westerners are those who commit such a thoughtcrime, the sample group may be skewed because I only get to observe in English, and you probably only get to communicate toward native English-speakers, and English tends to be the most dominant language on the Internet.

Such an unjust hate can certainly be revealed by speakers of other languages. I once observed an individual be insulted by mixing the word para with por in Spanish (toward vs. through), with Spanish being their tertiary language…

And so, I got to observe the kindest soul I’ve ever met generate a truly, righteously vindictive e-mail, one that openly mocked the native speaker’s inability to appreciate foreignness :3
