malice4you November 24, 2018, 8:23pm #839
zim:

those knifes are so ugly, why would you get so many of them?

Your opinion, you’re welcome to it.

I prefer function over looking “pretty” - which I feel a number of these knives also are. As far as I know, Spyderco were first to patent one handed opening knives, as well as pocket clips on their knives. Most tactical folders to this day copy Spydercos. Spydercos open fast, one handed, and are solid knives. Spydercos offer blade steels that are exceptional, and yet cost far less than other brands using lesser steels.

I’ve had the opportunity to use ZDP-189, N690Co, CPMS30V, VG10, AUS-10, AUS-8, AUS-6, H-1, ATS-55, 8Cr13MoV, GIN-1, 51200, and G-2 steels, which all have different capabilities. H1 for example does not rust, unlike other steels, including many ‘stainless’ steels. ZDP-189 rusts easily, but it is also incredibly hard and holds a sharp edge longer than any other steel I’ve owned. VG-10 and CPM S30V are both great general use steels which resist rusting well, yet take and hold a razor sharp edge.

A number of those knives are also rare, including knives from runs of 3000 to even as low as 600. And finally…I like variety. I switch up what I carry often, and I’m never with just one knife.
