Just finished building this today, I’ve named it the Spudchucker. I have yet to test it, but I can only assume that it works I guess.

https://imageshack.us/g/38/img0646du.jpg/

Consists of 4 inch abs piping for the chamber and 2 inch abs for the barrel. The chamber is actually composed of fittings held together with short pieces of pipe, rather than pipe held together by fittings. This way, the outer wall of the chamber is twice as thick for the most part. Which is comforting considering abs turns into shards when it explodes and would inevitably riddle my body with bits of plastic. I installed a mechanical BBQ igniter (the screws inside the chamber) in the angled handle as a backup in case the electronic igniter (the other set in the chamber) fails, for redundancy. The handles I filled with expanding foam so that hopefully they don’t explode and shred my hands.

The chamber is approx 22 inches long and the barrel is a shade under 4 and a half feet. I used threaded fittings on the end of the chamber and barrel so I could make a different sized barrel in the future if I need to.

Will post a video hopefully once it cures and I can chuck some taters with it. From what I’ve read, I’m hoping for 200 yards or so.
