I had the cheaper version of the first one posted for a long time. The problem with those was that the pots used had bad dust covers allowing specks to collect on the surface of the element beneath the wiper. Turn off the receiver and turn every pot 20 times to the left and the right. Verify the position of all of the dials so that your not going to explode the stereo when powered on. Fli the switch and you should have fewer/no pops. (If it was the Potentiometers that was causing the issues)
