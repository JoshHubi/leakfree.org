OT: I would recommend FCOM Converge, a sort of

Combined, the mod adds thousands of new items and enemies into the game. Also, bugs.
The reason I’m not recommending it is because it’s a bitch to install and the game has a tendency to break down completely.

You should try out Better Cities though.

Shinobi November 25, 2018, 12:27am #14
TheSuperNova:

recommend some mods for me people? first time oblivion playthrough here

My favorite mods:
Qarl’s Harvest- adds fruit to fruit trees
Real lights- the torches and light sources in the game actually become light sources instead of just textures
Badderfire/frost/shock- better looking spells for each of those
Bedrolesanyone- add bedroles to the game
Cliff_betterletters- more variation in the letters found in the game
CM_Better wine- drasticlly better textures for the wine
diversegrasses- more types of grasses
doofdillas_potions_recolored_sorted_v0_3_1- better potion textures
Enhanced Water v2.0 HD- better water
Dungeon Actors Have Torches 1.6 DT- self explanetory
Exterior Actors Have Torches 1.3 DT
Get Wet- in rain, characters will have water streaming down them. this one is only ok
ImprovedSigns
IWR-Lights/shutters/windows- better textures
Leviathan Soulgems-really cool soulgems
Oblivion Cats v0.22b-cats
P1DkeyChain-really nice, condenses your key chain
spelliconreplace-optional
Symphony of Violence-much better sounds for everything
Unofficial Oblivion Patch-this is required, there is also one for shivering isles if u have that
VA_BetterGold-much nicer money
Visually Realistic Deadly Lava
qarl’s texture pack- much better textures for a lot of things, dont remember them all

There are also some mods that make things look better when they are far away, i dont remember what they are called. google Oblivion LOD to find them.

All of the things above are just mods to make Oblivion more visually appealing, they don’t affect gameplay, since IMO the game is really really good the way it is. I also have the modular beautiful people mods installed as well as xeorace which adds a TON of new races, but everything is more animeish. You should def play it vanilla before adding things like that.

One thing a lot of mods require are the script extender, which allows for bigger mods, and the mod manager, which lets you reorder the load order of the mods which is required to make some work correctly together.
