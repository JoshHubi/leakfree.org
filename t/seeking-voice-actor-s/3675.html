I sent you a pm, but I’m posting here as well just in case.

It sounds like you’re not using your head voice at all, you need at least some reverberation in your sinuses. You don’t need much for Kyle’s voice but make sure you can hear some slight modulation if you plug your nose while impersonating. Another reason you’re not getting as rich a sound is you’re focusing on putting in fake gruffness in your throat rather than just dropping your voice and pulling back on the volume. Try lowering your adam’s apple (what happens when you yawn), flatten your tongue and pull back your lower jaw when speaking (proper air flow), and relax. This should give you a deeper richer tone.

Remember to relax your neck and throat and let the shape you’re holding do the work. Forcing a low tone out will sound fake. If you have trouble getting that gruffness (for example on “88” where your tongue comes up and restricts air flow) try pushing out your stomach the entire time while both inhaling and speaking. This pushes your other organs out of the way so your diaphragm can work more easily. Hopefully this action will help you keep a low volume (as to maintain the gruffness) but also be strong enough to be understood clearly.

Sorry if that’s not much help. The voice is a hard thing to control. I find realistically lower your speaking pitch is one of the hardest things to do convincingly.
