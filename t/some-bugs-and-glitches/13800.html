I can explain!

Source engine has two different kinds of water - Expensive water (real-time reflection and refraction) and cheap water (which uses cubemaps). There are multiple caveats to using water - for instance, both expensive and cheap water can’t be in the same PVS (potentially visible set). Another one is that, in a PVS, if you’ve got expensive water, it has to be on the same level.

What the level designer did here was split the visible areas along the “top” of the dam (so that you can’t see one water pool from the other) and create a “cheap” water brush with a blue texture on it to mask any oddities going on in the lower pool when the player’s looking down from the top of the dam. If you zoom in, it fades out.

It’s an inherent engine limitation, as is my understanding, so I don’t think there’s really much that can be done to “fix” it, per se (though I’d like to say, props to whoever came up with that workaround, it’s clever).
