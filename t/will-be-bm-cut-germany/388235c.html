You’re so wrong. The USK doesn’t censor games, developers do. And they do it prematurey these times around I’m afraid. It’s like this: If the USK rates the game a 18+, everythings fine and nothing can happen to the game i.e. nothing has to be censored. If the USK does not approve even for a 18+ rating (nazi references or other things like that), the game could come on the index, if the BPJM (Bundesprüfstelle für Jugendgefährende Medien, Federal Department for Media Harmful to Young Persons) decides to. If that happens, the game must not be advertised or even sold openly in a store. So naturally, developers censor the game, to avoid getting on the index. But most games don’t even get to the USK, because it costs a fee to let them be rated. So they censor the shit out of their games to not have them be rated again. And I think most of the games wouldn’t have to worry about their rating, developers are just afraid. Dead Space is completly uncensored in germany.

So, enough about that.
