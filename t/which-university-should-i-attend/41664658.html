Note-anator November 24, 2018, 9:18pm #32

The bottom line is the utility of what you will learn/retain when you are finished.

You have some questions to ask yourself in this process and really, only you can answer them.

I suppose a few things to consider is the enviroment you will be in. Coming from a well known school does not count for jack if what you end up with a mere name. On the other hand, a school with a good rep is a fractor, in that, they have a good name for a reason. Only you will be able to balance the schools name compared to how you measure up. If you simply want a title, then look for presteige but keep in mind, that then you run the risk of the WHO"S WHO game. (Not what you konw, but who you konw - Bleh - not so many are good at teh social wheel and deal game -just sayin’)

All fancy education is expensive, so if that is a factor, you need to include that in your final decision.

Is you choose one that is known for parties and goofing off, then that is not so helpful, if a life long career is your goal.

If there is some way to find out what the grads have gone on to accomplish from each school, then that is consideration. If there is a high placement rate, likely, they have a good education program.

Do you have a significant other? How far will any of those take you away from them? If you do, how likely is your emotional concerns going to be an issue for you?

Look to the practicle, not the popular, because your talent in your field will mean more in the long haul, than the glip and glamour of the short haul… Yes?

Only you can deceide, so consider many things and list them good and bad, and then choose from there. Smart cookie for looking early.
