Kairouseki:

I don’t see anything wrong with Portal 2 but I see a hell of a lot of people saying it sucked for some reason.

I don’t feel it sucked, per se, but I felt that it really offered little over Portal. See, when I played Portal, I enjoyed the puzzles, and the interwoven story bridging them felt like it came smooth and naturally. However, with Portal 2…I don’t know, maybe my expectations were just different from what the game delivered. I felt like the puzzles, while well done, were too much like the old ones, and were now almost like obstacles to getting more story and jokes, which wasn’t all that great anyway. Plus, since the time spent thinking about the puzzles makes up the difficulty, there’s not really a way to increase or decrease it…as such I completed the game in what I felt was a really short time. Also, I think sending Chell back into Aperture Science made about as much sense as sending Gordon back into Black Mesa. I was really hoping to see a new environment, get out into the world, and do some REALLY awesome new things with portals. Instead, I was…solving test chambers again. Again, the game wasn’t bad at all - but it really was a puzzle game first, and I was hoping it would be more story-oriented this time.

OrderOfDagon makes valid points on the story as well. I agree with your assessment of DE:HR’s story too. It took me some headscratching and a couple playthroughs to get it straight in my head exactly what was going on. So not the best presentation, but I liked the story, and loved the gameplay. Someone was mentioning that certain playstyles were favored…I don’t really see that very much, except for the takedowns. I don’t understand why they needed energy in the first place, and why lethal ones were necessarily penalized. Other than that, everything’s a viable option - sneak around everyone, or shoot everyone (ok, so the pistol is ridiculously OP), or hack turrets to shoot everyone for you, or beat everyone up, or stun everyone…they all seemed to work good for me, especially since the AI is pretty dumb.
