eharsh87 August 11, 2020, 6:00am #73

Don’t know if these games’ll interest anyone here, but the Yume Nikki series, and LSD: Dream Simulator are two of the most distressing games I’ve ever played.

Yume Nikki is an RPG Maker game (but not an RPG, oddly) based on a dream journal (actually, the title directly translates to dream journal, so…). The entire gameplay of the game is spent exploring the dreamscapes of the main character. It varies from slightly odd to actually pretty disturbing. The fan sequels are supposedly much worse in the distrubing content category, though.

LSD is a first person game for the original PlayStation and is similar to Yume Nikki except it’s much more random while YN is a bit more scripted and not as dynamic. (No, it’s not a drug reference. The developers claim it stands for Lovely Sweet Dream.)

Also, the white chamber. Kind of like Event Horizon, insofar as it’s set in space and hellish things happen.

And the Chzo Mythos (5 Days a Stranger, 7 Days a Skeptic, Trilby’s Notes, and 6 Days a Sacrifice), by Yahtzee, back before he sold his soul to the Escapist. (I was a Yahtzee fan before it was cool to be a Yahtzee fan. Anyone else remember Yahtzee Takes on the World?) First horror games I played, and they still get under my skin today. Keep in mind I was like. …13 when I would have first played 5 days. It fucked me up for a while.
