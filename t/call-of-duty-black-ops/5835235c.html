russilker November 24, 2018, 10:54pm #127

Personally, I don’t agree with that assessment. If anything, consoles helped bring in a larger choice of games for PC users.

Think of it this way: if a company spends its resources creating a heavily scripted, “eh” kind of FPS that ends up just being a port from console to PC, can you really believe they would deliver a quality game if consoles didn’t exist? I don’t think so.
IMO, consoles gave video gaming a HUGE boost, which in turn spurred more game companies popping up, more people studying game design in college, more money being spent on video games in general, which, lastly, gave us more games that we never would have had otherwise.

In most cases, if a company is able to make good PC games that aren’t just console ports, they will do so, regardless of whether or not its easier to develop worse games for consoles. Has valve abandoned its PC gamers? No, it just started porting its games to consoles, which gives it more money to create better PC games. Has crytec decided to make Crysis 2 PC just a poorly-coded console port? We’ll find out soon, but signs point to no, and its console Crysis 2 sells will most likely give it more money and more popularity to develop more “Wow”-worthy games, like Crysis was. Obviously, if a well-respected PC game developer went bad and started producing many mediocre console ports, then there’s cause to be concerned, but I just haven’t encountered any cases of this (ok, maybe Rockstar games, but here’s to hoping that GTA V will be a good quality port to PC, plus the PC GTA games were often buggy at launch anyway). Instead, we get the same quality games from the same quality PC game companies as before, plus the choice of other “OK” games to help us pass the time every once in a while.
