exion November 24, 2018, 6:57pm #63

Original Halflife: Awesomeness, but the Xen stages (sans the Gonarch battle) left a bitter taste in my mouth. Nowadays the engine feels quite dated so returning to the game kinda breaks the nostalgia. 8/10

Opposing Force: I actually preferred this to the original, ignore that many of the story elements are non-cannon (though I don’t see why they shouldn’t be as they don’t contradict anything more than Valve contradict themselves)… It had some really imaginative weaponry, and as was said earlier on with unfavourable response: The wrench>the crowbar (it had more of a sense of weight behind it, and I continued to use it even after getting the combat knife)! 9/10

Blueshift: it features some great moments but the lack of awesome new weapons, boss fights or real sense of accomplishment (as well as a really short length) hold it back from greatness. 6/10

Decay: I really enjoyed playing through decay, and it seemed to tie in closer to the originals story than most of the other spinoffs, and I really liked the spin that playing with a friend had on the gameplay, but the disjointed nature of the levels kinda ruined the immersion. Still, the bonus level where you play as a Vort at the end was excellent! “THIEVES, YOU ALL ARE THIEVES, YOU ALL ARE!”. 7/10

Halflife 2: A deep story, great gameplay and lots of variation in location and playing style (with the strategy of using antlions and rebels, and the vehicle sections!) plus epic setpieces… this game rose the bar, but I did feel like it was more of a showcase of the havoc engine than a game at times. 9/10

Episode 1: an enjoyable experience, focused on set pieces and the ability to play through the entire campaign only firing one bullet was a nice novelty, but from a story perspective it seemed pretty directionless (though it did bridge between HL2 and episode 2 quite well). The lack of variation in environments annoyed me a little. 7/10

Episode 2: The story was kicked up a notch and the setpieces really got back on form… the welcome return of bossfights and awesome weapons made this one of my favourite entries of the series to date, plus I really liked how they tied the portal franchise and halflife franchises together. It also added a lot more mystery to the G-man. Win. 10/10

now… I can picture Black Mesa being another 10, assuming it follows all of the setpieces of the original… from what Ive seen so far, it looks like its on track!
