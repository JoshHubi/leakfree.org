Grey_Acumen November 24, 2018, 6:45pm #12

I honestly can’t do a straight numerical rating. Each of them really excels at entirely different objectives.

Half Life set the bar. Op4 and BS are like 2 sides the the whole coin that HL1 was. Opposing Force had a bunch of extra content and nice boss battles, but really killed cannon and I felt it really didn’t capture the “flavor” that made HL1 so enjoyable. Blue Shift felt like it fit into cannon much more smoothly, and I actually enjoyed the fact that I didn’t even have access to the full weapon selection of HL1. The only problem was that it lacked a decent boss battle that made it feel like you really accomplished something.

Half Life 2 raised the bar from HL1. It has literally become the scale by which everything else is compared to. Episode 1 had the problem that the entire story consisted of “run like hell before shit goes down” and you don’t even fully succeed, BUT it took just about all the cool experiences of HL2 and boiled them down into an easily digestible portion; Gravity gun right from the start and Super Gravity gun within 15 - 30 minutes of gameplay, and by the end of the game you take on both a Gunship and a Strider. Episode 2 pretty much threw in everything that Episode 1 had missed including from HL2; drivable vehicles, the Ant Lion Guardian battles, fighting a Chopper, working with the Vortigons, and actually accomplishing something at the end of the game.

so I’m going to go:

HL1 - 9
OP4 - 8
BS - 8

HL2 - 10
E1 - 9
E2 - 9
