If you’re referring to spheres, which I assume you are, nothing in any game you’ve ever played is perfectly spherical. Its just not possible within the limitations of computing. More modern games have more polygons in their spheres, so the illusion of a sphere is less easily seen-through, but at the time of older games such as Half Life no users had computers capable of that caliber of models, and so less polygons were used so that the game would run better.
