Anticitizen 1 isn’t specific to Gordon Freeman, it’s a level of citizen status, kind of like saying “the most wanted” though surely Gordon is the only person to have ever attained it.

Since he’s who they’re referring to when they say it, it makes sense to define it as him, but you’re right, there is more to it than that.

Airwatch I believe refers to Combine Gunships and Helicopters rather than scanners… Scanners are apparently automated, so I don’t think they would take instruction through announcements.

I don’t know, I haven’t heard any announcements that are directly giving orders to the Airwatch. The only references I’ve found to the Airwatch is giving ground units updates as to what it has found or is doing.

Biotics (in Episode 2 refer specifically to Gordon and Alyx) seems to refer to human/earth lifeforms.

Possibly, but it is said in an area that contains an Antlion hive. This leads me to think that it is referring to them, rather that Alyx or Gordon, especially as you hear a soldier on the radio in Nova Prospekt talking about how “Primary Target is engaged in command and control tactics with Biotics”.

Containment Field means the Combine forcefields that do not permit non-combine organisms to pass through.

That’s pretty much what I meant by “shield”. Although it could also refer to the ones that let authorized citizens pass through, that you see in the beginning.

Control Section sounds more as if it refers to a district/designated sector of City 17.

It’s definitely the Citadel, or at least a description of it. “sentences.txt” gives a list of places, used for metropolice radio chatter (e.g. when they die), an extract of which:

// map 8 citadel
V_G1_LOCATION_MAP08P0 highpriorityregion
V_G1_LOCATION_MAP08P1 terminalrestrictionzone
V_G1_LOCATION_MAP08P2 controlsection[/SIZE]

Exogen specifically refers to Antlions.

You can sometimes hear soldiers say “Visual on exogens.” when they see reprogrammed sentry turrets. Of course, this may be an error on the developer’s part.

Anyway… bouncer and boomer don’t sound like Combine dialogue… but perhaps refer to hopper mines and thumpers? I sincerely doubt boomer refers to gunships.

Bouncer definitely means grenades, but Boomer is a bit more speculative. Thumpers are definitely called Restrictors, so I doubt it could be them. My definition is derived from a soldier radio transmission (filename “prison_soldier_boomersinbound.wav”) that you hear in the final bunker before climbing up to Nova Prospekt, which says something that sounds very like “Overwatch confirms double boomers dispatched, inbound. Coast drop inbound. Prepare for special heavy suppression and wrap 'em.”

Since you meet a pair of Gunships very shortly after that, this leads me to believe that the “boomers” that the soldier was talking about were Gunships. And they do make sort of boomey noises.
