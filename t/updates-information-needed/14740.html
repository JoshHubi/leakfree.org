I have explained (to you in particular, several times) why small frequent updates to MP are not particularly realistic for us. Our deployment pipeline is complicated, and Source is even moreso. It takes a significant amount of work on our end to get builds prepped and shipped (we normally have to lock the game down to have no further work done on the deployment branch for AT LEAST 2 weeks or more prior to an update release). Oh and working on branches is a nightmare too. You would be staggered at how much a small change can break X thing, or Y thing, in a completely unrelated way, and how much gets broken when we merge across our branches, which has to be done with every update. Or how often something can be broken on the deployed Steam branch but not on the internal development branch (the workshop in particular has been a horrible culprit of this - works perfectly on our internal builds and then breaks when shipped onto Steam). This was a nightmare to deal with when we launched and were trying to deploy more frequent updates. We’ve learned from our mistakes. We have to keep things manageable on our end as well, not just for us, but for you guys too. We’re going to stick to our model of sizeable content updates with hotfixes for immediate issues, because it’s what works best. You’ll get your MP changes in the next update, I’m actually doing some balancing work on it right now, as it goes.

The game is getting further and further along. Even if it’s slow, you can see the progress with each update. This next update is SP content, but it is awesome. I am so, so excited and can’t wait to get it into your hands. Which should be fairly soon, too!
