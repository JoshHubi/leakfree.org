Why would Gordon need to do HEV suit training when thats what he does every day?

Every scientist using an HEV-Suit has to be trained and introduced at the beginning, so does Gordon Freeman. Like someone already said before the hazard course probably takes place a few months or even a year before the events of Half-Life/Black Mesa. And making the player play another scientist than Gordon Freeman isn’t that good in my opinion because hazard course isn’t just training for the player to get familiar with the suit, but also with the character itself. You’re training as Gordon Freeman so you’re prepared to play Half-Life/Black Mesa as Gordon Freeman. The hazard course helps you to develop your own Gordon Freeman style, so you can use it later in the game.

To bring up my idea for the end of the hazard course: Since it is supposed to bring the basic functions of the suit and situation to the player, I’d change the tram ride at the end to an allround summing up obstacle course including all basic lessons you’ve learned on your way through the course. That’d be generaly moving with the HEV-Suit, jumping, crouching, using the longjump module, climbing ladders, and using a Glock. That way the player can get a feel for how it might go in an emergency case.

So he has to pass by dangerous places like radiation hazards, fire, liquid etc. I’d make it very basic so it looks like the course is supposed to prepare for every possible scenario.

And using a MP5 doesn’t belong to that one, I think, so I’d also rather replace that part with a Glock training on an interesting shooting range. The MP5 is introduced by the HECU and I think an invasion of the military doesn’t belong to possible scenarios, at least no one would think of that at the facility. The Glock is a standard equipment at the BMRF, so this could be trained, though.

For the hologram: I’m also with the idea of replacing it with a voice system, maybe CatzEyes’ voice recordings could turn out very practical for this. I always wondered why they’d place a very expensive holgram at every checkpoint just to tell you how things work and maybe just show one or two excersises.

You’d also have to make a special hologram generator for the longjump part that goes all the way she jumps if you’d want to make her show it as she did in the original, because in Half-Life she could move outside her perimeters. That doesn’t make any sense for me.
