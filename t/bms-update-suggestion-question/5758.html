higgsboson November 24, 2018, 10:46pm #18

Most developers don’t give out many details about development.

Does Valve give monthly updates about how Portal 2’s development is going?
Would we consider it normal for Valve to say “We just completed a chapter in Episode 3, it’s looking really cool but there’s a bug with the elevator that we are fixing.”? It would take them no effort to do so, but they still don’t do it.

This also applies for other companies such as Microsoft or Apple, they don’t tell you how their new OS is going along. They may give you some info just so you know they’re working on it, but you won’t know much until the release. Their only advantage is that they can give you a release date and stick to it, but that’s only because this is all they do all year, so they can actually predict it (but even then they can get it wrong).

More open companies like Google might be - obviously - more open. They often give updates about what they’re working on, but that’s their choice.

It makes sense for the Black Mesa team to not update more often than other companies would. We can actually talk to them on these forums, which is pretty cool in itself. You can’t do that with Valve much.

With a project like this, it’s impossible to predict things and it pisses you off when people act like annoying dogs waiting for food. We would all love updates, and we would all like to know much more about everything, but we have to understand that this is the way the world works, and it’s not just the Black Mesa team that’s keeping information secret.
