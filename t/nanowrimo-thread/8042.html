Flubly November 25, 2018, 1:03am #9

I think it’s more meant as an exercise than a way to write a legitimate novel. The idea is that most people don’t even know where to start or can’t get very far because they’re perfectionists and do 300 drafts of the opening paragraph and nothing else.

I’ve never done it, but it’s a good way for people to just throw away standards and write in bulk. It allows them to get used to the simple action of writing a lot. It makes it easier to start on something more focused and intentional later on. You have to write crap in order to get better and write something really good. Writing takes practice and this is a good way to get a lot in, throw it away, and then start on something worthwhile with no hard deadline.
