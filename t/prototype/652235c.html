MaxTheLimit November 24, 2018, 6:01pm #130

I didn’t want to reveal too much in the review, so I only tiptoed at those parts. But when playing as a character, naturally you try to juxtapose the main characters personality in the game with your own so that you try to succeed in continuing on for both your sakes. It’s hard to do that based on Alex as a person. Motivation in Prototype seems less derived from empathy for his situation than a violent cathartic romp. I’m not saying it’s bad, but it’s unusual. Folks trying to empathize with Alex may find it difficult, and may prefer to get lost in the violent ballet of the game without getting too tied up in the personal aspect.
