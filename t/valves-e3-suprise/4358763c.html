I would have been nicer if you weren’t literally the 10th person saying the exact same thing. For a start, they are trying to broaden the intended demographic. They don’t need to cater to us because we are already going to get the game. They need to cater to people who wouldn’t have gotten the game otherwise, i.e. people whose console wouldn’t run it. Also, they are trying to draw attention to it. Why wouldn’t you hype it? Its just how advertising works.

Regardless though, it did seem pretty insensitive of Valve to deliberately hype up their E3 presentation like that. I wasn’t necessarily expecting Episode 3, but as numerous people have said, the way that they marketed the ‘surprise’ made it seem like something big, as it wouldn’t have made a whole lot of sense to have a surprise about something we already know a lot about. Yes it’s how advertising works, but it’s rare that adverts are so cumbersome and clumsy in their delivery, and that was pretty damn clumsy advertising. Surely they realised that trailing a surprise just before E3 would have meant that most people’s minds would immediately jump to E3?

I’m not disappointed. I half-expected that anyway - I just wish that Valve wouldn’t toy with their fans’ expectations like that. There’s not a whole lot of justification in that, I just wish it. But I do personally think that they should have just stated that they were going to release it for PS3, or stated it in a creative way rather than leading people on like that. It doesn’t do much to their reputation.

I was hoping something for Episode 3 myself - not a trailer, not even necessarily a screenshot. Just a piece of artwork or something enticing that, considering the hype around Episode 3 already, would have got the crowd raving.
