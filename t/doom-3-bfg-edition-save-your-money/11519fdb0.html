I posted this in the screenshot thread but I’ll do it here too.

Go into your folders and open DoomConfig.cfg. Now go through and look for:

seta com_videoRam “2048”

seta image_downSizeLimit “0”
seta image_ignoreHighQuality “0”
seta image_downSizeBumpLimit “0”
seta image_downSizeSpecularLimit “0”
seta image_downSizeBump “0”
seta image_downSizeSpecular “0”

And maybe a few other variables around that area. Some of them are probably wrong. I know that my video ram was set to something negative and most of the second part for me were 1’s.

xalener:

It’s an HD remake because the console version is rendered in HD now

That was the -whole- point of this re-release.

Therefore it’s not a remake, simply an HD upscaling.

A few examples of remakes:

  1. Black Mesa: A remake because they made everything from scratch with more detail than the original, in a completely different engine.

  2. Bionic Commando: Rearmed: A remake because they made everything from scratch by implementing fully polygonal environments, with more detail than the original.

  3. Super Mario All-Stars: Three remakes on one cartridge that implemented entirely different sprites, with much more detail than their NES counterparts.

  4. The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time 3D/Starfox 64 3D: Practically the same games as the N64 originals, but with all new models and textures. Still qualified as remakes.

Doom 3 BFG Edition does not fit any of those qualities as the textures are the same. It’s a rerelease that takes advantage of the upscaling, that this generation’s hardware allows for, from whatever the native resolution was initially.
