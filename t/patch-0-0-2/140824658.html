We believe the Gluon is appropriately powered for MP. We think perhaps that people have not learned how to properly deal with it yet. In our near year and a half of testing MP we did not think the Gluon was overpowered. It is quite a lot weaker than its HL1 variant. It does less damage. It consumes ammo faster. It does not do splash damage like it did in HL1. It does not have a headshot multiplier like it did in HL1.

We believe the issue was partially that players who got the Gluon were typically also able to arm themselves to the teeth with suit battery, making them very hard to kill as well as extremely powerful. We have made it significantly harder for players to accumulate vast amounts of suit battery (and more in line with HL1), so this will alleviate this balance concern slightly.

Bear in mind that a large portion of our arsenal is able to kill a player in one hit, and yet the Gluon cannot. Engaging a Gluon user head on is a very unwise decision, but he’s just as vulnerable as anyone else to a sneakier approach with a Crossbow, or a .357, or explosives, etc. We will continue to collect data and opinions about it and we can and will change it if it is determined to be too strong, but right now we still believe it is in an okay place.

Man that sucks you’re having that issue! It sounds like it will keep re-occuring after every Black Mesa update until Norton fixes the false-positive… but you can also Whitelist the offending file. I don’t have Norton but I found this from another thread:

“Open norton and click the performance tab. Look on the timeline for where it was detected. Mouse over it and click on view details on view details. Then click on the instance in your security history listed as vlocalize.exe. Click on it then under recommended Action click “options” then click “restore & Exclude” then tell it okay. After that verify your install then update. If you get stuck in a verification/validation loop then delete the local files and reinstall.”

We’re still working on Steam Cloud integration, but your save games should be in Steam/SteamApps/Common/Black Mesa/SAVE/ … If you have to delete/reinstall the game, first copy that entire save folder and stick it back in the same spot after the game redownloads, and that should preserve your saves. Worst case, if you do somehow lose your saves, you can open the console ingame and type sv_unlockedchapters “99” and restart the last chapter you tried.

Sorry for all your troubles, I much prefer Windows Defender to Norton Antivirus myself. Let us know if there’s anything else we can do.
