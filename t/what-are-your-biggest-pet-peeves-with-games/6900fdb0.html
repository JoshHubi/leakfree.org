Steagle November 25, 2018, 12:03am #76

Where do I start?

  1. Publishers charging full price for half a game. In the age of lazy developers using multi-player as a crutch, a new pricing model is needed. If you are going to release half a game, release a version stripped up multi-player and vise versa. I don’t do multi-player so I almost always wait until the price drops to a reasonable level. Crysis 2 is the first game I have bought at full price since being burned by COD 4 but then again, I got a $20 pre-order credit plus pre-order pricing. The same with Portal 2 and Fear 3.

  2. Lack of auto-save. Look, I have children and other responsibilities. I often time need to needlessly replay sections of games after being forced to quit during the middle of a section. Doesn’t need to happen.

  3. First person shooters without the ability to go prone.

  4. Endlessly spawning bad guys.

  5. Games that should be great but aren’t because of console limitations. Biggest offender, Crysis 2. You could see it by playing it. This games should have been the greatest game since Half Life 2. Even though it was clearly developed for the PC and ported to consoles, far too many compromises were made which turned what should have been an all-time great into a merely above average first person shooter.
