In your case it’s more likely to be sound driver’s fault.

I’m assuming that you have your audio transmitted over HDMI to the monitor? Or at least you most probably have NVIDIA High Definition Audio devices installed in numbers if you check for them in Windows device manager. If you happen to have any of HDMI audio outputs enabled among output audio devices in windows control panel there could be sound-related problems in case your monitor can’t process the audiostream which gets transmitted on HDMI interface and fails to ACK/NACK packets propely. Then you could get stalls while audio driver waits for the monitor to answer until it times out. Rinse, repeat, say hello to the resulting stutter.
