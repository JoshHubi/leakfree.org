Fine. Wall o’ text incoming

I had to film a local football team working out and make a couple of interviews before an important match took place (it’s going to be played tomorrow) as an assignment for uni.

This workout session was originally going to be in another place , but it started raining heavily the night before so they had to move to another part of town, called Bajo Flores. In regular circumstances I wouldn’t go there because it’s known as a hellhole -it almost overlaps with one of the biggest and most dangerous villa miserias around here, called the 1-11-14, full of drug dealers and some of the worst scum you can think of- but as we had to do this project ASAP we didn’t have lots of options to reschedule. (by the way, this happened at 9.30 in the morning; I though it would be safer because of that, but that shows what I know. I had never gone to this place before).

Earlier that morning, we had taken a cab with a friend, a cameraman, and -obviously- the cabdriver. I was sitting in the front seat; the other two guys were in the back seats. After about 30 minutes, we were really near our destination. We couldn’t find the entrance to the place where the players were training, though we could see them through the metal mesh that surrounded the football fields.

We had asked a passerby and he told us there was a gate on the opposite end of the sports complex. Just as we turned around the corner, we saw that there were a lot of houses built on the dirt street, made of wood, scrap metal and cardboard. I didn’t like what I saw so I told the cabdriver to turn around, but he didn’t (later he told me that the dirt had turned to mud because of the rain and he didn’t want to get stuck in that place).

Two guys appeared out of nowhere, one dressed in green, and the other one wearing a yellow raincoat. I don’t know how, but the guy in the raincoat was suddenly just 10 meters away from us. He pulled out a gun, cocked it (this was all during a split second), and just as he pointed it at me (because I was in the front seat), the cab driver stepped full throttle and hit him with the rightmost part of the front of the car. We didn’t look back, so I don’t know if the guy stood up or did anything after that.

I just know that I always thought I would do this or that if I was in a similar situation, but I just froze, the time seemed to slow down (I remember everything in some sort of slow motion movie), and I didn’t even manage to take cover or anything.
