Krattle August 11, 2020, 8:59am #4

That’s a very good case you bought. When you’re building a computer, you need to keep in mind your purpose. I presume this a gaming computer. In that case you will need mainly a powerful CPU and a powerful graphics card. 8GB of RAM would be more than enough for gaming, and it gives you tons of room for multi-tasking. Anymore is a waste unless you do stuff like make movies or use Photoshop.

As for motherboards, I actually highly suggest this:
https://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813128532&Tpk=gigabyte%20ga-x79-ud3

This is the best CPU in the world right now: https://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16819116491

But it is very expensive and massive overkill for gaming. You just need to make sure you buy a socket LGA 2011 CPU or it won’t fit in the motherboard I suggested. Basically, you gotta make sure those two always match up.

You’ll need a good CPU cooler, and if you don’t want to go water cooling, then go with this: https://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16835608024

You’ll need a big power supply with lots of watts and amps - Corsair is the way to go:
https://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=17-139-034&SortField=0&SummaryType=0&Pagesize=10&PurchaseMark=&SelectedRating=1&VideoOnlyMark=False&VendorMark=&IsFeedbackTab=true&Keywords=%28keywords%29&Page=1#scrollFullInfo

As for hard drives, Caviar Black is damn good: https://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16822136533

However if you want to go with Solid State Drives, that’s up to you. They’re much more expensive, not as much storage capacity, but they’re more reliable and faster in many respects.

I suggest adding Blu-Ray capacity to your computer: https://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16827106451

Here’s what I suggest for RAM: https://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16820233164&Tpk=CMZ4GX3M2A2000C10

If you want quality sound and surround-sound capability, go with this beast: https://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16829132010

And last but not least, your graphics card. I highly, highly suggest this top-of-the-line beast: https://www.evga.com/products/moreInfo.asp?pn=04G-P4-2690-KR&family=GeForce%20600%20Series%20Family&sw=

The GTX 690 is $1000 but it’s the most powerful GPU in the world at the moment and can handle everything you throw at it. If that’s too expensive, go with the GTX 680 or GTX 670. Both have the latest technology but are a little slower and cheaper than the GTX 690.

All of the parts I have suggested are fully compatible.
