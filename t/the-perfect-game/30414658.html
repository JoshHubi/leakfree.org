When cops aren’t on calls, they’re wondering around the city for something to do, or passing time by hunting down speeders.

In this game, you’ll be in a virtual world, full of crime. This isn’t going to be some shoot 'em up game; however, hostage situations and shootouts may occur. You have to follow all the rules of ROE (rules of engagement). I.e. you can’t shoot someone robbing a bank with a baseball bat. That’s legally and morally unjust. You’ll also have to pass training before you can play. Don’t compare this to AA. I guess a ranking system could be involved.

Game-play: So, you start out in the P.D. and you gear up and chose your weapons at the locker room. You can choose which your fire arm you’d like. The glock .22 (very common in police force) or a beretta. You can also store either an M16 or some type of pump shotgun on a rack above you head in the cruiser. You also get a variety non-lethal weapons: taser, mace or pepper pray, possibly a bean bag gun. After you suit up, you go to briefing. Here the sergeant will tell you any important issues you should know (weather, gang activity, drug dealing, city events, etc…). After that you head to your car and you do what you do.

While wondering the city you may get a call from the dispatcher that their has been a fight (i.e.). You must go to the location, break up the fight, secure both potential suspects and then question them. Once done, you ask “Do you want to press charges”, and whatnot. If you find that one should go to jail, or both, you throw them in your cruiser and drop 'em off at the downtown jail.

If you’re not on a call, that’s when you take the time to look for crime yourself, or just shoot the radar gun at a busy intersection. You get to pull people over and whatnot, give them the ticket, you know. If you see anything suspicious you have the right to search their vehicle. If you find any contraband you get to arrest them.
