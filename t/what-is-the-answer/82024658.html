That’s the thing, it’s not just any 4 answer multiple choice question, it says this question. So the numbers in the answer do affect the chance of correctly guessing the answer, because it’s entirely dependent on guessing the correct answer to this particular question.

Well what messes with people’s heads is that most people would assume that you have a 50% chance of winning after the host opens one door, because there are 2 doors left. So people will just stay with their first gut instinct and not switch because they figure there’s no advantage. But in reality, you should always switch, because the other door actually has a 66% chance of having the prize, not 50%. When the host takes away one choice, he doesn’t change the probability of each door winning.

“As Cecil Adams puts it, “Monty is saying in effect: you can keep your one door or you can have the other two doors.” The player therefore has the choice of either sticking with the original choice of door, or choosing the sum of the contents of the two other doors, as the 2/3 chance of hiding the car has not been changed by the opening of one of these doors.” - wikipedia
