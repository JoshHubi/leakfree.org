Okay, maybe I should explain better.

The “old friend” is the FN FAL (first two pictures). In use by the German Army during the 70’s. It was replaced by the G3 (3rd picture, from Firearms Source). Before the Cold War ended, Heckler & Koch were working on a successor of the G3 that should use 5.56mm ammunition, which was intended to be the G41 (4th picture - no broken link seen here). As the Cold War was over, there was no need anymore for the G41, so the order was cancelled. Later the Bundeswehr requested a modernized assault rifle, again it should be capable of firing the same ammunition as the American M4 and M16. That was the G36 (5th picture), which is currently in use by the Bundeswehr. The next generation of assault rifles by Heckler & Koch are the G416 (5.56mm) and the G417 (7.62mm), seen on pictures 6 and 7.

And the “little companion” is what I prefer using in CS: Source. The Sig 552. The other assault rifles on the last picture are all basically the same design as the Sig.
