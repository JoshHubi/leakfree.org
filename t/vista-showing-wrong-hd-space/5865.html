{80GB of stuff, and my PC has 250GB of total space, is showing 7GB, 20GB}

Alright, 250GB hard drive, or 250GB of reported total capacity?

250GB hard drive will end up being ~230GB once Windows addresses all the space. 230-80 = 150GB maximum free space. Take away the swap/page file, System Restore, temporary internet files, the Windows Temp folder, hibernate file, and other junk, and I’d bet you’re down to at least (most?) 100GB, depending on how often (if ever) you clean your drive, how many System Restore points you have, etc.

Step 1 - download CCleanerand run it - it will clean the useless crap off your harddrive

Step 2 - download WinDirStatand run it - it will tell you the storage used by all files/folders on your drive

Step 3 - upgrade to Windows 7 as previously mentioned - Windows Vista has issues with displaying the right amount of freespace.
