I think we have too little information to sink this idea too quickly. We have a CEO “hinting” that CoD would go subscription based.

Now, subscription base makes sense for games like WoW or any other game where its playability would require constant monitoring/service from the developer. For wow, they need to maintain boat loads of servers and accounts, all of which cost money. You are spending your 15 bucks a month to get something.

If COD goes MMO, then this model is logical.

But an FPS? Unless this next CoD comes with massive amounts of updates, add-ons, new maps, new campaigns, and basically gives you something to pay for every month, it simply is not worth it. Simply getting the ability to play the game for the month is not worth 15 dollars, unless you only intend on playing for 4 months.

As mentioned, Valve has released FPS games that have come with constant updates and additional free content. This content is worth money, yet they give it away for free to the PC owners.

Not that I WANT to pay for Valve’s extra goodies, but lets face it: If Valve asked for a small fee for the TF2 updates (I know, they do on XBox… not their choice) it would actually be quite fair.

Oh, and Activision throwing some stupid idea around hardly comes anywhere near even possibly being the “future of gaming.” Over react much?
