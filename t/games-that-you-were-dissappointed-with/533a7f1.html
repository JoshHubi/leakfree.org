BaTz281 November 24, 2018, 9:09pm #278
Mr.Shadow:

To be honest, I was a little dissapointed with Borderlands. Don’t get me wrong, it’s a really awesome game, but the aiming is just bad. Other then that, great game.

I agree and disagree at the same time. As long as you chose a weapon that had an accuracy rating higher than ~85, you were set (excluding, of course, shotguns and explosives). With scoped weapons, you definitely had to have a weapon with ~95 accuracy or better if you were looking for headshots (unless you’re using a shotgun, of course).

One of the best weapons I found as Roland was a Glorious Havoc Combat Rifle (lvl 19 requirement) that I used all the way through level 37. It had the stats that I wished every other weapon had - very high recoil reduction, very high fire rate (12.7) AND very high accuracy (92, great for a Combat Rifle). With that weapon, I could pretty much zoom in (iron sights), hold the trigger down and get 70 headshots in a row.

On the other hand, not many weapons you wanted to use (high elemental damage, great clip size, etc.) had the accuracy you were looking for, and you have to take into consideration the balance mechanic in place. There is no PERFECT gun, because the game is loot/stat based - what stats do you gain/lose for each weapon you’re comparing?

Back on topic - Another game I dislike is Black. I love shooters, and I rarely care if a FPS has a good story line if the combat/graphics make up for it.

But with Black, they just gave you loads of ammo, stuck you against opponents with auto-aim and rocket launchers, then told you to kill things and collect intel with no indication why you were doing it. So the combat pretty much sucked, the story was nonexistent, and the graphics were meh (as compared to other PS2 games).
