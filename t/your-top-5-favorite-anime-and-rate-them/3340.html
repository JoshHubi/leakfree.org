Orpheon November 24, 2018, 8:25pm #19

Hmm, tough. Well, of what anime (not counting any manga or light novels) I’ve seen:

  1. Trigun: Great anime, one of the few with a good English dub. Shame more and more anime seem to be afraid to use adults as main characters. - 99/100
  2. Azumanga Daioh : Was not expecting to like this, but thoroughly enjoyed it. Lots of laughs. Made me actually miss high school - 98/100
  3. Lucky Star : A spiritual successor to Azumanga Daioh, with a gamer/otaku as a lead. Had to watch it, and glad I did. I hear this sort of show is getting over done though. - 92/100
  4. FLCL - A thoroughly wacky show with a surprising amount of deep meaning. The symbolism may or may not be coincidental, but it’s the only work of fiction I’ve enjoyed analyzing - 88/100
  5. Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya - it has flaws, but also an interesting premise and a main character I can relate to. The second season loses a lot of points for the infamous Endless Eight, but it also had a lot of shocking moments and left me wanting more. I’m eagerly awaiting a sub of the movie that just came out. - 80/100

Honorable mentions:
Full Metal Panic! - FMP!, Fumoffu, and The Second Raid, in order, are - awesome, hilarious, and super awesome.
Ranma 1/2 - Funny and ridiculous. And fanservice. Hooray!
Neon Genesis Evangelion - The first 19 episodes were incredibly awesome. The last 7 were incredibly insane. I know the reasons why it happened, but it was still painful nonetheless. Same thing with End of Evangelion. First half, amazing. Second half, W.T.F.
Tengen Toppa Gurren Lagann - Successfully mixes Evangelion’s robots with FLCL’s art style and humor.
Aim For the Top! aka Gunbuster - a formulaic show, but the time dilation really makes you feel wonky.
Voices of a Distant Star - a feature length anime movie made by a dude in his basement on his Mac. And it’s better than a lot of studio anime.
Excel Saga - pure insanity.
