Maxx_Skywalker November 25, 2018, 1:18am #98

Yes, I’m reviving a long dead thread, simply because I didn’t get to participate in it.

Well here are my top three overall favorites:

1. Perfect Dark ~ N64/360
A well rounded package with excellent visuals for it’s time and VO that doesn’t suck. PD defined multiplayer shooters for me, as I spent hours splitting the screen with my younger brother against 8 other bots. This was the first game that I played that truly allowed me to enjoy a multiplayer component of a game by myself. No other game has succeeded in even matching the options that were available in PD’s multiplayer, especially with bots. The 360 port made my favorite game even better by improving the controls and tightening up the textures. Too bad you can’t find any open games online.

2. Super Smash Bros. Brawl ~ Wii
A Nintendo fanboy’s dream come true. Brings together an insane amount of characters and environments from amongst their many franchises into one complete package. It has a solid single player campaign with some awesome cutscenes, but the multiplayer focus is where the game truly shines. The amount of content in this game alone is ridiculous, with stunning visuals to boot. Always a great party game. Period.

3. Counter Strike: Source ~ PC
At the time I acquired CS: S, I didn’t exactly have my own internet connection, which in turn made this game my go-to multiplayer bot game. I lived and breathed Condition Zero’s offline bot matches, so the visual upgrades certainly had a huge impact. Later when I actually started paying for internet, I discovered custom maps and weapon skins, which extended the life of the game I had been playing since its inception. I still have good times taking my computer to my brother’s place, and linking up just to play some good old CS:Source.
