I for one would be very interested to see this thread take off and to see even more spectacular screenshots from the fanbase. I also know there is a certain gentlemanfrom the “freeman chronicles” that is looking for some high res awesomesawce screenshots, particularly of the first stages of the game to be used in the next stage of their filming.

I dont think we need another “pound the print screen” thread like some might be thinking, but instead a true creative do your best screenshot of your favorite parts of the game.

That said, here are some tips I have gathered from the net on how to get some really badass screenshots.

  1. High resolution (1600x1080 or 1920x1080) & high (at 4x) MSAA is preferred. Crank your settings as high as you can go.
  2. Action is nothing to be scared of. This could be anything from full on fighting to a simple enemy in the scene…but avoiding blank environment shots (which has been 99% of our media so far) is preferable.
  3. Use host_timescale to play the game in slow motion. This is great for getting action/combat shots.
  4. If you really wanna go crazy, try some advanced screenshot techniques such as recording demos and playing them back in slow motion, where you can pause time and frame your shots.
  5. Hide your hud (cl_drawhud 0). no HUDs allowed in these shots.
  6. Hide your weapon (r_drawviewmodel 0) if desired. Ideally if you are noclipping/framing in a non-standard way, the weapon is hidden. If you are walking around normally, weapons can be okay.
  7. Use FOV changes if desired.
    8 ) Think about what the public has/hasn’t seen. Getting cool shots of iconic moments & unseen stuff is preferred.
  8. Think about getting interesting combinations of weapons/enemies. Can you get a shot of a marine hitting a tripmine? or taking out houndeyes with grenades? Mix it up.
  9. Don’t use weapons out of context with chapters…eg; no gluon in Office Complex.
  10. Take a lot of shots, and narrow them down yourself. Don’t post 5 shots taken within seconds of each other…you pick the best one and give that.
  11. Think about framing, fore/mid/background, etc. Composition is 90% of what makes a screenshot great or terrible. You want this to look like a movie director took this screenshot, not some fan wandering around while playing.

Also: https://developer.valvesoftware.com/wiki/How_To_Take_Artistic_Screenshots
