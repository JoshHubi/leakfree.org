Psych November 24, 2018, 10:11pm #13

As someone who owns both games, I’d go with MW2. I’ve gotten much more play time out of it. It’s single player is definitely a step & another above BC’s, with the length of it being about the same as BC’s. The MP is quite addicting due to it’s constant & relaxed reward/leveling system. Coupled along with the “perks” system, it does a really good job at keeping the player interested. A definite con will be the lack of dedicated servers. But I’m sure you’ve already heard about that. Yes it does balance the teams, but it does get annoying at times to have to switch in & out of lobbies.

BC2 is a great game in it’s own right. The leveling is a little slow & tedious, but nonetheless also rewarding. Just expect unlocks to come at slower intervals. It’s MP definitely covers a wider spectrum with it’s destructable cover & vehicles while doing it with less bells and whistles. The SP was kind of disappointing however. It went off to a great start, but that just sort of dissolves into a predictable & cliched storyline after the first three or so levels.

Personally, I’ve gotten a little used to MW2’s “DUN DUN DUN!!!” adrenaline pumping sort of gameplay. When played, the player will definitely notice that BC2 is a bit more bland in a few areas from the character models, to the terrain. But as I said before, it’s personal taste. I suggest you do some more research yourself & decide what fits you best.

Just to use as an illustration, I’ve played MW2 for about 75hrs since release & BC2 for about 35hrs since release. You can’t go wrong with either game though.

Psych November 24, 2018, 10:11pm #14

Oh & BC2 definitely requires a better rig to run decently compared to MW2. I get about 46 FPS on avg with all settings maxed for MW2. In contrast, my avg for BC2 is only about 30 or so. So factor that as well if you intend on purchasing this for PC.
