Maxey November 24, 2018, 9:42pm #96

Microsoft focused on the gaming part of the 360 more than Sony with their PS3.

When Sony launched the PS3, their marketing logic was to announce it as a multimedia system, rather than just a game console that also plays blu ray movies. That ended up alienating consumers, thinking that Sony downplayed the console gaming part. Add to that a weak online component on launch, a selection of multiplatform titles that performed better on the 360 because the PS3’s architecture is tricky to program for and a really steep price tag, and you end up with a system that suffers from identity crisis and ended up disappointing gamers.

The 360, however, was marketed from the start as a straight gaming machine, with a strong online support, far easier to program for and cheaper to buy.

If you’re a straight up gamer and look at them in that perspective, which system you end up getting?
