Grey_Acumen November 24, 2018, 7:51pm #117

PS3 Pros: Stronger CPU, BluRay Drive, Standardized Harddrive, Motion controller support, Wifi Included, Free Online Support, extra features when using PSP, Hardware Backwards Compatability(Old models only), More fully exclusive games(160)

PS3 Cons: Weaker Online Support, Weaker GPU, ZERO backwards Compatibility(Current models), Less Total Games(558)

360 Pros: Cheaper, Better Online Support, More Ergonomic controller, Software Backwards Compatibility(ALL models), More CONSOLE exclusive games(118+74Console=192), More Total Games(736)

360 Cons: Weaker CPU, Paid Online Support, Nonstandardized Hard Drive, Wifi costs extra, Less Fully Exclusive Games(118)

The reason why I list “Cheaper” as a 360 Pro, but not “More Expensive” as a PS3 Con is because only the 360 Arcade version that has no hard drive is cheaper. The Elite is about the same price as PS3 120GB, and the Elite 250GB is actually a little more than the PS3 250GB but also includes a free game or something.

While some might say that the stronger GPU of the 360 makes it the better gaming system, both are actually comparable to each other in terms of overall strength, but because of CPU/GPU differences it means that any game designed to capitalize on the strengths of one system can’t just port straight to the other system. Since 360 managed to release first, developers designed their games to take advantage of a stronger GPU, and thus ports to the PS3 take a heavy hit. Games designed exclusively for PS3, and therefor use the stronger CPU to make up for the weaker GPU, generally stand toe to toe graphically with 360s best.

So basically it comes down to this: If you use your PC for gaming and don’t already have a bluray player, PS3 wins hands down. If the console you choose will be your primary gaming system OR you already have a Bluray player, then it depends which one has the specific exclusive games you’re more interested in, but if both then 360 wins.
