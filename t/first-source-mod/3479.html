JeffMOD August 11, 2020, 3:22am #8

TWHL is a good place for mapping tutorials for the basics, and the userbase will most likely be able to help with any question you have, regardless of which stream you’re working in.

Of course, mapping is where all the other streams come together- without maps, all a mod is is new code and models, with nowhere to show them off.

If you’re going to mod for source, you need the SDK and the SDK Base for whatever engine subversion you want to mod. Open the SDK, select “create a mod” and follow the instructions. Your mod will be fully set up shortly! (Of course, it will be a copy of the Lostcoast source test, so you’ll want to do actual work before you ship it.)

Shaktal November 24, 2018, 8:31pm #12

Blender has some functions which are not readily available in Commercial packages. Because it is open-source, they can put in functions that haven’t been fully stabalised, because they have nothing to use.

But for immediate ease of use, and render quality etc. use 3DS Max, that comes with excellent functions, It is easy to use at first, and although the learning curve gets steeper the move you delve into the program, it is a great choice of 3D Modelling program.

But basically, you need to try a multitude of different Modelling programs, and make your own decision based on that.

EDIT: The main 3D Programs (As far as I’m aware) are:

  • 3DS Max
  • Blender
  • Maya
  • XSI

Try those if you can, and then chose your favourite one out of them. And don’t always go by what other people say, a lot of people seem to heavily bias in favour of the modelling program they use.
