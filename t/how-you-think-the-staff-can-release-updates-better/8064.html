ShadowNate November 25, 2018, 1:05am #8

The updates are handled very well for the mod, especially for one this far into development.

Frequent updates are for the first months of development (still not regular). Monthly/regular updates are not part of the development model for a mod nor a requirement. If they wanted to adopt it, they would have.

There are tons of media already released, one very nice teaser and a professionally done trailer. The devs said they want to focus on finishing the mod, so next big update will probably be the mod’s release. (Still not saying that “next” means “coming soon”).

There will always be people that will claim a mod is dead (for any mod), when they are frustrated because it wasn’t released within their own timeframe. Even if the devs explicitly reply that they are still working on it. They won’t bother to even look for information in the mod’s wiki, or forum, especially threads like the sticky threads in this subsection. Also, there are far too many people who for some reason are so personally pissed with the mod (I believe that they are largely the same crowd that Fiberawptic thinks that would “calm down” with upodates), that wait “around the corner” just to diss any kind of update attempt.

Finally, troubling yourself with what other “people” expect or would like to see will not get you anywhere. There is no possible way to calm all people down.
