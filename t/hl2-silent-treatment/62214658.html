Mack November 24, 2018, 11:14pm #24

I think that the connection between the not talking and the cut-scene is very serious and deliberate. Both allow for us, as the gamer, to project ourselves into the character. Gordon not speaking means he never says anything that we wouldn’t say. I know that most of the time while I was playing, if someone said something to Gordon, I answered out loud! Having control in the cut scenes works in the exact same way, Gordon never made a move without us making it for him. The cut-scenes where we couldn’t move, like when the Hunter gets Alyx for example, were done so to exaggerate the feeling of helplessness and frustration of not being able to move, when we normally could have done something.
An interesting subject! Good luck!
Mack
