Muhb November 24, 2018, 8:31pm #26

When I decide to play a game, graphics are one of the last things that’ll influence my decision to continue or cease playing. If I don’t complete a game, it’s never because of the graphics.

That said, I think I kind of know how OP feels, with regards to “jumping back in time” with Half-Life. I played Portal first, (it’s what caused me to cave and buy Orange Box, and later to try and figure out what all these nonsensical “Half-Life” shenanigans were about :fffuuu but I managed to put off Half-Life 2 and the Episodes until I had gone through the first title. While in the Hazard Course, I couldn’t help but think “dang, I miss the graphics for Portal… :’(” but it didn’t take long for the gameplay to take hold, and I found it very easy to get lost in the world of Half-Life, regardless of the dated graphics. :3

At any rate, I’d urge you not to give up on Half-Life simply because of the graphics. Even though you already know the story, and have gone through HL2 and the Episodes, I think there’s something different about seeing and playing through each room yourself. Also, I definitely think you’ll be able to appreciate Black Mesa more, once it finally comes out, than if you hadn’t played through the original at all. I know I will, at least.

PS: Not sure if you’ve heard about this yet, but there’s a Half-Life Hi-Def Model Pack available from Steam that will give the game a bit of a graphical boost, if you’re interested.
