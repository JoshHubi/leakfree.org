Actually no. A birthday is technically a celebration of the date of your birth, not a celebration of becoming one year older, so even though Gordon Freeman would have lived for 27 years, it would still come out to his 47th birthday (give or take depending on how long he actually was stuck in stasis, since it was never specified as exactly 20 years)

The same issue comes up in the classic “Pirates of Penzance” in which the main character has reached 21 years of age and is thus free of his indentures, but the letter of his contract actually states that he is only free after his 21st birthday specifically, and he was born on February 29th. This resulted in the number of actual birthdays being technically only 5.

Of course if Freeman also had his birthday on Feb 29th, then he would really just be coming up on his 12th birthday when 48 years had passed since his actual birth.
