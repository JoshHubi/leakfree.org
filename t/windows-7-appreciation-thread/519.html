Siggs November 24, 2018, 5:37pm #2

Well, technically, it comes under technology, but that’s up to a mod to decide.

Having a decidedly mediocre laptop that came with Vista on it, I installed W7 and instantly saw the performance boost, in-game, and on the desktop too. Best features imho is the improved networking(it connects instantaneously), the improved plug and play(I could connect a logitec MXrevolution, and use it instantly, without logitech drivers), and the context sensitive boxes they’ve put onto the start menu.

In your recently used items on the start menu, and on the taskbar buttons, clicking or hovering over the little arrow will show recent internet pages, or mp3’s played, or image files opened, etc. On microsoft programs, such as msn, it will show status options and other context sensitive links. And the pinning and unpinning of items has been streamlined. Even the window snapping to half/full screen, I find very useful.

It’s cleverer, and more intuitive. What Vista should have been
