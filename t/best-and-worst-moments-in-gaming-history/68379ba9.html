Best: 90s generation of gaming. As someone pointed out, it really was a great time for expression of creativity in video games.

A time when tech was good, and so were the games that were powered by the tech. ID established themselves with Doom and Quake, Blizzard North became an important part of Blizzard and created hack&slash games with Diablo, The Final Fantasy series was pushing the boundaries of the PS1 and providing a fleshed out great narrative that rivals many books, Metal Gear Solid came, Half-Life was conceived, the Descent games were a new concept (and many people played them) and Modding was becoming a standard and mods were played almost as much as games themselves.

Off-topic: As a full on, long time Halo fan, I feel obliged to correct anyone who states that Halo brought on regenerative health. Please don’t level that accusation at it, it doesn’t deserve it. Level it instead, at its bastard sequel, Halo 2. I’m a Halo fan, and even I dislike Halo 2 for a variety of reasons that I won’t get into. Halo had a standard health system just like every other FPS around at the time, although the health kits in game are of the “derp-instahealtofullhealth-derp” kind. It admittedly has a regenerative shield (read: armour) system, but not a regen health system. That was Halo 2. The bastard sequel that should never have happened.
Also, I think the reason so many people rave/raved about the first Halo, is the same reason people rave/raved about Goldeneye: It’s an FPS on console…that works. Even running on a 360, it quite simply has control design that must come from the mind of some sort of god. Nothing in the controls of that game trips over itself or other commands. Ever.

tl;dr: Halo 2 gave regen health, not Halo. Halo likened to Goldeneye as a console FPS that works. Halo control system is awesome.
