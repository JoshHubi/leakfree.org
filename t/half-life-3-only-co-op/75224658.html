Gabe Newell:

What we’re trying to talk about is the fact that, not that we’re not doing single-player games… It’s more that we think we have to work harder in the future.

Entertainment is inherently increased in value by having it be social, by letting you play with your friends and recognising that you’re connected with other people. That’s the thing we’re trying to say. Single-player is great, but we also have to recognize that you have friends and want to have that connected as well.

So it’s not about giving up on single-player at all, we actually think that there are a bunch of features and capabilities that we need to add into our single-player games to recognise the socially-connected gamer.

Every gamer is instant messaging, every gamer has a Facebook account. If you pretend this doesn’t exist you’re ignoring the problems you ought to be taking on, so it’s “single-player plus,” not “no more single-player.”
