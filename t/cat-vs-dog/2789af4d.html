Grey_Acumen November 24, 2018, 7:51pm #94

Cats generally are less work, and can usually take care of themselves as long as you provide remember to change food, water, and litterbox. The only time you have a problem is if they don’t use a scratching post then you’ll need to keep their claws trimmed or risk having them shred furniture. (I dislike declawing, but that is an option, provided you don’t let your cat outside unescorted) Generally only Siamese (both full and mixed breed) are known for more commonly having temperament issues.
Personal favorite breeds are Maine Coon, and Scottish Folds.

Dogs take more work, both to train as well as just general involvement needed for them to be happy and healthy, but also are much more enthusiastic about showing love and support for you. Large dogs generally have issues recognizing their own size, but also tend to have a much more mellow temperament. Smaller dogs are typically far more high strung and yappy.
Typically mutts are much smarter and less prone to health problems than just about any pure dog breed other than herding dogs. Hunting dog breeds are usually still okay in brains, but start having a higher risk for health issues. Guard/Fighting dogs are about even with Hunters, but even properly raised ones you do have to be careful around strangers. Show dog breeds are generally okay in temperament, but usually are the dumbest and most prone to health problems.
Personal favorite breed is Shetland Sheepdog and Welsh Corgi.
