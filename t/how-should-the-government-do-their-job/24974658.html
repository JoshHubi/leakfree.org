If you want to talk about how the American republic is supposed to work, it’s worth looking into what the founders originally intended.

One of the things the founders most feared was the tyranny of the majority - that a group would take control of their government and reform it to their own ends. This is why they created a republic instead of a pure democracy: the idea being that while a pure democracy could fall to tyranny by creating a situation where someone could take control of the “unwashed masses” of the public and use it to overpower the government. They felt that a republic was better because the elected representatives - ideally, the best and brightest that the nation could produce - would resist such things. Well, that didn’t work out so well (see: political parties.)

The other thing they were really big on was trying to get things to move slowly when being passed into law - which makes sense. Better one thinks things through instead of heedlessly passing something that turns out to be garbage (see: stimulus.) The way they tried to do this was to create as much gridlock as possible in the congress by having people elected to represent, not the American people as a whole, but a set electorate. A senator was to represent only the people of his state, and a congressman (i.e. member of the House of Representatives) was to represent an even smaller area - his own district. In this way, they would be representing very diverse groups of people, and the idea was that - seeing as the interests the people of a state like Massachusettes would conflict greatly with the interests of the people of a state like South Carolina - it would take a very long time and much negotiation for the two to agree on anything. Thus a bill could only be passed after it was truly good and helped the people of both states.

As can be seen, the idea was not, as we see it today, to pass as much legislation as possible, as quickly as possible. It was to pass very LITTLE legislation, so as to ensure that the legislation was of good quality. If we MUST have government passing legislation, this is the way it ought to be.
