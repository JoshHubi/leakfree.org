From some reading, this seems to be the solution:

I have recently resolved my black ascreen issue, Dale or ManUPhilip’s answers were meaningless to me.

The problem lies with the graphics card and Motherboard. I researched into this and found out that the newest drivers for the 7, 8 or 9 nvidia series graphics card along with an asus motherboard gives you the black screen due to the “physixs” system.

My solution consisted of using my old nVidia series 7500GT graphics (or you can try onboard graphcis), installed Windows 7 (it’ll work as long as you aren’t using a series 8 or 9 card). Once windows booted, install the oldest set of drivers that supports your nVidia 8 or 9 series card (I used the 175.65 driver set), power off your pc, plug in your nVidia series 8 or 9 graphics card and it’ll work.

The only problem with this method is if you need to re-install Windows 7, you’ll have to re-do the above steps. For me, I just bought an extra series 7 nVidia card and SLI’d the two cards together to replace my nVidia 9 card which I sold.

Hope this helps your problem too
