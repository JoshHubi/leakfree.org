Indeed.

Halo 1 had health and a regenerating shield, with the health only being taken away when your shield was completely drained. You had 8 health points and they could only be restored with med-packs.

Halo 2 used just a recharging shield, which if drained would leave the player open to a few shots before they would die if it did not regenerate quickly enough. The shield indicator changes colour the closer to death you are when you have no shield left.

Halo 3 is a bit more complicated. The Player has 115 hit points, 45 health points and 70 shield points. After a player is damaged, health will begin to regenerate after 10 seconds without taking damage. Health regenerates at 9 health points per second. If you are almost killed but recharge your shield, you can still die, as your health is independent.
