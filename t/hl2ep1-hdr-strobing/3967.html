Andy0101 November 24, 2018, 9:01pm #2

The light usually dims according to the amount of light you previously looked at. It effectively emulates real eye as it adapts to different amounts of light. For example, if you look at sun, it should gradually become darker and darker, and then when you suddenly look in the shadow you won’t see a thing and it takes some time for your “eyes” to adapt. The same thing goes when looking from dark to light - when you are in complete dark, suddenly looking directly in a source of light should temporarily blind you.

So I don’t think there is a problem, that’s what it is supposed to do to look more realistic. Try looking in a bright source of light and then look in a shadow and vice-versa. If it adapts gradually according to where you are looking at, everything is okay.
