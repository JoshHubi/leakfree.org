Ramirezoid:

Is it me, or has a new Battlefield been coming out every year since Bad Company

Looks like a lot of studios are annualizing due to the economy. In theory, annualization is good if you can keep a consistently high level of quality content yearly, but this in turn hinders more radical changes to a given gameplay formula. Assassin’s Creed 2 --> Brotherhood --> Revelations is an example of a yearly annualization turnover done right. Each game improves the engine and adds upgrades to the game’s code or gameplay (The combat mechanics in Brotherhood being tweaked to set a more aggressive play style being a standout feature illustrating this point), whereas something like CoD4 --> World At War --> Modern Warfare 2 --> Black Ops would be an example of inconsistent quality. World at War was decent, running in CoD4’s engine with some upgrades. MW2 was a giant graphical step up and a further tweak to CoD4’s gameplay elements. Black Ops, while having a strong story, was a step backwards because they were still using CoD4’s engine (tweaked further, but a graphical step down from IW4.0 tech).
