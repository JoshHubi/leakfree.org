It takes a long time because they’re reimagining the entire game using the Source engine (not simply slapping the HL1 maps into Source and fiddling with some of the physics and stuff as HL: Source did).

Look at how long it’s takes Valve, with paid full-time employees, to come out with stuff. Half-Life 2: Episode 2 came out latter part of 2007. Four years later, we still haven’t heard a peep about Episode Three. Or Half-Life 3. And, again, that’s with employees working full-time on the game.

The (unpaid) devs, a smaller group, are doing this in their spare time and are all over the globe. And, from the news they’ve put out, they basically have a working game (you can play through from beginning to end) but they’re still working on fixing problems and putting spit and polish on stuff. It shouldn’t be too much longer.

We’ll all just have to wait.
