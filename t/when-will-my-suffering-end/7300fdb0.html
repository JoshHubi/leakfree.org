Solokiller November 25, 2018, 12:25am #67

i have experience with 1:1 remaking maps, it’s easy compared to actually designing a new version of the map altogether.

Now, you might not see it, but a lot more work goes into even a basic room than you’d think, it’s a lot more than just adding a floor, 4 walls and a ceiling. When you’re playing the map you don’t see all the stuff going on in the background.

That light that’s flickering on and off? To make it work properly the values for it have to calculated so as to not increase the map’s size too much, or cause maximum settings to be hit.

That door you love to use in that one particular area? Everything around it has to be designed in such a way that its state doesn’t causes the FPS to drop by half, or glitches to occur when a prop is left in its path.

Even a simple hallway may require some special techniques to be used to prevent the engine from rendering more than needed, and causing problems as a result.

And then there’s managing progression from map to map, making sure that you don’t end up inside of a wall when you hit the changelevel.

I’d love to see you try and remake even one of the maps from half-life, but if you try, try to pick one that isn’t really easy to make, try one of the large outdoors maps, where displacements are common, or one of the on a rail maps, which are all linked together and require the train tracks to be setup just right so your train doesn’t clip through a wall or gib the player when it turns.

If you think remaking a game is easy, think again, because people here are getting sick and tired of your type of person, who think that just because the dev team set a release date for 2009, that suddenly, they owe you something.
