Elpants:

Yeah, it’s nice and fast (I have been using Chrome since December), but the addons are kind of limited in their functionality. Like the ad-blocking ones don’t actually block the ad from loading, it just hides them from view, while in Firefox they are literally blocked from even loading.

It’s really worth keeping in mind the technical benefits of the more limited extension design, though. The interface is VERY consistent (you don’t get extensions polluting context menus, or the odd extension that wants to be in the status bar for no reason), and with that consistency we get goodies like badges to indicate the number of new items.
Extensions can be installed / enabled / disabled / individually killed (and checked for resource consumption) instantly without restarting the browser, and they have far less capacity to screw things up.

Besides, who needs ad blocking when the performance hit is practically invisible?
