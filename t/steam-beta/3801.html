Grey_Acumen August 11, 2020, 3:41am #17

I love it.

  1. All the mods and games I have can be organized into different categories. All mods and shortcuts that haven’t been assigned a category get defaulted into “games” so I can easily pick out which games I haven’t configured yet.
  2. The Steam overlay seems to work much better. I use a fairly small screen size in source games, so before it ended up really stretched out and hard to use. Now it works perfectly for me.
  3. I really like the detail view that gives achievement and friend information on games that have it, but they also brought back small mode so I can just get the bare essentials too.
  4. I don’t know if this was the case before, but you can customize the menu list that shows up when you click on the icon in the notification tray (next to the clock) I have it set so that it only offers my friends list, store, and exiting steam. It displays the last 5 programs you’ve run by default. So that lets you get even MORE barebones; you don’t have to even open steam to play your games.
  5. The Store section has been overhauled so sales stand out much more obviously.
  6. Downloading, as well as pausing and resuming downloads feels MUCH more responsive. Before the beta I almost religiously kept from pausing ANY downloads simply because they always took so long to resume that I wondered if they weren’t just starting over completely. There’s still the problem that smaller games that could usually get downloaded in seconds will get a huge chunk stolen out of their download rate by any larger games you also have downloading.

On a side note. A closed steam beta for mac started a little while ago, has anyone managed to get into it at all? I signed up for it using my cousin’s computer, but I haven’t heard any word back yet.
