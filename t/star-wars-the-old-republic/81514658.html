I played this from day 1. I was looking for something to take me away from WoW. It did kind of succeed in that i gave it 3 months to convince me to keep playing. In all honesty the game did have great potential but it failed realy badly.

First thing that should be right above anything else in an MMO is the controls need to work. Which for most classes they didnt, huge lag delay and sometimes abilities not even activating at times, took them 2 1/2 months to sort that out.

Second the UI really needs certain things which werent there, UI scaling, Target of Target among other things. That took them atleast 4 - 5 months to bring into the game.

Third and the major part of an MMO is the endgame content. Not only were you fighting your UI and controls but buggy bosses aswell. Along with all that the PvP was always breaking in certain areas aswell as repeatable quests.

Unfortunatly the MMO community dont give developers that much time before they say, you had your chance but its just not good enough. Im not sure if thats because monthly subs are involved (that was certainly my reason) or some are just impatient.

My thoughts are the game could have been great and i would have played it for a long time i think. But it was released far too early. If EA had waited a little longer for certain things to have been corrected im pretty sure that quite a few more people would have stuck with it. Many of the points ive just mentioned and more were pointed out by the testers apparently but they pushed it out anyway.

Things need to work in an MMO to keep players going back to do the same bosses, but fighting bugs aswell as UI is just not acceptable.
I understand things take time to get right but certain things need to be right from day 1, it felt like an alpha release never mind a beta or even release.

The game did have many good points though. The questing was fun, the PvP was actually quite entertaining and coming from me thats a good thing becuase i dont usualy like PvP. The story lines for each class were nice to go through aswell, really opened up the lore of the game to everyone and the backgrounds of where all the different classes started and why. Crafting and companions were nice in this game too.

I did go back to the game after a few months to check out what had and hadnt been sorted but unfortunatly everyone that i knew on that particular server had left to play something else, obviously my real life freinds i knew about but others id met in the game had moved on aswell. So i cant give an accurate description of what state the game is in other than the fixes mentioned.

To sum it up, if all is sorted with the game which i know some things are now, like UI scaling and the bad contact with the character controls. Maybe if they keep working on it properly to fix the endgame it should be a pretty good F2P. Of course that is aslong as it isnt play to win with silly restrictions in certain things that would annoy you from the beggining. Personaly i blame EA for pushing it too quickly, as it could have been a really fun game.
