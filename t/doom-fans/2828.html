Bladex1200 November 24, 2018, 7:47pm #12

Well, that’s a lot of replies!

Yes, I’ve played Doom 3. I found it frightening on the first play-though. I think I exaggerated a bit by calling it a piece of crap. But still…

As a game, it’s a fine one. But it’s not Doom. Doom is a game that’s action-themed. Sure, it had its share of scary moments, but Doom mostly was a run-and-gun game where you ran around blasting baddies. Doom 3 threw that formula right out the window with a more cautious approach reminiscent of Half Life.

Now, don’t get me wrong. I love Half Life. It’s NOT Doom. By slapping “Doom” 3 on the cover, I expect an action game (Serious Sam is something I expected when I popped Doom 3 in, if you need a comparison). The game itself is fine. However, it lowered my expectations for the series.

One other thing I didn’t like was the flashlight. Why? Why can’t he duct-tape the flash light onto his gun? If the point of not having a light source on his gun was for the horror aspect, why couldn’t the developers use different forms of horror (ie. physics related horror, such as floating objects; psychological horror, such as ambient horror music). I honestly thought the game was okay, but it’s pretty crappy for something that has the ballz to claim it’s “Doom”.
