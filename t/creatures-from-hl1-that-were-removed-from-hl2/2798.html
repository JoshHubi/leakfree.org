YourArcenist:

I thought about the idea of the Combine exterminating them all, but it’s impossible to kill every single one of them.

I don’t think it’s impossible that the Combine exterminated them. We’ve managed to do that with many of Earth’s native inhabitants without even trying, so it doesn’t seem that far fetched that an advanced alien race would be able to do it if they had the motivation. Even if there were any left, there’s going to be so little about that it’s not unreasonable that we haven’t seen any. Also, we don’t know how many were teleported here, or how well they can survive in this environment for an extended period of time.

YourArcenist:

I understand them keeping the headcrabs as some sort of biological weapon, but the barnacles just sit around, eating whatever shit passes bye, and it looks like they didn’t even make an attempt at killing them.

Well, that’s just it. They just sit around, so the only people they’re going to have much of an opportunity to eat are people hiding out in dilapidated areas where they’re going to grow. The Combine probably already quickly remove any which are going to be much of a threat to them or their citizens.

In conclusion, I think the reason we see the aliens we do are thus:

Headcrabs: Biological weaponry. 'Nuff said.

Barnacles: Already explained above.

Antlions: Breed too fast, and can generally be kept back with thumpers/restrictors in any case.

Leeches and Ichthyosaurs: The Combine don’t seem to express much interest in the ocean, so they likely don’t think it’s worth getting rid of them. Also, they’re apparently draining the seas, so they probably look at it in the long term.
