Brother_Shrike August 11, 2020, 2:56am #436
gugamilare:

I don’t like your comment. I was referring to advanced specific disciplines, here you don’t get to choose the disciplines you are going to have, and we don’t learn calculus, psychology, latin (we do have many classes of English, though, I had classes in both Junior High and High School). The education here is more general.

But, yes, primary and secondary education mostly sucks here. That does not apply to some good schools (like the one I went to) nor it applies to higher education. And, no, we are not a bunch of poor people at all.

We get to choose between Latin, Chinese, French, and Spanish, you have to take at least 2 years, it’s recommended to take 4. I also took latin in Middle School, so I’ll have 5 years of it by the time I graduate.

In my school you have to take 4 years of science. Freshman year is physics, Sophomore year is chem, Junior year is Bio, and Senior year you get to choose from any of the science classes offered - you can choose AP versions of the classes already offered, Psych/Neuro (which is also AP), Environmental Science, Astronomy, Design Tech, Disease/Forensics/Technology, Zoology of Aquatic and Terrestrial Organisms or Anatomy and Physiology. I’m taking AP physics and chem this year, so I’ll have at least 5 science credits by the time I graduate.

I don’t think Europe has AP (advanced placement)classes. They’re basically a college level class that you can take in High School. At the end of the year, you take the AP test, and if you score high enough on it, a lot of colleges let you skip the introductory class in that subject (ie Physics 101 for AP physics). There are AP classes for almost everything, English, latin, Chem, Bio, etc.

My school has 3 levels for most classes - CP is basic level, Honors is a little more challenging, and AP is really challenging. You either take one or the other, although often you’ll take the AP class after you’ve taken the Honors class rather than instead of it.

On an unrelated note, English isn’t the official language of the US - we don’t have an official language. Most states specify official languages, English in most cases, although Hawaii has both English and Hawaiian. Wikipedia says that English is the official language of 28 states.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Languages_of_the_United_States#Official_language_status
