I’m a bit behind on the playing of these games but I was always hesitant to buy both in fear that they wouldn’t work well on my PC but they both run exceptional…

   Anyway I played and beat AC in a few days and Immediately started on AC2. Having played the games back to back I got to see how much the game changed from the original to it's sequel and how drastic the changes were. (Maybe I should say how drastic the improvements were.)  Everything from the animations, music, graphics, involved story line, and down to the small subtleties displayed by town folk and guards blew me out of the water. I haven't seen a sequel turn out to be so much better than the original and how many elements were added to really put you into a different world and give you a sense of being there in Italy. The great vistas at the top of viewpoints are phenomenal and makes every climb something to look forward to. 

Tuscany is the most beautiful piece of art I have seen in a game.

Hellblazer-14 November 24, 2018, 11:10pm #2

I just recently purchased AC2 and thought it was incredibly good too. Additions of certain scenes such as the chase scene with the horse drawn carriage made the game a lot more fun, and the ability to purchase ones weapons and ammunition made it more interesting… being able to swim, renovate your own small city etc etc.

Blending the Animus gameplay with the ‘real world’ of the main character was very smart of the developers, especially so since he gained the abilities of his avatar Enzio.
I really loved the addition of the dual blade system, assassinating 2 unsuspecting guards from the roof was amusing. I also found Enzio to be a more believable character, more thought out and well balanced, and the story managed to remain interesting despite being a tad repetitive.

The only thing I disliked is the fact that it was no longer set in the middle ages, but that’s just my personal opinion since I love that period in time.
