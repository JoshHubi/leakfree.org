Bloodshot:

Been playing the demo, the game itself has nice lighting and looks pretty, especially with the lens flares. It severely lacks detail though, the textures on hardcore are pretty bad, it seems they only have normal maps.

The motion blur is horrid though. As annoying as the first one’s motion blur could be at times, at least it was disabled in MP, and you could adjust the amount. 2 Seems to combat the annoyance by forcing yet more motion blur down are throats, they throw it around like it’s the highlight of the graphics almost - i guess they figured they could make up for the lack of visual detail in the demo by blurring everything. No console, so i can’t disable it, and gamer has a style of motion blur that looks even worse, so lowering settings isn’t an option.

On to the gameplay - It is fast paced and fairly fun, but It feels too much like a cod clone to me. The nanosuit’s 3 modes (1 passive and 2 active) are fairly easy to switch between, but they gave us the suit menu in this demo and it’s pointless to use now. Binoculars are pointless in MP, and it’s easier to switch with shortcuts, rather than open the menu.
Which is odd because the menu was so much more fluid in the first.

Power is a joke mode though, because it does nothing but let you sprint like a normal soldier (not realistically, I mean compared to other games) and there is no way to power up your bashes. It’s almost like they threw in power mode for per…ahem, modules, and the other little things like jump height and sprint. Maybe i would feel differently if you could charge a punch up.

Armor and cloak are nicely implemented though, I like the stronger feel armor has, and Stealth killing isn’t spam happy like other games, and the cloak has a nice balance of visibility and range, so you can’t just walk right in front of your enemies.

Air stomping enemies is really fun, but I have a feeling it will get old pretty soon. Though it can be quite useful since you can kill people with a stomp from a normal power jump now.

The small maps are too big for a 12 player limit, half the time i find myself running around looking for people in a TIA match, should be 16.

Overall, it’s not bad, but it feels like the bastard child of Crysis and CoD, and the whole design of things seems very generic to me. Probably will get it when it comes out just because I am bored of the other games that I have. Assuming the editor comes on launch day.
