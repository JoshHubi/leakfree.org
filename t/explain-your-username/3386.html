danielsangeo November 24, 2018, 8:26pm #12

Mine went through an evolution not entirely in my control.

Let’s turn the dial back to 1993. I was introduced to something called a “BBS” which was a way to talk to people over something called “the Internet” and play games like Tradewars. I didn’t really get into it much but I had to pick a username. At the time, I was on a big Karate Kid kick (probably due to the main character having the name “Daniel” which was my name and me having a crush on Elisabeth Shue). So, my user name became Mr. Miyagi’s name for the main character: DanielSan. With my membership, I got this weirdly formatted thing called an “e-mail address” with the format as danielsan@[BBS Name].com

I got maybe two e-mails in the first six months and those were from the sysadmin. I didn’t really use the account that much; I hated Tradewars and I didn’t know the people on the BBS. Then, one day, this option shows up called “SLIP WWW”. ‘That’s new,’ I thought. I tried it and it launched Netscape. What’s this thing then?

Three months later, I had created my own webpage on Geocities. Green background. Yellow text with red header. And embedded midi that looped playing a monophonic version of “Daniel” by Elton John (where my name actually came from). It was a horrific website, but I had the e-mail address danielsan@geocities.com with it.

Flash forward to 1999. Yahoo buys Geocities. Flash forward more. I buy a new computer and lose the password (I had saved it in the e-mail client and forgot the password). I signed up for a Yahoo e-mail address, using the e-mail address danielsangeo@yahoo.com (to reflect that I still had a Geocities website). Yahoo begins shutting down inactive Geocities websites (mine was shut down because I didn’t have my password anymore and I was using other servers anyway). I no longer have my Yahoo e-mail account.

My nickname, on the web, is danielsangeo now.
