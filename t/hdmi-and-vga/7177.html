VGA is analog, and HDMI is digital… so the same monitor might interpret these 2 signals diffrently and create a slightly different picture. Also, the VGA’s signal will depend on the quality of the cable, its length, and anything that could induce noise, while a digital signal is not affected by these things.

[…]
Also if your monitor isn’t 1920x1080 or higher, get with the times pal. VGA shouldn’t be used any more

Not true. Picture quality is practically the same between those connector types, unless the cable is damaged or cheap (or so long that signal degradation occurs), or a device on either end is messing up somehow.

VGA cables are able to handle 1920x1080 (and much higher resolutions) without issues.

Some monitors using HDMI connections sometimes have weird limitations on refresh rate and colour settings. It’s also quite unhelpful if your graphics card decides to think that your monitor is a HDTV and so gives you a washed-out picture and limited configuration options.

I don’t know your monitor but some higher end ones do signal conditioning to incoming HDMI signals like televisions do. This is to compensate for quirks in modern HD broadcast and BD-movies. Make sure the digital signal is unaltered in settings and that you are using the same color temps for both monitors. (set them both to user mode)
