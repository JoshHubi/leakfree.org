BaTz281 August 11, 2020, 4:33am #78

Some pretty good suggestions so far, but it’s a bit hard to tell from all your posts (Hubi) what you’ve been able to test and/or rule out. Did you rule the memory out with an overnight of memtest86?

Your CPU is still running a bit hot. The i-series SHOULD have pretty good thermals at stock speeds, but it also helps to have a good HSF with quality, correctly-applied thermal paste. The application techniques vary (one rice-grain sized stripe, 3 rice-grain sized stripes, one pea-sized dot in the center, apply and spread with credit card, etc) but I usually go with the dot in the middle and let the HSF spread it out. With an AMD X2 6000+ stock clocked, Arctic Cooling Freezer 64 and Artic Silver 5 in a 80°F environment, my CPU sits around 39°C. That i7-860 should be a few degrees cooler than that, especially if you have a cooler environment.

Double check EVERY power cable in your system, especially those powering important components (24-pin motherboard, 8-pin CPU, 2x6-pin GPU) but also your drives. And double-check means unplug, blow in the socket, and reseat until it clicks.

Go through your BIOS and check for power management settings on the CPU and memory. First your CPU voltage was 1.16, the last screenshot is 1.09, that’s a bit much of a jump (if you didn’t adjust it yourself). Double and triple check your memory timings - a few years back, I had WEEKS of unpredictable hard-resetting, BSODS saying IRQ_LESS_OR_EQUAL (which is usually heat-related), and general shitty performance all because my uncle had overclocked my memory by FIVE MEGAHERTZ! It didn’t matter if I was gaming, writing a paper, watching a movie, or idling, it would just reset whenever it felt like it.

Also, using this here I roughly calculated your minimum PSU to require around 450 Watts, and that DOESN’T account for system load (which with your GPU and CPU can jump another few dozen watts, if not 100+). You could try swapping out the GPU for a less power-hungry model. And while we’re discussing power, are you running your system on a surge protector and/or UPS? Before I had my UPS, my system would sometimes hard-lock and/or hard-reset when my sister turned on her blow dryer. Especially now that it’s summer time, people are running their AC, the power companies are browning out the grid (in the US, anyway) - it’s crucial to have clean power via UPS or at least surge protected.

In short, here’s a summary of issues that cause random hard-resets:

  • High temperatures
  • Improper memory timings (under-or-overclocked, voltage, etc)
  • Improperly seated power cables
  • Not enough quality power delivered to the system (UPS or different PSU)
  • Not enough power in general (remove GPU, try different PSU)
  • IRQ Conflicts (in extreme cases, try removing all unnecessary hardware)

Keep us updated
