I think the big reason they are calling it a spiritual successor because of the gameplay (edit: and graphics, of course). They are responding to the two problems with Crysis 2: really linear levels and a PC version which was (although the DX11 and High-res texture update helped to resolve it) as limited as the PS3 and Xbox 360 versions. Not that those had bad graphics, but Crysis 2 (until those updates, you might say) didn’t push the graphical limits for the PC like the original.

As for Crysis, if you haven’t finished the game yet…

I’ve installed something called Natural Mod for improving the lighting. I’ve also downloaded some nice high-resolution nanosuit textures. I’d also recommend the Reygel Texture Mod. It’s over 2GB of high resolution textures.

I found the nanosuit textures and high-res texture packs here: https://www.overclock.net/t/1014933/a-guide-to-crysis-mods-56k-warning Natural mod can be found here: https://www.crydev.net/viewtopic.php?t=14195

At least download the nanosuit textures- the default textures aren’t mapped very well and the new ones make it look stunning.

There’s lots of stuff out there for Crysis.

Also, if you’ve tried out the sandbox editor, try this:

https://tirido.blogspot.com/p/delorean-time-machine.html

It’s the DeLorean time machine mod for Crysis. And yes, pressing T to turn on the time circuits, and then accelerating to 88 MPH will make the Delorean jump through time! (different time of the day, but cool enough) When you emerge into a different time the DeLorean is all cold and icy, just like in the first movie. There’s instructions in the RTF file. Just be careful not to save levels after you mess around with them. If you’re not comfortable jumping into the sandbox, there are still videos of the mod on YouTube that are pretty cool.
