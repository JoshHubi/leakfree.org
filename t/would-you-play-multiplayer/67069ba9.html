OrderOfDagon November 24, 2018, 11:45pm #54

I remember back in the day, my bro had a server and a clan that ran a specific game that we made on there.
It went threw many names, so I’ll just explain it:

There would be 5 marines and 3 security guards versing each other. The guards had to retrieve a scientist like capture the flag. They had to protect and escort him to the other side of the map; just imagine a capture the flag game, but with a scientist. The guards had a one minute headstart, because the marines were not supposed to get the scientist; the only objective for the Marines was to kill the whole other team, and one minute seemed like a long enough time for the guards to get the scientist and begin to work their way back to base. After the scientist was captured, the guards would usually walk to let him keep up.

When the guards capture the scientist a noise would sound and the marines would finally get dropped at a point made knowable – there was a specific spot guards stayed away from because it’s where the marines spawned after their (the guard’s) one minute headstart. The marines had to decide whether to split up and find them, or assume they were somewhere and all go together. The marines got just Mp5s and the guards got glocks, satchels/tripmines, and grenades. It was surpisingly balanced.

Everyone walked in that game to keep quiet and so the scientist npc could keep up.

The game ended up having the guards set a lot of trip mine and satchel traps. Usually, a single guard
would stay behind and let the other two take the scientist. The guard that stayed behind would stay with the traps and satchel charges for an ambush. This usually resulted in the guards winning most of the time. But it was not unbalanced, because some of the time the marines would really just dominate the balls out of the guards.

If the marines kill the scientist, then the only way for the guards to redeem themselves is to get to the point where they captured the scientist or kill all the marines, which was suicide.

We personally knew all the people in our clan, so it usually ended up being a huge LAN party. God, I miss those times.

Good times, but they were short times.
