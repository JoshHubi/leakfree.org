Lanthanide November 25, 2018, 2:24am #6

I did get hit and killed by it, but only while learning how to use the grenades appropriately.

The thing with the tentacles is that when you throw a grenade, one of the heads will directly hit the spot that it exploded at, another will strike just slightly to the right of it, maybe 10 degrees, and the 3rd will be about 70-80 degrees to the left of the first one. So if you throw a grenade at some spot away from you on the same level, there’s a good chance that the 3rd head will still strike you.

The real way to handle this is to throw grenades onto different levels, then there’s no chance it’ll hurt you at all.

Going down the silo is therefore easy, as you can simply throw it a level or two below where you need to be (and break up the wooden boards with grenades from a distance, too). Coming back up from the bottom is a lot harder though, as you’re not able to throw grenades to the level above. In the end, I found throwing a grenade and simply sprinting the easiest way to get back to the top.

It’s a pity BM doesn’t have walking like HL1 did, as that is the ‘proper’ way to finish this section (not crouching, although crouching up/down HL1 ladders lets you climb them silently, whereas walking didn’t).

bmesakoki November 25, 2018, 2:24am #10

The trick to getting past the tentacles without being hit is definitely to crouch walk to each of the ladders. Just be generous with the grenades. The tentacles are on a timer, so even if you don’t make any noise after tossing a grenade, they get bored and start knocking around again. It takes patience, but is doable.

One trick is to hang out half-way on a ladder — you won’t always avoid tentacles, but if they come knocking a bit after you throw grenade, it gives you time to throw another one onto a different level. To make getting past the wooden barricades easier, I toss a grenade their way first to take care of those road blocks so I don’t have to worry about it once I reach that floor.

I probably use 4 grenades (maybe 5 on the way back up) per door. There’s enough refills along the way to keep you stockpiled.
