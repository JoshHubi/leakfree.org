exion November 24, 2018, 6:59pm #32

my flatmate is currently writing a thesis on how breaking the 4th wall can actually make a player feel more immersed, or at least garner more enjoyment from a game…

MGS is a prime example, with Psycho mantis not merely breaking the 4th wall, but reaching out of it and moving your controller with the powers of his mind! Can anyone really claim that this broke their playing experience? how about in MGS2 when Arsenal Gear is infected by a virus which starts messing with the game (with fake game over screens going off, and insane garbled orders from your superiors coming in over the codec)? Sure, that may have confused people, but that was the intent!

Further than that, the insanity effects of Eternal Darkness broke many 4th walls (with the fake savedata deletion scenes, etc) but actually improved the game in the process…

Spoof RPG, Hikkikomori, features constant 4th wall breaking moments which make the game awesome. Attempting to go into peoples houses makes the character question what he’s doing, eventually feeling he has no control over his actions and eventually killing himself to escape being manipulated by the player! Awesome!

There’s a time and a place for 4th wall breaking, and it can be done really well, but I don’t think this minor example of “Health!” is particularly game breaking. a minor annoyance perhaps, but no moreso than NPCs repeating dialogue…
