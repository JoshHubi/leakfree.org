I know what you mean: it is important to be able to rely on someone’s word. Their reaction is understandable, though not logical in my opinion.

To explain the flaw in the logic: Being promised by the devs that they will get a free game in the future, some people have grown attached to the idea that they WILL play it, meaning they look forward to it SO much, they presuppose its existence AND by that the devs’ effort and numerous other factors involved in the development. They thus hope to gain something of real or virtual value (mainly entertainment which is IMO absolutely overemphasized in today’s culture anyway). They have mentally added something to their property, although it does not even exist. It is in a way a loss of reality, by neglecting the value of the present over that of the past. The devs have not promised something easy and here your comparison fails: They have not just promised to bring back some money in an hour, but they have promised to bring you a million dollars they yet have to earn in the course of the next years. (They literally could make a million or more if they sold the mod, of that I am certain.) To detach yourself from things you “really” possess is difficult enough, I do not think you should get attached to things you may or may not have at some point in the future.

But imagine this: one day you go to the front page of the board, and you will see a download link, and below it the following sentence: “Thanks to all the fans who supported us over the years and believed in our success”.

Then, after finishing off Nihilanth, you stand in front of a beautifully rendered G-Man. The credits roll and you see the same sentence again. Would you not feel ashamed knowing that somewhere in the BMS board you wrote that you were disappointed with the dev team?
