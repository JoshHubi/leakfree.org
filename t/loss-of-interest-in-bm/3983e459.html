Code_Warrior November 24, 2018, 9:23pm #553

The way I see it there are a few issues here:

1) The problem wasn’t saying it’d come out in 2009. The problem was not updating the community when it was clear that they weren’t going to make it.

When your fans trust you, if you have to move the date they’ll be disappointed. But it’s better than leading them on for the entire year and just letting the date pass.

2) Dev teams give release windows all the time.
Sometimes they make them, and sometimes they get moved back. So “we might have to move it” is no excuse for not giving any at all. I’m all for taking time to do things correctly, but:

  • There is no perfect game.
  • Teams generally make better progress when they have a deadline to crunch around, regardless if you end up having to move it or not. Just having a real target makes a difference.

3) If anyone was around the Duke Nukem Forever community… They said the exact same things the whole way. There was plenty of “when it’s done”. And by doing that, they angered enough of the community that many people probably wouldn’t buy the game even if it magically came out tomorrow.

And… The nonexistent deadline never came (surprise). If it had, the game would probably have been irrelevant compared to the massive hype it started with.

This isn’t to say that Black Mesa is Vaporware. But when you don’t have a target, and you don’t communicate, you can blame your prospective base for feeling antsy.

Anyway… Just another 2cents.
