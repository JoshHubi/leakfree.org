There’s a few differences between versions.

P3 standard is obviously the original version of the game.

P3FES is a re-release on PS2 with additional Personas and a 30+ hour epilogue called “The Answer.” Fans criticized it for derailing characters and a lack of the social link gameplay that defined the original game (“The Journey” segment).

Persona 3 Portable (P3P) is the most radically different of the three versions. The extra Personas from P3FES are present but not “The Answer.” The battle system gets some tweaks from Persona 4, allowing direct teammate control and the ability to guard (which makes combat a whole lot more controllable). The anime cutscenes are sadly reduced to panning across still frames from them and the full 3D school exploration was cut due to space constraints, but going between the locations is faster due to the new perspective and graphics. Tartarus (the main dungeon) is still in full 3D though. The other big change is that you can play as a female protagonist instead of the male default, which completely changes the game’s social links to use different characters, and also switches up the music. For my money, some of the FeMC’s music pieces are actually better than the standard P3 soundtrack, but both are really good regardless.

I originally wanted to get a PSP from my friend and play P3P, but P3FES has been surprisingly fun so far.

But no matter what, definitely try Persona 4 at some point- it’s even better than P3. (P4 is getting a Playstation Vita re-release, which supposedly will add on a bunch of new content, so if you have a Vita get that version).
