Katana November 24, 2018, 11:08pm #79
Samapico:

Hmmmm , I played Aquaria for like 15 minutes and I wasn’t really tempted to play more… It starts slow indeed, but what is it like later on in the game?

Similar to a Legend of Zelda game, you get a number of different powers, n the form of songs that each transform you into a different form.
The first one, and often the most useful, is Energy Form, allowing you to shoot up the place with homing energy shots.
The second is beast form, which lets you swim through more powerful currents (using new powers to bypass certain barriers is a common thing in the game) and turns your ‘dash’ into a biting attack. If you eat something, you regenerate health.

You eventually work your way up to a superpowered form a bit like the Soul Cube; once you get a few kills with its normal attack, you can use its special move to destroy everything onscreen. Of course, this all happens just before the final boss.

There’s also a recipe-making system in the game. Once you pick up one of a resulting item (ie, strawberry pie) you learn the recipe (strawberry + pie). Stocking those up becomes very helpful with a number of the bosses, especially since many of them give effects like additional defense, speed, etc.

If you didn’t get to Energy Form, at least try that much and finish that dungeon. It pains me the game takes a while to get to its good parts, because I kind of wish other people would finish it.
