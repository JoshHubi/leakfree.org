I’m kinda wishing Valve had cut a deal with AMD for Mantle support on SteamOS. Unless AMD is lying about slowly loosening the reigns on Mantle until it’s open like OpenGL/supports Nvidia GPUS, Valve picking up Mantle would make things easier on devs by reducing the number of APIs they’re expected to work with (not bother with OpenGL, just port to Mantle), theoretically (because we haven’t seen any actual benchmarks yet) get even more performance than what low OS overhead alone would offer, and maybe even get BF4 onto SteamOS given that they’ve made a big deal about partnering with AMD and are gonna port to Mantle. Not to mention, it’s hard to break into a heavily established market, as with APIs, so it helps to get as much force backing a single API as possible. With Valve and AMD each supporting separate APIs, it makes it more likely that both will fail (yes, I know, OpenGL isn’t a new API, but, given its lack of use, it certainly has to break into the market).

I’m guessing Valve is trying to be as open as possible and not rely on a shortsightedly competitive manufacturer, so maybe we’ll just have to wait the long time for AMD to let mantle go open source.
