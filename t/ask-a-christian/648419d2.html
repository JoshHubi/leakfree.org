I’ll address this point because it is key to understanding evolution and, actually, even the origin of life:

The reason that “c-h-a-i-r” conveys information and “h-c-r-i-a” doesn’t, even though both have the same letters, is via the fact of natural selection. The letters “CH” bind together more easily in that order than they do in the “HC” order. It’s more likely that the letters C and H will form “CH” than they would “HC”.

A word also is more apt to survive in the wild if it has at least one vowel (A, E, I, O, U and possibly Y and rarely W) versus not having a vowel. The word “CHAIR” also survives better in the wild than “HCRIA”. Eventually, after hundreds of thousands of generations, it is rare to see a misspelled word.

Again, this is an analogy. Words themselves aren’t alive but creatures are; they can adapt and survive, their mutations either being beneficial or neutral (in which case, they’re passed on) or deleterious (in which case, they’re removed from the equation). HCRIA would be a deleterious mutation and removed. CHAIR would be a beneficial or neutral mutation and therefore passed on to future generations.

Eventually, a mutation might happen where more letters are placed onto the word and you would have something like “HIGHCHAIR” or “CHAIRMAN”. Both of these words have the common ancestor of “CHAIR” but they explain different things (one is a raised seat for babies for feeding and the other is a person at the head of a group that makes decisions or acts as a spokesperson). This is known as speciation.

Again, this is an analogy, but hopefully you get my point.
