Do you know that if you use a power adapter not made by dell (same voltage and amperage) the laptop’s bios will run the CPU at 1ghz and will refuse to charge the battery? Ok, many dell users are okay with this.

Did you know the same thing will happen if you break the 3rd pin on the adapter? Which means it’s not a feature but a crippling bug added on purpose. Every other laptop manufacturer (except apple) only has 2 pins + and _ on their adapters.

Did you know that if pin 3(data) and pin 1 or 2 short (which is likely to happen after the wire is old) the laptop’s internal “legit charger” sensor can get damaged and after that no matter what adapter you use, you won’t be able to charge the battery or run the CPU at it’s normal speed, even though there is no hardware fault inside the laptop, dell will just make you buy new chargers until you realize it’s time to move on and buy a new motherboard from ebay.
