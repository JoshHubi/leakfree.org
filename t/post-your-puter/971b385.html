malice4you November 24, 2018, 8:12pm #583

I run hardware color calibration on my displays, and while the CRTs may fade over time, I still trust the color accuracy over TN-based LCDs, which only can display 6 bits per channel, instead of 8+. Of course, for the higher-end LCDs, you get 10 bits (or sometimes even 12 bits), but you pay out the ass for a high resolution, large screen displays using those panels. Since the CRTs work just fine, I feel no need to spend $3400 for two new displays…

Widescreen does indeed give less vertical space under regular use, and especially a lot less space for photo editing. A 1920x1200 display has 2.3 million pixels, 1920x1440 display has 2.76 million pixels, and a 2048x1536 display has 3.14 million. Stepping down to a 1920x1200 display means I’d lose 460k pixels or 840k pixels of useful space (my 19" CRT runs at 1920x1440, the two 21"s run at 2048x1536). My laptop uses a shitty 1280x800 widescreen, and it is quite hard to use the computer to do much of anything, after being used to high resolution…
