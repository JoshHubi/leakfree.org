I think some of you guys are missing the point that graphics in vidya games aren’t accurate representations of the real world; they’re artistic interpretations that are made with gameplay in mind. It wouldn’t work very well if every section of the game were illuminated realistically, it’d be pretty drab. You need contrast to draw the player’s attention, you need to enforce a colour triangle to make it aesthetically appealing, you need dark shadows or harsh lights to induce unease, etc.

We’ve made plenty of concessions to preserve gameplay over realism, I don’t see why this should be any different.

The appeal and unease of HL for me was the accuracy of it’s portrayal. I’m not so fussed about the lighting in the ‘behind the scenes’ areas of Black Mesa, like the Blast Pit, that’s not really something I can identify with, but it is something I can believe in.

HL was a realistic interpretation of an unrealistic scenario.
