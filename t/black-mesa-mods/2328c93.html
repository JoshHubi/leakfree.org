w00ties:

I agreed with the ridiculousness of this statement initially, but then I realized how great Blue Shift or Opposing Force mods could be in terms of compatibility or continuity if they used Black Mesa as a base. We could play all these remakes and they would all fit together perfectly which would add to the experience.

We wouldn’t have to experience three different versions of the exact same room (say, for instance, the Black Mesa entrance to give an idea of how blatantly obvious the contrast between different variations of a room can be [despite whether or not all mods actually see that room, it’s just an example]) when playing through these remakes.

I find it ironic how uptight / flat out hostile all three remake teams (BM, OBM, GD) are to any suggestion of working together or sharing assets, or even basic coordinating to maintain some sort of continuity. I just think that’s odd because these remakes are themselves completely conceptually unoriginal by definition.

Basically, if Valve were even half as uptight as the mod teams about people “reusing” their ideas and work everyone would have gotten cease & desist letters a long time ago. As I said, ironic.

I have enormous respect for the mod teams (well particularly Black Mesa) and I appreciate their hard work, but IMHO this policy can only drag down the final quality of the mods.

this policy makes me particularly sad because frankly some of the OBM models are hideous (Bullsquid) and crap all over the original (Valve) design. I was kinda hoping someone would be able to smooth over the rough parts of the other mods with superior Black Mesa assets.
