MaxTheLimit November 24, 2018, 7:14pm #159

I’ve kinda avoided voicing my own opinion in here. So far I’ve just been refuting what seemed to be ill conceived points.
I think the power of arguments on this subject varies depending on the point in each cycle. RIGHT NOW I think that, with all aspect being considered, PC gaming is a more preferential avenue. PC hardware is so cheap right now. Options are so open, and functionally things are pretty good for games. Steam has done a lot for casual gaming fans. It’s implemented a simple functional way for people to get games that stay up to date, and work in an easy layout. I think one of the biggest things against consoles right now it the way the cycle is working for them. All of the big three are close to the same point in their cycle, which is now rather deep. While right now consoles are becoming a bit dated in capabilities ( and they are not looking to update anytime soon ) , it’s interesting to note that when the n ext gen consoles DO come they will likely be VERY powerful and FAR cheaper than comparative PC hardware. All of the big 3 will likely release a next gen console around the same period. This could be a period where the arguments for PC gaming become less valid. Comparative hardware will be more expensive. New gaming features and gaming abilities on the console will make consoles look good for over a year.
I’m just saying that both sides have merit. Right now isn’t a good time for consoles, but with consoles looking decent even in these deep cycle years, PC gaming better hope to make the most of it’s sunny days.
