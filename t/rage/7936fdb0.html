Maxey November 25, 2018, 12:57am #80

You’re probably thinking Megatexture is some sort of more advanced texture streaming tech. It does that but that’s not its point.

Megatexture is, literally, a huge resolution texture that covers the whole terrain of the game. Instead of reusing a limited number of textures throughout the world, this game actually uses a single huge resolution texture that allows artists to make every single inch of the game totally unique. Texture streaming is part of it, since the game won’t and can’t render the whole thing at once, obviously. This also requires more video memory per pass, since every texture is unique and they’re not reusing assets to save memory, which is the reason why texture pop-in is particularly bad in this game.

However, and unrelated to the megatexture tech, id wanted the game to run at a steady 60 frames per second on console and PC, and as such they had to trade off visual quality for performance, so that’s why we’re not getting very detailed textures or real time shadows in the world.
