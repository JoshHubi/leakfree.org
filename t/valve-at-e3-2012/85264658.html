gibbelin November 25, 2018, 1:31am #31

I’d bet money it’s not a console. That’s just journalists being lazy.

Everything that’s “leaked” points to an open hardware standard that works in the living room. It sounds to me like they want to bring an open experience to console players, as well as get their new biometrics peripherals out to people who aren’t just PC gamers.

So yeah, it does sound kind of like a PC that also works in the living room (in terms of hardware), but that’s also not quite right. One of the PC’s main problems as a media center is the level of commitment it takes to make it work in the living room, and the lack of good UIs for doing so.

There’s no way in hell that Steam on PCs won’t support everything this does. That would totally stupid. This isn’t about replacing the PC, it’s about broadening the PC-like experience.

The article I read that compared it to the upcoming Apple TV was a lot closer to what’s probably going on here: a more open, comprehensive living room product that combines some of the best things about PC’s, consoles, and other media centers, and brings in some completely new input devices.
