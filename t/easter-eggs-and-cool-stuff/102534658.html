I’ve stumbled across a few.

In Office Complex, you can find this sort of shrine to Richard Feynman (esteemed scientist and partial inspiration for Gordon Freeman’s character) and Hedy Lamarr, the inspiration for Kleiner’s pet headcrab’s name in HL2.

In Questionable Ethics, you can find this portrait of Schrodinger’s Cat, from his quantam mechanical model that demonstrates singularity collapse from the observation of a hyperposition of two states.

Next to the dopefish, is a Lewis structure for tetrahydrocannabinol, or THC, found in marijuana. The writing beside the illustration states “Found in Fig. 4 Specimen,” which is the dopefish.

Right beside the dopefish is another Lewis Structure, but I can’t figure out what it is. It’s also referred as being found in the dopefish, but I can’t for the life of me think of what it is. I’m sure it’s humorous if I could think of the molecule. >:I

This isn’t an easter egg…rather, I file it under "cool stuff.

Has anyone else found the gibbing system to be utterly hilarious when it comes to physics-objects?

Example: In “Office Complex”, in the room where the two scientists are hiding behind a…whiteboard? Or a screen of some kind…I knocked it over rather than throwing it away like I did the last couple of playthroughs and walked over it to go grab a few points of health from the health station…and one of the two guards following me walks over/through it and instantly explodes in a shower of blood and body parts. It was impressive, and extremely funny. Probably a bug, but eh…I consider it a feature because I have fun with it.

Had a similar thing in Residue Processing with a (dead) bullsquid getting blown up by a barrel rolling towards it at about a third of a meter per second.

On the actual “cool stuff/easter egg” side of things, many will have noticed that if you go back to the microwave after the resonance cascade, there’s a headcrab in there you can fry. If you look closely however, “Headcrab” is one of the preset buttons on the microwave". Black Mesa truly is prepared for anything…I’ve never been able to use one of those buttons on a real microwave.
