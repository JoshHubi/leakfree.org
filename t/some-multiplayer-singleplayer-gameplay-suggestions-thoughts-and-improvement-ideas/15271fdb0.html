Yes Tau currently is the strongest weapon. But it’s completely different to HL and AG because

  1. It’s almost impossible to move unpredictable and dodge enemies shots because of slow ground acceleration
  2. In current BM Tau shots register on your enemies bounding box (a lot bigger space than the actual playermodel hitboxes). This is a bug wich will be fixed in next BM update. So it will be harder to hit Tau shots.
  3. Atm a charged Tau cannon is silent in almost all situations. I think this doesn’t work as intended, because 3D sound generally doesn’t work well in BM. Silent walk is useless in BM because it’s almost impossible to hear footsteps anyway. It should be worked on this (make footsteps and charged tau louder).
  4. Normally the other weapons like Mp5, Shotgun and Crossbow are very good counters to Tau Cannon if used in the right situation. The problem is that BM’s weapons are less effective to use compared to the way they work in HL1DM and AG: The shotgun has a huge post-shot reloadtime (very low rate of fire) and accuracy of both primary and secondary shot seem lower (maybe it’s just that hit feedback could be better). MP5 has 5 more shots in HLDM and no screenmovement while spraying. This makes it a lot easier to trace your enemy and deal damage constantly while he moves through the air (overall DPS seems to be higher in BM but to me it feels like i’m missing most of the important shots because of the annoying screen movement). In HLDM the crossbow is used to set up ambushes, staying away from your enemy and dealing damage. The tracer effect in BM makes it impossible to keep your enemy unaware while shooting him. You see… All other weapons are nerfed in a way which make Tau cannon less counterable.
  5. In HL1 Crossfire is a very very agressive and dynamic map. A lot of Weapons are spread around the map but Tau Cannon is the most effective (besides Egon, which is overpowered in vanilla HL1DM, anyway) because it’s hitscan, it allows you to use a positional advantage against your enemy and wallbang him and it allows you to move around the map quickly and dodge. That said it’s obvious that Crossfire is a very Tau heavy map.

Selfgauss is very easily preventable and hardly random, all you have to do is one of three things:

  1. Do not aim at walls that can be penetrated and shoot using low charge (e.g. the pillars in Gasworks)
  2. Use the Tau only when players are in front of map walls that cannot be penetrated.
  3. Charge up your shots before shooting.

As simple as this sounds, it’s much more of a problem playing HLDM where everyone’s moving really fast and one bad shot can get you down 60hp or more, and this mechanic rewards quick thinking.
Certain maps have a higher selfgauss potential than others, Crossfire IIRC does not have many thick walls that aren’t the map walls so it’s harder to selfgauss, but other maps do.
Also, the gauss cannon in vanilla HLDM is broken in the fact that LMB uses up two ammo but deals 20 damage, whereas RMB uses 1 but deals much more (i am assuming 60-80), but then the drawback is high selfgauss potential as the charge is very low. This isn’t present in BMDM as each shot requires 3 ammo to use before firing, which is a good change to make.

However, I have not seen AG’s take on the Gauss, perhaps it’s much better than the HLDM version.
