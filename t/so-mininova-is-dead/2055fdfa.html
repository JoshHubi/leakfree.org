Why in the world do so many people seem to believe that other people pirating games doesn’t affect a person who chooses not to do so? It affects EVERYONE.

The point has been made multiple times: stealing games, as a principle, reduces profits. I don’t care if you personally only steal games that you wouldn’t buy, or if you buy games after you pirate them. Not everyone does this, and the more accepted piracy becomes, the more people will steal and not buy.

This equals less money for companies. I don’t care if you think it’s bad and evil that people should wish to get an actual benefit for their work, instead of wanting to put hundreds of man-hours into a final product solely because it’ll give them some mystical feeling that will somehow translate into food and rent. This isn’t how the world works. People work for a tangible benefit - and if the benefit disappears, so does the quality of the product and eventually the product itself.

I like games. I don’t want to see game quality disappear. Even if pirates today spend more than they steal, I highly doubt it’ll be like that forever. I quite don’t want to see games I enjoy disappear, or at least go down in quality, because others chose to steal.

So yes, it DOES affect me. Don’t try to tell me otherwise.
