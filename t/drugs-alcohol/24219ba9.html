Economically because a lot of government revenue comes from taxes on alcohol, and a lot socially because you lose all control on the sale and distribution of alcohol. People who are addicted to alcohol, or who suffer other medical conditions because of alcohol can’t access the services they need. Just telling them that “its their own damn fault” isn’t the answer that you should give as a government; you send them to hospital, fix them up, put them into rehab (or give them to AA) and let them become productive members of society. Restricting people’s access to such treatments means that they’re less likely to become healthy again, which means they’re less likely to contribute to society which means you lose social and economic productivity.
