^ This. You should look at the two games as completely seperate entities. The plot connections are very loose, and are purposely kept that way so Valve doesn’t have to worry about continuity issues too much. If they do goof and state contradictory facts, nobody should care. The events of Portal aren’t going to affect Half-Life, and the events of Half-Life aren’t going to affect portal too much. As I said, they may take the combine invasion of Earth and near genocide of humanity to explain why nobody messed with the facility, but I think that is where it will end, and even then I don’t think they are going to dig too deep into it. I don’t even think they’d mention the combine by name.

The whole reason the two games are even connected is that Portal was just an interesting gameplay experiment that Valve was conducting. Portal had eight developers working on it. Valve didn’t want to waste too many resources in its development in case it didn’t go well. So they recycled elements from Half-Life to form the back drop of portal. In Episode 2 they mention Aperture Science and their work with Portal technology as a plot device, and Kliener mentions their recklessness. So Valve just decided to play around with that. There are no major plot dependencies between the two. You really aren’t going to learn much about one from the other, and you really aren’t going to need to know much about one to understand the other. Yet, despite this, people go all out to connect every minor reference and formulate every kind of crossover. Its not what Valve intends people to do.
