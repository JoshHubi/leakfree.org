Interesting Findings on the Airstrike

Black Mesa Archive The Cafeteria
absolute_imperial November 25, 2018, 3:41am #1

So I was playing through Black Mesa again and I got to my favorite part of the game: the airstrike on the desert facility while you are on the cliff. I’ve always thought the visuals of this part as well as the massive scale of the scenery were very enlightening.

I began to wonder what the actual scale of the entire scene was. To determine it, I timed the shock wave from its instance to when it reaches the player on the cliff. The average time is almost exactly 3.5 seconds. Knowing the shock wave travels at the speed of sound in Earth’s atmosphere, I calculated the distance from the player to the facility. It is about 1.232 km or roughly 0.77 miles.

I was somewhat disappointed in my findings because it appears at least visually that the facility is about 2 to 3 miles away. If that is the appearance the Black Mesa team was going for, the jets and the shock wave should have both traveled considerably slower.

I’m sure I’m not the first person to think to check this, but I thought it was worth sharing none the less.

Swaggless November 25, 2018, 3:41am #9
stormseeker:

First of all it’s important to note that Level Designers tend to use the human comparison (because Gordon is not 4ft tall) for scaling in Hammers unit system, whilst we use the standard of 128 units for wall height etc, we rarely use that for actual corridor heights as it looks odd. As such we use the 1unit = 1" rule rather than the .75" actual. It’s why you’ll often find info_player entities scattered around the map.

As such my calculations were as thus.

Date of incident 16th May (average temperature outside is usually between 50F and 80F in new Mexico, however the high on that day was 105F - [105-32/1.8 = 40.5c]

Spd of sound @ 20c = 343m/s approx (767.3 mph)
Spd of sound @ 40.5c = 355m/s approx (794.1 mph)

Distance to Comms Base Facility from skybox camera is at an angle, therefore:

O - 2783 units
A - 5477 units

H = 2783^2+5477^2 = ¬37742618 = 6143.5 units (6144 units)

angle = 2783/5477 = 0.51 tan^-1 = 27 degrees.

6144 units @ skybox scale (0.0625 or 1/16th) = 98304" or 8192’ (8192/3.2808 = 2496.9 [2497m]) or about 2.5km (1.55 miles)

Therefore the Shockwave from the blast should be 2497 / 355 = 7.03 seconds.

Actual shockwave timing as per entity reduction (for gameplay purposes)

= 3.6 seconds. [This is based on the trigger onPass from the planes, enacting .55 seconds after the planes pass the pathtrack point ‘b_pt09a’ to allow for bomb drop timing, up until the sound is played at 4.10s in from trigger - ie 3.55s]

This would mean the actual speed of the shockwave is approx 695m/s or 1554.7 mph. Which would require an air temperature of 1000c, so obviously not accurate.

Speed of the planes.

“on trigger set speed 750.”

AV8-B.II for USMC max speed = 673 mph
750 units = 1000ft/s or 304.8 m/s = 681mph

however there are the AV8-A and AV8-C variants that have a top speed of 731mph, so this is not unreasonable.

Distance therefore takes - 8.2 seconds to reach the base.

Total time for full scene (approx) -

Original before testing = 15.23 seconds.
After testing and reductions = 11.8 seconds.

so about 3.4 seconds sooner than in reality.

That was really interesting. A lot of work and thought went into that. Thanks!
