Orpheon November 24, 2018, 9:54pm #6
Fuskox:

I’ve been moderately interested in EVE, but it looks like you have to really play a lot to keep up with other players, I do, however like how people make their own alliances and factions, what’s the combat like?

Actually EVE is probably the game you have to play the least. When you play the game, you play to get money because the game mainly is based around the economy. Your skills you queue up to train over time - the first level of a skill trains very quickly, in a few minutes. The very highest skills that are possible to train literally take months. But your skills train constantly whether you are logged in or not, so you can set a few to train and not play for days, come back and they’re done.

Combat is actually pretty fun, but this is where the math comes in. Your weapons have accuracy falloffs and tracking speeds. Basically, you have to know what range to attack from. Small ships with small guns have short ranges but a high tracking speed, so they can circle larger ships at a short distance and attack them because the large guns can’t track them fast enough. Large ships, however, can carry drones, small, agile attack fighters that you can command to attack specific targets. Then you have to know how to outfit your ship to withstand certain types of enemies, whether to shield-tank or armor-tank your ship, if you just want to have powerful guns or if you want to do electronic warfare…all sorts of stuff. Like every game it’s pretty complicated but IMO it’s not too difficult to figure out.
