higgsboson November 25, 2018, 2:35am #3

I think that “Valve quality” varies quite a lot: HL1 was quite low quality in terms of what you mention sometimes, and there were many moments in HL2 in my opinion that aren’t that great either. Though both games are overall amazing, and so is Black Mesa, and I think that it has the same amount of attention given to it. I really don’t feel that Episode 2’s graphics and ideas are better than Black Mesa in any way.

Some things, like voice acting and final polishing (like precise music placement, weapon balancing, etc…) might not always be absolutely spot on in BM compared to most of Valve’s stuff, but these are quite minor and yet they require lots and lots of testing, which takes a long time. I’m sure that the BM devs could have gone through more playtesters and taken a few more years polishing the game, and they would have been able to easily make those things absolutely perfect, but it would simply not have been worth it.

If you think about it, things like balancing weapons were terrible in HL at some times: the useless Snarks, the sparse ammo for the Gluon gun, the extreme amounts of MP5 grenades you got, the similarity in strength of most weapons… were all quite badly done, and BM does this a lot better.

MaxTheLimit November 25, 2018, 2:35am #4

Well no, I wouldn’t say BM is quite what we would see if Valve released it. But Valve is a very large company with lots of resources. They would be able to locate bugs on a much larger scale. They would have more bodies dedicated to polish and finish. They would be able to hire enough voice actors so that no two key characters would have to share the same voice actor.

This really isn’t a fair comparison. Having access to a budget of millions has benefits. Nothing to do with dedication, ability or planning. It is just practical limitations that hold you back when that asset isn’t there.

That BM is so damned near that quality though…well…it’s downright astounding. I can’t think of an adequate superlative for how impressed I am.
