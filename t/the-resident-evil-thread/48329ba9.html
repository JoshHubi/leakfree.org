GordonFreeman November 24, 2018, 10:09pm #43

I would say that my enjoyment of RE1, RE Zero, and Code Veronica (the only “original-style” ones I’ve played) were hampered by the bad camera angles, weak weapons and the awkward controls. Getting eaten by an enemy that I didn’t see coming can be frustrating after the fifth time. Can still appreaciate the games for what they were. A new direction in gaming.
RE4 for me is a blast. It does stray from the survival horror feel of the older Resident Evil games, but that makes it a refreshing change from the “silly-premise-taken-seriously” plot of the old ones. RE4 by no means takes itself too seriously. The characters are outrageously hammy, the plot is simple and the dialogue is hilariously absurd. And the way it’s executed is brilliant. I love it. Plus, the Mercaneries mode has fun gameplay and in-game benefits. The over-the-shoulder, rotating camera REALLY helps me enjoy it as well.
Playing RE5 can be really unenjoyable if you take the game’s plot as seriously as it takes itself. I have thoroughly enjoyed RE5 past all it’s annoying gamplay aspects, the blatant rascism and frequent quick-time events for one reason. It’s HILARIOUSLY rediculous story. The dialogue, the characters, the settings (maybe not so much, except for the VOLCANO). All makes for one funny, absurd tale. It’s different from RE4 as RE4 was somewhat self-aware as to how silly it was. RE5 assumes you’ll take all the bullshit it gives, and that can be entertaining. The cutscenes with Albert Wesker (who’s been stealing clothes from JC Denton’s wardrobe) are side-splittingly hilarious. One scream of “CHRISSSSSSS!!!” makes me just crack up. They also keep the Mercaneries mode in this one too, which still maintains it’s awesomeness.

Final Personal Verdict:
RE 1 -> RE 4, Good games, frustrating controls and bad camera angles
RE 4 +, Enjoyable parodies of survival horror games

RE5 is too heavy on the action and light the survival, RE has historically been a survival/horror series. For instance, action elements include the waves of enemies, lots of ammo, and a real-time, limited-in-size backpack (cant upgrade size and cant pause the game to manage). The only real survival element is the OCCASIONAL puzzle and inability to shoot while moving which is simply annoying because RE5 is so action oriented. RE4 is the best blend between action and survival, while RE5 just took it over the edge with the action. The earlier REs can be considered more horror/survival with their puzzles and treasures, lack of ammo, fewer numbers of enemies to fight at once, more startle/scare tactics, etc.
