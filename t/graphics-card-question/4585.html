Orpheon August 11, 2020, 6:56pm #7

You are definitely going to want to get more RAM when you buy your card as well. I don’t know how you’re managing on 512mb but it can’t be very well. Get at least 2GB, 3gb or 4gb is preferable. Your CPU is decent enough, since it’s a P4 and listed twice, that means it’s hyperthreaded (simulates having two cores while only having one).

I would recommend either a Radeon 5770 or a GeForce GTX 260. Both are good performers and can be had for about $150-$180 on newegg.com.

The most important thing though is that you make sure what your bus speeds are. That is, you need to make sure what kind of RAM your system takes, and you need to make sure your computer has a PCI-Express x16 slot. It should, but with somewhat older computers you can’t be entirely sure. Make sure you have a slot that looks like this:

600×617 63.3 KB

If you don’t, then you have an AGP slot and you’ll be greatly limited in what kind of card you can get. As for the RAM speeds, use CPU-Z to find out what kind of RAM you need.

I would recommend upgrading your CPU to something more modern (You can get a Quad 2.5Ghz CPU for $150 over at neweegg.com!). A RAM boost would be a good idea also 4GB is good. For G-cards I prefer Readon just because of it’s better pricing over Nvidia, Nvidia does have nicer drivers though.

If you want Nvidia G-cards go for the Geforce 250’ish and above cards or if you want Readon G-cards go for the 57xx and above G-cards. Biggest problem is if you don’t have the right motherboard sockets PCI-E 2.0 x16 is what you need for newer G-cards and LGA 775 CPU socket for anything above a single-core CPU. If you don’t have those you’ll need a new computer, although if you follow my advice you’ll basically have a new computer.
