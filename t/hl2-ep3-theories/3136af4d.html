You can either be of the opinion that time travel is possible, or that it isn’t. Valve seems to believe that it isn’t possible, as each time something happens that seems like time travel, it’s actually not.

Gordon is put into stasis by the G-Man: this is not time travel, it is just allowing him to stay preserved without aging or dyeing.

The slow teleport from Nova Prospect isn’t time travel either: it does not allow information to travel from the future to the present.

Time travel always causes a paradox and personally I think it’s impossible. As Valve seems to keep avoiding it, I think they would not suddenly introduce time travel into the Half-Life universe. With time-travel, you could just go back and prevent the Resonance Cascade, and the entire point of the game would be gone.

Oxidious November 24, 2018, 11:35pm #96

With the game Portal, Valve already introduced time travel. By creating 2 portals to travel between, Valve not only introduced a way of travelling through space, but also a way of travelling through time. Ask yourself, how can Chelle step into a portal and appear at another portal much further away instantly? The way I see it, the portal gun creates a pocket universe which allows Chelle to travel through. A pocket universe which has a slower timing than our own. These pocket universes are created and destroyed each time Chelle creates a new portal path.

Now with the Borealis, Aperture was able to create a portal/pocket universe whos timing was much faster than our own, resulting in the Borealis appearing in the future when it travelled through the portal. The future/present portal is now closed and Gordon must figure out how to activate it to travel back in time to the past/present.

Everyone seems to be quoting theories, so there’s mine.
