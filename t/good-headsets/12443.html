Bolteh November 25, 2018, 3:36am #2

I gave up on headsets because I have never found one with proper sound quality on the Mic.

So I simply went with the more expensive combination of having really good Sennheiser headphones (wireless) with an external condenser microphone. I use both the headphones and the microphone for playing guitar as well, so it was worth the extra cost.

So if you’re seriously considering doing “Let’s Play” videos (don’t, by the way, there are already way too many of them and they’re just boring as fuck), I’d suggest looking at external microphones.

Bolteh August 11, 2020, 10:31am #11

I recommended a condenser mic, any condenser mic is better than a headset mic.

Anyway, this is what I use: https://www.apogeedigital.com/products/one-mac.php, it’ll be useless to 99% of you on the account of it not having Windows drivers, but the internal condenser mic on that is great and the added ability to plugin a guitar and even a second external mic is just superb. There’s no lag, quality is great, I can pop it at the end of the desk and people can still hear me fine (albeit a bit roomy).

The headphones I use them with: https://en-us.sennheiser.com/digital-headphones-wireless-home-audio-rs-180

Great sound quality, no annoying cables to bugger me, no need to worry about batteries since the receiver station can recharge the headphones. The only slightly annoying thing is that I have to turn the volume of my speakers way up when I plug the headset into he control-puck of said speakers.

The speakers I use, by the way, are these: https://www.bose.com/controller?url=/shop_online/speakers/computer_speakers/companion_20/index.jsp

And since you don’t expect anyone to watch your LPs and you’re just doing it as a way to spend time, go ahead. I just didn’t want you to get disappointed when your videos get 10 views (9 of which are by your friends or family).
