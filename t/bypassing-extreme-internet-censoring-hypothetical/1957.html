Bypassing extreme internet censoring? (hypothetical!)

bur November 24, 2018, 6:56pm #1

Let me just say, all of this is purely hypothetical, I’m not a conspiracy nut. I’m not planning on overthrowing my government and installing a new government either :3
It’s important to a story in development by someone I know.

First of all, if a future totalitarian government wished to monitor and censor all internet traffic, and prohibit connection to foreign networks (of course the government itself would still be able to do that), how would it go about it?

My idea was some sort of replacement for the Internet Protocol that only allows you to connect to networks with a certain signature.

How would one then bypass such censoring? In other words, how would one connect to networks that don’t have a particular signature for example.

My idea on that was to use old hardware with old software (still using IPv4, for example) to connect to other networks or computers with the same protocols.

Which brings me to my final point: how would people communicate? There would only be a few servers using the old protocols still running (without the government’s knowledge), so how would one easily find eachother, with standard present-day applications?

TL;DR: how do you use present-day household technology to circumvent a hypothetical future censored national (i.e. cut off from the rest of the world) internet, and would that ‘national internet’ work?

I hope someone can help me with this.

So, it’d be some system that only allows you to go through to certain sites you mean? Depends where the block exists. If it existed in your computers hardware or software, there would be modded cards or cracks made to circumvent the system.
