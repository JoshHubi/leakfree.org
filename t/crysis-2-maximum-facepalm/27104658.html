I consider it the game’s fault when the average frame rate decreases by half after a significant chunk of the game (and from there on out), especially when the issue does not appear with any other visual effect.

Part of good game development is maintaining stable performance, because a rapidly fluctuating frame rate will pull players out of the game and force them to tinker with settings. Their testing process should have identified the inconsistent draw on resources compared to other stuff in the game and, if they were interested in providing a decent tech demo (something that said “hey, this thing scales!”), they would have implemented a functioning fallback.
