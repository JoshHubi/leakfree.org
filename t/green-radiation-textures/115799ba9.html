The fuel pellets are dissolved in concentrated nitric acid, then mixed with a solution of tributyl phosphate in kerosene, this forms a complex which removes most of the uranium and plutonium from solution, leaving the other nasty radioisotopes behind (primarily Cs-137 and I-131, at least those are the most dangerous two). This gunk left over (basically, a mixture of nitrate sales of Cs-137 and I-131 in nitric acid, mixed with kerosene) is dumped in underground tanks.
