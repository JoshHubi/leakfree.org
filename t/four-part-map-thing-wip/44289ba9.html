Wow, it’s cool to see this evolving. Definitely good for a first effort. I’m glad you figured out that pure white lights look awful and unrealistic in a setting like this… a lot of first-timers live in denial of that fact. As a tip for your further lighting endeavors: you look like you’re starting to get the hang of lighting areas pretty well, but try playing around with the Constant parameter of the light entities (by default, the prevailing setting is the Quadratic one). A Quadratic mode of lighting makes the light fall off somewhat more realistically, but it leaves the intense splotches on the ceiling around the lights that you see in your first screenshots and, to a lesser extents, the latest ones on this page. The Constant parameter causes the light to cast a glow in all directions that is - believe it or not - constant. One light with a relatively high “constant” setting (and with Quadratic and Linear set to 0), placed in the center of a room, will brighten the entire room.
