Grey_Acumen November 24, 2018, 5:21pm #52

I usually look at it like this:

  1. fun
  2. logical
  3. true to original

any decision made should at least have 1 of these points, but if the option is to grab 2 or all of them, go for whichever gets you the most points.
In the case of the crossbow, using the source style would have been fun, but neither logical nor true to original. Keeping it original would get you logical and true to original, even if not AS fun.

another point to consider is that for Half Life, the fact that the game at least TRIED to be logical. even if many points were contrived, they at least weren’t completely arbitrary “x button on one end of the map opens Y door on the other side for no apparent reason” I had always assumed that the suspended crates actually had some logical reason for being there, but it simply hadn’t been properly presented in the game. Perhaps that black abyss actually led down to a much deeper level into which the boxes could be lowered for storage? I have no idea, but even if the logic wasn’t apparent, it was assumed to be there. The same is not really possible when you’re talking about a tranq dart that hurls its victims 30 feet and pins them to a wall.
