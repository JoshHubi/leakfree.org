The hardest part is balance. When I was first starting, I had to have my feet really wide apart, and I almost needed to have my upper body twisted at 90 degrees so I could push out to the side to lift myself up instead of pushing forward, but as I got stronger I was able to bring my feet closer together and bring my body more parallel with the ground, and let my arm handle more of the balance control.
Also, if you still aren’t able to do one while your feet are set wide apart, you may simply need to build up your strength a bit more. Start with regular pushups and try to work up the number until you can do at LEAST 20 and try working towards 50. At this point you should be able to start raising your feet up higher, like putting your feet on a chair while your hands are still on the floor. This will put more of your body weight over your shoulders so you’re lifting more when doing pushups and help get you ready for when you attempt to do a full pushup with one hand.

And of course, remember to keep your back straight with one hand or regular pushups. You don’t want your butt or stomach sagging so that you’re only lifting your shoulders up.
