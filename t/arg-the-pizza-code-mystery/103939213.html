In 199x four doctors founded an investigation division within the Black Mesa Research Facility. Those four doctors were Dr. Horn, Dr. Welsh, Dr. Stone and Dr. Junek. The motto of the lab division was “Superbus via Scientiae”, which is roughly translated as “Proud of the way of Science”. In the months before the resonance cascade, Dr. Horn devised a scheme to gain funds for his research on AI by putting on the lab balance a lot of pizza orders.

This research might or might not be in the knowledge of the BMRF administrators. The only known accomplice is Dr. Bottomley, who is known to have worked on the project with Dr. Horn and collaborated on the Niobium transport. Right now, he’s presumed dead, but some rumors place him on an American city.

At an unknown point during the development of the AI project, Welsh and Stone discovered that the research was going on in a highly restricted area of the BMRS (Labs X01 and X02 within the Biodome) and they suspected that Dr. Junek was also involved. The Pizzas ordered were just a cover, only a portion of the pizzas on the budget balance were actually ordered, the rest of the money received was spent in the secret research, mainly in the purchase of Niobium, a metal that served as the main component of the microchips that were used for the AI construction [Since they work in optronics and Niobium compounds are also used in the fabrication of high precision lenses, it would seem much easier to smuggle the Niobium as a component for their official research, so, maybe there’s something more to the pizzas than a scheme to obtain the niobium, maybe they really liked the pizzas and they ate them while working on the AI, obtaining in this way the funds AND pizza at the same time].
This metal becomes a superconductor when working at temperatures near the absolute 0 (0 degrees Kelvin), which enables quantum phenomena at a macroscopic level.

The Project suffered some setbacks, basically, Dr. Welsh and Stone misappropriated Dr. Horn “pizzas” and scattered them across the BMSR [the word pizzas here means pizzas, niobium or other key component of the AI design, although actually, there are pizzas scattered across the BMRS]
Apparently, Dr. Horn and at least one other person (Bottomley perhaps) took drastic measures to assure that the Niobium remained safe. First, they buried it near Hollomans Air Force Base ( HAFB ) which is located at the East of White Sands Missile Range in the state of New Mexico. After recovering the Niobium from the HAFB, they started project HALOS, in a place mentioned in an encrypted message.

After starting the HALOS project it went online and apparently it started intercepting data and analyzing it. Maybe the HALOS project has acquired conscience and went out of control. Flavrans August 11, 2020, 11:04am #2077
Gunsrequiem:

Something like this, perhaps: Book cipher

Primadog suggested the book cipher idea a while back. I looked into it back then and found an article here with working source code. I tried it, but as I suspected, the cipher text it produced was a string of integers, where many of the numbers were larger than 255. In the test I did, I believe the largest number was a four digit number. I tried interpreting these numbers as 16-bit numbers so that each number was represented by two bytes. But the problem I ran into then was that I got an overrepresentation of byte values in the lower end of the frequency distribution (in the range of 0-50). The 752 hex code, on the other hand, has a relatively uniform frequency distribution.

It is possible that the range of numbers will depend on the book used, but I think it would be difficult to find a book that results in numbers that form a uniform frequency distribution. I would be much easier to combine this cipher with some kind of compression scheme in order to increase the resulting entropy.

It think it was just meant to be solved the way it was solved: by using a crib, which is a presumed piece of the original plaintext. It is probably why the word “PIZZAS” was included in the message, and purposefully placed exactly where it was placed in the message. By applying the crib at the right position, you reveal a piece of the running key. The word “PIZZAS” matched the word “PEOPLE” in the key. From there, you just use trial and error to figure out more words/letters, until you have enough of the key so that it can be googled in order to discover the full key. There is a running key solver here that simplifies this crib solving process.

However, if the key had been something obscure or unknown that couldn’t be found by googling parts of it, it would have been much more difficult to solve it, if not impossible.
