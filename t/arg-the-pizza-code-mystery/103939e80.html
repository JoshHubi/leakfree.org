The whiteboard you mentioned also has some key reasons it might be important:

  1. It too mentions leptons.
  2. The Higgs-TauTau is a reference to the Higgs boson and its decay into tau leptons. Furthermore, the cannon is also called the “Tau cannon.”
  3. Yet another reference to the cannon: “Interesting side effect of neodymium optronic causeators”–I’m pretty sure it says “causeators,” but it might be some other word.

Adding some notes on this one:

  1. The symbol used to denote an anti-up quark in the feynman diagram of Tau-lepton decay looks like the letter J, instead of the dashed “u” symbol that it should be.
  2. The circled “Interaction here” is the Tau-lepton decaying into a Tau-neutrino and emitting a W(-)-Boson under the weak interaction.
  3. It seems to read “calibrators”, instead of causeators.
  4. Higgs to Tau-Tau decay is a mechanism that’s only extremely recently been proven to exist (The data was collected during 2011 and 2012, with the results being published here in the beginning of 2015.)
  5. The symbol ∅ used in the middle of the Higgs-Boson in the Feynman diagram for Higgs to Tau-Tau decay is not used in Feynman diagrams.
