That is not at all how HORN-SAT works.

First off, SAT is short for satisfiability. It is the name for the general problem of finding an assignment to BOOLEAN variables such that the resulting conjunction (ie. logical AND) of boolean clauses is TRUE. It is a very difficult problem to solve in general.

HORN-SAT is a special case where the boolean formula is a a conjunction of HORN clauses. A horn clause contains at most one non-NEGATED variable. This problem is much easier to solve due to its structure.

The variables in these formula are over boolean space, meaning that they are 1/0 (TRUE/FALSE). Nowhere does it state that you can just plug in integer values and start performing arithmetic to arbitrarily declare TRUE/FALSE.
