Because violence existed before video games. Ergo, video games does not cause violence. Its not a simple causal link, since kids were bashing each other up long before MW2.

Here’s a post I made on another forum outlining the two cases and which one wins:

loony636:

Video games don’t cause violence, they’re just a tool used by some to create a moral panic. The theory of moral panics works thusly: A politician/media outlet/‘expert’ advertises a particular problem; in this case, youth violence. It advertises a particular cause for the particular problem; in this case, video games. Hence, it advertises a solution for the problem; banning ‘violent’ video games. They convince everyone that the sole cause of youth violence is video games, and gets everyone so worked up about the situation that they ban violent video games, and celebrated the politician/editor/‘expert’ as a hero of the people. This concept is used all the time and has a major role to play in politics, but you shouldn’t think that these moral panics have no basis in plausible fact, its just that they end up being used merely as a political tool, rather than a cogent theory supported by conclusive evidence. Here are the arguments for and against video games causing violence:

For:

* It normalises violence in kids lives, meaning that they will feel more comfortable doing it and more likely to partake in violent activities in real life.
* Kids often play violent video games in households where the parents have no idea what they're playing, hence there's no control on these activities, meaning that their influence is unlimited and highly dangerous.
* By banning them completely, you remove all possibility of them every impacting on children again, since even if they're 18+, children can still get their hands on them.

These arguments are archaic, backward and disappointingly biased.

Against:

* Kids are able to differentiate between reality and fantasy, and they realise that violence in a video game is not the same as violence in real life, hence it doesn't normalise violent tendencies.
* It provides a safe outlet and opportunity to explore feelings that can't be explored in normal society (like driving too fast or shooting a gun with unlimited ammunition.
* Youth violence was just as much of a problem before video games were created, hence there's no causal link between violence in kids and video games.
* Its indicative of broader cultural values in such a society; if a kid is violent, then its because of the culture of where he lives, not of what video game he plays.
* Its the role of the parents to provide a stable upbringing; you disguise the true problem by clouding it with the purported 'solution' which will do nothing to solve the problem.
* Even if you ban the games in one country, any kid with a modem can download the games off the internet. Its better to keep the available in stores only at MA15+ or R18+ ratings so that parents at least know what games their kids have access to (since generally people will buy games before downloading them, both because its easier and more convenient to buy them, and because people do have moral qualms about downloading games, so they wont do it unless they have no other means with which to buy the game), and so you can actually maintain some control over their distribution.
* Video games are fun. Depriving children of video games is more likely to make them bored/angry/frustrated, which means they're more likely to partake in anti-social behaviour.

Every generation thinks that the next generation is far worse than they were, but in reality youth crime rates have decreased compared to historical norms.
