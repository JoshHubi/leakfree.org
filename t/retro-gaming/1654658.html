Phil November 24, 2018, 5:27pm #35

From the 80s: An action adventure game called Exile on the BBC. This must be one of the most sophisticated games written on an 8-bit micro. It had a huge game world with a semi-procedurally generated cave system (because otherwise it wouldn’t have fit in 32K of RAM), physics engine and AI where enemies could hear you and remember where they last saw you. And the game design was phenomenal. There were amazing ideas here that are still worth looking at.

There are loads of games I still love from the 90s, many mentioned here. I guess Doom (even if Half-Life is the more important game IMO), the Secret of Monkey Island and the original GTA would be my favourites.
