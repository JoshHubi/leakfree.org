That_One_Guy November 24, 2018, 11:37pm #111

I was not a fan of Beyond Black Mesa. To think that people would call this a fan film while totally ignoring both the canon and the art-direction of the Half-Life games is pretty unforgiveable.

Fan films thrive on fan service, and by fan service I don’t mean tits in anime, I mean nods to the game for fans to enjoy.

Though I could commend this for taking the basics of Half-Life and creatively interpreting them, it simply was far too different, and far too mindless.

And quite frankly, sword-fighting Combine Soldiers and 28 Days Later-style running zombies don’t belong in anything Half-Life. Either slap a headcrab on them and have them run like the feral, ferocious Fast Zombies, or don’t include them.

This film worries too much about the look and not enough about characters and actually emulating the tone of the games. At no point did I feel the desperation of these resistance fighters. Simple as that.
