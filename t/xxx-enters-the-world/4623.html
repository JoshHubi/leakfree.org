Bolteh November 24, 2018, 9:44pm #1

That’s right kids, porn sites will be able to register a .xxx extensions.

Anyway, that’s not really the biggest news (according to me, at least).

People have been trying to persuade ICANN (the company that is responsible for the domain-system) to allow for custom extensions for certain companies and regions. Banks could register a .bank, or the state of Florida could register .florida. ICANN, until now, had always declined these requests.

Porn getting .xxx is hopefully the first in a line of many to get this treatment.

Could also be used for better indexing and categorizing. I also opens up the market for companies. For example: a new bank called “Money’r’Us” opens its doors, they want to launch a website (duh), but https://www.moneyrus.com/us/net/org is already taken by something non-bank-ish. That company could now register https://www.moneyrus.ban.

This can also add a certain (albeit thin) layer of security, because if you get a phishing mail, supposedly sent from your bank, and it leads you to a website that is nameofyourbankplusarandomcharacter.com, you can quickly see that it’s fake because it isn’t .bank (people notice the extension faster than the actual domainname).
