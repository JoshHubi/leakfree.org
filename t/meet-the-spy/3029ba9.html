Wasabi:

Learn to ‘masculine and feminine’ mate.

Mon, referring to a masculine subject.
Ma, referring to a feminine subject.

Indeed, but it’s the gender of the word that matters, not the gender of the person being referred to. So it’s still “mon petit chou-fleur” rather than “ma petite chou-fleur”, since the noun “chou-fleur” is masculine.

Interestingly, “chou-fleur” translates literally as “cabbage-flower”, but “chou” can also be used to mean “darling”, which is possibly why “chou-fleur” can be used as a term of endearment - “darling-flower”
