I’ve been reading up on big picture mode. Sounds like it’s an option to consolize your pc. A mode where you can interact with steam in the same way one interacts with an xbox.

So, if this is console mode with controller support, might Valve add splitscreen? It’s the obvious direction to take this, and though controller gaming would never work with competitive games, games like L4D and Portal 2 would do just fine with controller splitscreen on the pc as would any other co-op campaign. It doesn’t even have to be limited to Valve games, with the feature in existence I wouldn’t be surprised if other developers would work with Steam to make it happen in their games too, not unlike steam workshop support for Skyrim. I’ve brought pc splitscreen up before and most people said no one would ever take advantage of the feature. They’re absolutely full of shit, and steam big picture makes a splitscreen controller option a hell of a lot more appetizing than it already was.

It may be wildly optimistic, but I think it’s possible that splitscreen will be announced as a component of big picture either at E3 or somewhere down the line. If anyone is going to see the advantage in a new idea like this, it’s going to be Valve.

Also note that a fairly simple mod gives L4D1 splitscreen support using code left from the console version, so it’s obviously not difficult to add the feature.
