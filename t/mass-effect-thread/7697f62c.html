to the question asked “Why did they think it was okay to do the exact same plot device they used in the first game, down to forcing him to kill him self. It was incredibly lazy.” allow me to rant a bit

ME2’s writing was extremely lazy.
How many times did someone who was believed to be a good guy, turn into a bad guy? Wilson at the very beginning, the warden on jack’s fetch mission, nikket for miranda’s, jacob’s dad, Malin during Mordin’s loyalty, the SPECTRE in hte shadow broker DLC, the scientists in the Arrival DLC, Archer in the Overlord DLC,
how many times did a gun get pulled at the end of a loyalty mission for “dramatic emphasis”? Miranda on nikket, Mordin on Malin, Jacob on his father, Jack on that other guy
not to mention most of the “decisions” throughout the whole series result in either you meet the same person you did before because you saved them, or another person who does the same exact thing anyway with a different name. That’s what bugged me the most in the series. there was hardly ever an occasion where if someone died, or was convinced not to do something, it made a difference later. killed the rachni queen? cool they made a new one. rewrote geth? eh well we’ll just make it so they ally themselves with the reapers again anyway. and there are so many other examples… its like… if bioware really wanted to make decisions matter and have a big impact, things needed to be more permanent. Killed the rachni queen? they are gone. done. no reaper-rachni. rewrote the geth, cool now just deal with geth-quarrian relations, don’t bring them back to the reapers. someone died, tough luck. mordin for example, i think it would have been really interesting for the only way to cure the genophage, was to both save the data and mordin lives. (curing a genitic desease that has plagued a race for hundreds of years should not be easy)

Also i feel like the a lot of the animations for stuff was really off and not fluid. (slight tangent rant on indoctrination theory) the whole unlimited ammo bit… that’s bioware saying “we just really dont want you to run out of bullets and thats all” they did the same thing at the beginning of the tutorial. when you first get your pistol it has unlimited ammo. also, i saw in one argument “the speed that you walk doesn’t match the speed that the ground is moving so its as if your floating” or the animations for walking that slowly just have a bad sync… The “armor burned off + in space = Shepard would die”… Bioware has shone a HUGE disregard to skin exposure to space. jack liara grunt and plenty others have exposed skin at times when they are exposed to space / zero pressure vacuum and nobody seemed to care then…

i dunno, i just really felt like a lot of the decisions made ended up having hollow conclusions. i’m probably looking at it a bit too harshly, within reason it would be extremely difficult to pull off the expectations i had… but still bioware is suppose to be one of the top story driven RPG developers… if not them to pull it off who else?

end rant.
