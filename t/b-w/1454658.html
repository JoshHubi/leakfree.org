I got a bit of talent. My dad went to art school himself when he was about my age. But he didn’t teach me anything directly, though he does encourage me. I remember he tried to teach me watercolors, but that didn’t get anywhere.

I took one year of art in highschool, Freshman year. (I’m just about done with Sophomore.) It helped my shading a good deal. Also it let me practice my quick-drawing on a regular basis: it was a first period class, and we practiced object drawing almost every day and I often slept through a good 30 of the 50 minutes. 20 minutes is definitely in the quick-draw range.

The sketch I keep talking about wanting to post, is basically the culmination of the quick-draw lessons. I did it for myself, not for the class (well partly to show off, really). But it took a lot longer than 20 min, and it’s also based off a photo, not a live object.

Other than that, I just draw a lot. Especially from reference, because that’s how I learn. The trick is to use the reference in clever new contexts.
