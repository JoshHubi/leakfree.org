I really did like the whole Assassin Brotherhood concept, though. In the previous games, it really felt like it was you against the Templar Order without any help. They made you feel like you were the last member of the Brotherhood. But in this game, they extended the concept to the point where it felt like you were actually leading a huge group, the size of which is breathtaking.

The contract missions were done terrifically, your apprentices had this personal feel to them, and when you called upon their aid it seemed much more fluid.

The Altair missions especially, where as an old man you hardly had to kill anyone because your followers did it for you.

k3nny1550 November 25, 2018, 1:13am #25

I agree- Ezio felt like a leader of men this time around. You really get why he’s so important. In ACII, he was a naive man looking for justice. Over the course of the next two games he gets older, wiser, and becomes a competent leader for the Order. It’s rare to see a character so fleshed out in terms of living through a person’s entire lifespan over successive games, and in that regard Ubisoft took a gamble in thinking that Ezio’s life over three games would remain compelling. Surprisingly, it worked. But yeah, bring on 3.
