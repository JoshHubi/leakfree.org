Open Source Forever:

How come there’s no “On a Rail Uncut” version of this captions fix?

I changed up how the captions fix works by having 2 separate downloads. One download would be the Stock and Surface Tension Uncut versions of the patched maps (which also includes the patched On a Rail Uncut C map if you have that mod installed) and the other would be the captions fix for whatever mods you have installed. This is done so that I don’t have to make an On a Rail Uncut version of the captions fix. You’ll need to download both of them in order for the captions mod to work properly.

Now that I think of it, I could add a patched version of Text’s B1 map to the Stock and Surface Tension Uncut versions of the patched maps so that I can get the captions for the “Shoot him in the face!” scene to show up.

[COLOR=‘Red’]Closed Captions Update v1.29 is now out![/SIZE]

This update includes captions from the B1 map. In order for the captions for that map to work properly, you will need to download one of the versions of the patched maps first and then download the captions fix for what mods you have installed in your BMS folder. After you extract the patched maps to your BMS folder, make sure the graphs folder and the map files (bm_c2a2b1 and bm_c2a2c) are moved to the BMS folder’s maps subfolder (this is for anyone who’s playing On a Rail Uncut). After that, you can extract one of the caption fixes to your BMS folder.

[COLOR=‘Red’]Update: Hotfix for v1.29![/SIZE]

If you already downloaded one of the versions of the patched maps, please redownload it. I realized that I made the sound for the dialogue much louder than it was in the original version of Text’s B1 map. This should be fixed now.

[COLOR=‘Red’]One more thing![/SIZE]

Apparently, the stock version of the captions fix didn’t have the sci_eli_notellingwhatdanger01 reverted back like the other 3 versions. If you’re using the stock version of the captions fix, please redownload it.

A few days ago I discovered a working len:X command, that can be used in subtitle text. It’s necessary if we want the subtitle stay on screen for longer than 30 seconds. Just set the desired duration in seconds (replace X to a number greater than 30, that is). See the code below.

"VO_inbound_tram.tram_06_pleasekeep_wav" "<len:32><I>Please keep your limbs inside the train at all times. Do not attempt to open the doors until the train has come to a complete halt at the station platform. In the event of an emergency, passengers are to remain seated and await further instruction. If it is necessary to exit the train, disabled personnel should be evacuated first. Please stay away from electrified rails and proceed to an emergency station until assistance arrives." "VO_inbound_tram.tram_07_areminder_wav" "<len:36><I>A reminder to all Black Mesa personnel: Regular radiation and biohazard screenings are a requirement of continued employment in the Black Mesa Research Facility. Missing a scheduled urinalysis or radiation check-up is grounds for immediate termination. If you feel you have been exposed to radioactive or other hazardous materials in the course of your duties, contact your radiation safety officer immediately. Work safe. Work smart. Your future depends on it." "VO_inbound_tram.tram_08_doyou_wav" "<len:38><I>Do you have a friend or relative who would make a valuable addition to the Black Mesa team? Immediate openings are available in the areas of Materials Handling and Low Clearance Security. Please contact Black Mesa personnel for further information. If you have an associate with a background in the areas of theoretical physics, biotechnology, or other high tech disciplines, please contact our civilian recruiting team. The Black Mesa Facility is an equal opportunity employer."

  3. Download one of the versions of the Closed Captions. If you have HC and STU installed, click on here. If you have HC and Uplink installed, click on here. If you have all 3 of the mods I’ve mentioned installed, go ahead and click here.[/size]
