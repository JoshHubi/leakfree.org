Duggeek November 24, 2018, 6:52pm #91

Survived the forum crash of '09, but just awoke from the coma.

When I first got my hands on HL1, I was 27, and I played for five hours straight. :jizz: I went to bed at half-past-four in the morning on a Wednesday, and I had to work the next day. I had never played a game that drew me in so much.

That spazzing zombie creeped me out (hairs on the back of my neck and all) and once I got over that, there was a strange satisfaction to jumping into the room and smacking the hell out of it.

The houndeyes encounter right after that was more tense, because they moved so fast. I wanted to move ahead so badly, that I didn’t see how to get the scientist to open the armory until I started over.

One word: bullsquids
Two words: charging bullsquids
Three words: charging, spitting bullsquids
They freaked my sh!t. :fffuuu:

The tentacles in Blast Pit only worried me a bit. Watching Barney get gibbed was an effective warning; don’t get too close and don’t make too much noise. After all, they were seen in just about every demo/trailer/promo that came out that year.

Head-humpers and ducts/pipes… always a startling combination. Of course, now we’ve come to expect it.

The first encounter with an Alien Grunt was probably the most tense moment in the game. The room is eerily lit by the glow of the specimen chamber, and while the Grunt seems to be trapped… not for long! :fffuuu:

However, there’s one moment in HL1 that frightened me to the point of, “that’s it, I’m gonna die, there’s no way to get past this.” That moment was the Garg in Forget About Freeman. If you enter the parking garage right as the script starts, you’ll attract the attention of two soldiers, but they are promptly flattened by an SUV… because a Gargantua nudged it… ten feet. :zip:

And then it sees you…

The very first time (yah, I died) I was fascinated and paralyzed by it… (maybe it’s friendly? :retard: ) then it spat those bio-incendiary claws at me and- (bee-ee-ee-ee-ee…) :’(

Next time, I ran like hell and found how to survive my way into the Lambda Complex. :freeman:

Love that game, and I still re-play it to this day. Even the “source” edition. (oooh… shimmery water!)

If the BM team can pull it off, I’d love to experience some of those feelings again. Especially now that I know what to expect from HL1. Shiny? Definitely… but does it “take me there” in the same way HL1 did, and that was the blocky GoldSrc era!

Make me jump, guys.

Release Black Mesa!
