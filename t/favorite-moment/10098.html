Brent November 25, 2018, 2:21am #11

I can name so many favorite parts of the game.

The tram ride was exceptionally cool, but I was even more amazed at the research facility! For the first time in a long time, I really felt like I was a part of the game world I was in and had genuine interest in the characters on screen. I went around to every single NPC I could fine and ravaged every bit of dialog out of them that I could. Valve definitely pioneered in you being able to interact in a world to the level of believably that they did, but Black Mesa wins in making you truly want to explore that world.

But the Resonance Cascade was even better! I did not expect it to be so frantic and terrifying. For the first time, the Resonance Cascade was actually terrifying, and watching it take place put me in a state of awe I haven’t felt in such a long time.

Seeing the aftermath was absolutely incredible, and I truly felt alone and helpless playing these parts. Watching that headcrab zombie pick up the security guard and strangle him to death was one of the coolest and most memorable things I’ve ever seen in a game.

I could go on forever, but to keep my ramblings short, I really loved the Xen labs in Questionable Ethics. That level was always a good level for me to simply blow aliens and soldiers away, but with all the subtle tweaks made to it, that level came alive in such an engrossing way. But out of all of the additions, the recreated Xen habitat was so intriguing.

And the second time you fight the gargantua too. That was so fucking cool.
