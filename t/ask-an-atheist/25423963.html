There has always been a lot of confusion and debate over what it really means to be agnostic. However, I think more people are leaning toward this definition:
One who believes that it is impossible to know whether there is a God.

So then agnosticism and gnosticism answer a completely different question than theist and atheist. You can be an agnostic theist. In fact I would bet at least 80% of all theists are also agnostic.

These correspond to the two questions:
Do you think it is possible to KNOW without a doubt that a god(s) exist or not?
If yes, you are gnostic (and also an idiot). If no, then you are agnostic.

and the other question is:
Do you BELIEVE that a god or gods exist?
so in short:
(agnosticism/gnosticism) - answers what we can KNOW.
(theism/atheism) - answers what a person BELIEVES.

I myself am an agnostic atheist. I know that it is impossible for us to prove whether or not a supernatural being exists. BUT, I believe that there is no god(s).
two completely different things.

and an agnostic theist for example would be someone who does believe in god, but admits that it is impossible to know for sure.

the Gnostic theists are the scary ones that you have to watch out for
