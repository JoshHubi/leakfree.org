Agnosticism and atheism talk about two different things. Agnosticism is not some point between atheism and theism. If you don’t know if a god exists but you believe there might be, you’re an agnostic theist. If you don’t know if a god exists and you don’t believe there to be one, you’re an agnostic atheist. If you know that a god exists and you believe that one exists, then you’re a gnostic theist. If you know that a god exists but you don’t believe one exists (not sure how this works but let’s go with it), you’re a gnostic atheist.

Gnosticism/Agnosticism deals with knowledge.
Theism/Atheism deals with belief.

You either know or you don’t. You either believe or you don’t.

If you don’t care either way, you are still either believing or not, knowing or not, but some like to use the term apatheist or apagnostic.

(In the interests of full-disclosure, I repeat what I said earlier in this thread: I am an agnostic atheist. I don’t know and I don’t believe.)
