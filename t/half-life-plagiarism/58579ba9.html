nietzschesaurus August 11, 2020, 5:56am #57

Dear god, this thread is hurting my brain. Alright guys, look. It’s a science fiction story. There are idea you can use that are free game. space travel, aliens, teleportation, government conspiracies, strange fucked-up tentacle monsters, scientists that get slaughtered en masse, strange weaponry, anti-gravity machinery, super-powered techno suits, plasma guns, otherworldly godlike beings. These things are all typical sci-fi fare, and they’re all in Half-Life. Original ideas typically don’t come out of the blue; they’re ideas built upon other ideas. You would know this if you’ve ever come up with something of worth.

The headcrab was invented by Ted Backman, who has admitted “his inspirations for the Half-Life creatures was H.R. Giger’s Necronomicon paintings and its sexual innuendos” (sauce). That doesn’t mean he COPIED!!!111!1~1!3243~! H.R. Giger. If you’ll notice, the headcrab and the facehugger have two things in common: jumping and claws. That’s it. This man did not look at the facehugger and say “LET’S USE THAT BUT MAKE IT DIFFERENT BSRLKJDASDF!” That is retarded. He is an extremely talented artist and it’s an insult to artists everywhere to assume that simply because something has a vague, superficial resemblance to something else that it must be a direct rip-off. This is something that annoys me greatly about the internet.

Half-Life is one of the most original games in decades. These guys busted their asses creating a game that was unlike any other game at the time, in both gameplay/mechanics and storyline. Of course they were inspired by other works. Of course they paid tribute to other things. This is not wrong. Nor is it illegal. Is the Madonna and Child any less a work of art simply because it’s a painting of the virgin Mary? OBVIOUSLY MICHAELANGELO WAS RIPPING OFF DA VINCI’S THE VIRGIN IN THE ROCKS DURRRR.

I don’t mean to offend anyone, and this isn’t a direct attack on anyone in particular. I just get rather zealous about things sometimes. You understand.
