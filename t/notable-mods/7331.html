geekofalltrades November 25, 2018, 12:28am #16

^ Then you’re an idiot.

To actually answer OP’s question: if you bought Half-Life: Source, then you won’t be able to play most of the mods listed so far, because they’re for the GoldSource engine, which ran the original Half-Life. Half-Life: Source, by contrast, runs on the… Source engine.

If Half_Life Source comes with the Source SDK and the SDK Base 2007, then it’s possible that you’d still be able to play source mods with it. In which case, probably the best of the bunch is MINERVA by Adam Foster. It was so good that he now officially works for Valve. Also check out Mission Improbable. Do NOT play Combine Destiny, no matter how many fanboys rave at you about it.

In years past, I would have been able to list many, many more mods, but it’s been a while since I’ve had a ton of time to play games.

EDIT: Human Error for Half-Life 2 was pretty good as well, and had a ton of original content. The Citizen (Half-Life 2) has kind of a neat “homebrewed” feel to it, although I didn’t personally think that The Citizen 2 was as good as the first one. Eye of the Storm (Half-Life 2): pretty, but unfortunately nothing revolutionary. Hmm, see if I can’t remember a few more…

Don’t know how I was forgetting this, but for Half-Life 2, Dear Esther. A THOUSAND TIMES DEAR ESTHER. It’s an art mod, so don’t go in expecting action, but it’s absolutely fantastic; the first time I’ve ever thought of a videogame as art.

Also, check out planetphillip.com for all the Half-Life and Half-Life 2 mods you could ever want.
