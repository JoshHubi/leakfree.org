Prism123 November 25, 2018, 1:28am #209

That’s the thing, we’ve already tried all of this, and it got us nowhere. We tried doing frequent news updates; people complained. We tried doing large new updates; people complained. We tried setting up a devblog; people complained. We tried releasing video trailers; people complained. We tried setting up a YouTube channel; people complained. We tried setting up a Twitter account; people complained. Do you see where this is going?

No matter what we do, people are going to complain, so it makes the most sense to do nothing. We can’t keep everyone happy, and it’s a waste of development time to even try, especially at this late stage. We have more important things to do.

The people who complained that there wasn’t enough media or that the Twitter/YouTube stuff wasn’t of a high enough standard/interesting enough were a lot more vocal than the people who thought it was enough. We can only act on the feedback we receive though, and here we are today.

Now obviously the developers can do whatever they want and we’re not entitled to any information, but this reasoning kinda strikes me as understandable given the circumstances but somewhat flawed. For one thing people upset about stuff are obviously more likely to voice their opinions, so its no surprise that the loudest voices will be people complaining. Pretty much every corner of the internet has trolls. And while it is certainly impossible to please everyone so it would be ridiculous to try, I would guess that an occasional status update or twitter post would have pleased the majority of fans, where as no updates period pleases no one. Now I’ve never had to deal with rabid internet crazies criticizing my hard work and reacting against that by shutting up completely is certainly a natural reaction, but simply ignoring the haters might have been better than punishing everyone for the actions of a couple idiots.
