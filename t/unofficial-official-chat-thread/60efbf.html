Soup November 24, 2018, 6:32pm #3445

My last dream was awesome enough that I rushed to write it down before it dribbled out of my mind-parts:

The first part I don’t remember, but I think it was one of my few nerd dreams and was something to do with OAR…

Anyway, it really got going when I found out that my brother, my father and I (also a rare occurrence for me to dream about family) were part of a space flight programme ran by none other than Terry Pratchett. It was all in the backgarden of his house, which was on a road in my hometown: the rocket was inside his shed which lifted up Thunderbirds style - I don’t know why the rocket wasn’t going straight up, but there we go.

Sadly, since the hydraulics lifting the shed were solar powered, when the clouds blocked out the sun, the shed stopped mid rise. Bummer. To avoid arousing any suspicion (this was a secret space programme, obviously) I jumped up and pulled the shed back down.

Our space programme jeopardised, we travelled back to the family house, and my Granny joined us. On the way back, she casually pointed out a bear on the roundabout near our house. However, as luck would have it, the sun came out again! Huzzah!

So, back to Pratchett’s and inside the rocket! For some reason that probably made perfect sense, the spaceship looked exactly like my living room inside. Fair dues.

Then, the best part of any dream I’ve ever had happened: I actually felt weightlessness. You know how in some dreams you can feel textures and stuff? I felt zero g: I felt my blood rush to my head slightly as it stopped having to fight gravity, felt my stomach contents drift around (weird, but not really unpleasant) and lost all sense of up or down. It was friggin’ badass.

From there on it got a bit hazy: there was something to do with a woman who was a robot trying to blow up Pratchett’s house and almost succeeding.
