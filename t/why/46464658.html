Unlike in Half-Life 2; Half Life 1 had at least several references to radiation, isotopes or nuclear energy

  • The game took place in Black Mesa, a missile testing facility during the Cold War Era. No doubt the testing range also conducted nuclear weapons test
  • Radiation Hazards was always present in at least a part of each chapter
  • Radioactive Isotopes were used as ammo for two of the experimental weapons you could pick up
  • Nuclear Reactors were present in 2-3 levels

Plus you could also say that the term was also applied to the character Gordon Freeman. Afterall Valve played around with using the term Half-Dead, but they figured Half-Life would have add some form of Science aspect to the game
