gordonfreemantshepha November 25, 2018, 2:24am #350

So, I finally finished Black Mesa and despite the some minor bugs and performance issues, this is the mod we all dreamed of and waited for. The dark and brooding tone conjures up a new perspective on how the game can be viewed, the stellar soundtrack punctuates this, the level design is fantastic and the gameplay is distinctly Half-Life in all it’s glory. Which brings me to my first point, the gameplay is cuddling hard as hell and it’s perfect that it is. The developers seem to have a great understanding of the fact that Half-Life was very much a product of it’s time in that it is reflective of a time when shooters didn’t hold you by the crab claw the whole way through, you make do with what you have, or you die. And you will definitely die a lot in this game, but each time you succeed you will have the biggest grin on your face much like every time you played the original. Every firefight with the HECU soldiers in the game is gruelling but rewarding, and our favorite alien adversaries are back and in top form. The vortigaunts pack a punch with each electrical charge taking away much more health than before, the houndeyes are significantly more challenging as when they are in packs you pretty much have to run like hell and shoot fast because you’ll need a great amount of health packs after dealing with them. The headcrab zombies are more intimidating than ever as the astonishing level of detail put into them will make you simultaneously shit yourself and splooge in your pants in absolute awe.

The voice acting is stellar too, bringing about a sense of life in this game’s world more so than how the original did. There’s also thousands of lines of new dialogue too, more so than even Half-Life 2’s amount of dialogue, a welcome treat for anyone who’s played the original before. It really build upon the immersion in a fantastic way. The level design is at the same time fresh and familiar, Black Mesa manages to find a great balance between nostalgia and discovery. As I said before, the soundtrack brings about a new tone which makes the game’s story feel a bit darker, the sense of dread when you know the horrors of what’s to become of the black mesa facility and the world beyond until the events of Half-Life 2. This tone completely works in the context of a remake and is absolutely excellent to listen to as the events unfold. If there’s anything I can complain about, it’s the fact that the game leaves off at possibly the most exciting point, when you literally jump into the portal to Xen, and with everything in Black Mesa being so excellent that ending to be continued in the credits makes the wait for the standalone Xen release that much harder to bare. It brings about a sense of excitement while at the same time Xen is the portion of the game that is very divisive among the Half-Life fanbase. It’ll be interesting to see if they’re able to make Xen unanimously agreed upon as excellent. Another complaint that I have is performance, during a few of the early parts of the game it was rather stuttery, not game breaking but noticeable, however in Blast Pit my frame-rate took a nosedive. That could just be my individual experience however, since a lot of people have had no issues in that regard. I’m sure any issues will more than likely be patched within the coming weeks.

Overall. Black Mesa is a triumph, after eight years of development it is exactly the type of remake that we wanted it to be, all the little details bring this world alive in a way that is only possible in 2012. This is the quintessential version of Half-Life. Long live the Freeman.

10/10.
