Level August 11, 2020, 2:06am #8

Sigh.

Okay, go to this page: https://www.newegg.com/Store/Category.aspx?Category=20&name=Motherboards
See the intel on the left side, click on that, and it’ll give you a list of all the potential intel motherboards. Now scroll down until you see “CPU Socket Type” on the side. Find out the socket type of your processor (if you’re keeping your old one), and make sure the sockets match. If you don’t, your processor won’t fit in the system.

Also, if you haven’t read a guide on building a computer before, do that ASAP. Buying and building computers is easy, but you have to understand the ground rules for it, what works, what doesn’t, and why.

You said you were replacing your processor in your first post, but only posted a link to a motherboard, not the CPU.
