There is a connection, it’s just old and not, well, used very much anymore. Husband literally means something like “manager” or “master of the house,” (and, as a verb, “management”) the -ry suffix means the action/profession of such (like carpentry). In terms of an agricultural society (which English society was) this could mean banking or finances (and, indeed, husbandry can be used like this, but you probably won’t hear it often), but most often would mean taking care of the farm. Husbandry generally means anything relating to the keeping of animals (producing the grain for them and taking care of them) but over years of linguistic drift we get something along the lines of:

Management -> Farm Management -> Farm Management esp w/ regard to animals -> Farm Management esp w/ regards to the breeding of animals.

So you were correct in relating the two words, the time period you related the words in was just off by a few hundred years or so.
