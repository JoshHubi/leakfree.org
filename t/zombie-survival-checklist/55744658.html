BaTz281 November 24, 2018, 10:38pm #31

Realistically (and by that I mean, under the assumption that an outbreak is possible) my biggest concern is going to be food/water. You can only stock so much in one place, and even if you’re on a farm and have animals and a large storage stocked up, there’s still a time limit.

The biggest problem is time - take, for example, Resident Evil: Extinction. In that movie, folks are down to raiding old convenience stores for canned goods, and the liquid contained therein doubles as your liquid intake. Since most of the world would conceivably be focused on straight up survival, the amount of production would decrease, and nobody would be restocking the Twinkies at your local 7-11. And even if you had a water filtration system, if shit REALLY hits the fan and electricity ceases to run, there’s no water getting pumped to your house and thus nothing to purify anyway. So you’d be down to recycling urine/rainwater/nearby creeks, if applicable.

That all said, you should also consider a first aid kid, various medications, some form of entertainment (think the mp3 player from “Book of Eli”) since there will be some periods of downtime in which you must maintain your sanity. Speaking of sanity, there’s sanitary - some way of cleaning yourself (alcohol wipes, for example).
