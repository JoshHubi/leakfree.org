Floyd November 24, 2018, 6:49pm #2028

  • Pre-placed bullet holes in some of the later maps where there has clearly already been fighting.

  • Signs of both alien and human casualties. In both HL and HL2 half-decomposed corpses were ubiquitous and dull, and were only ever scientists or citizens. Soldiers and aliens would be a good inclusion.

Basically, it felt extremely unrealistic to walk through a pristine battlefield, even though there might be a few destroyed buildings here and some rubble there. Placing bullet holes along with gibs, corpses, a human grunt trying to crawl away (for you to choose whether or not to finish off) makes the whole area feel far more alive. You’d be smoothing out the transition between obvious level design and real life.

I don’t know how well this would fly with the minimal performance demands, but keep as much detail from firefights as possible. Draw the line at shells or even gibs, but blood-splatter, bodies and bullet holes remaining make it more detailed. I realise that Half-Life is all about a linear journey and you almost never backtrack, but on the odd occasion where you do - it’s an amazing immersion tool.

THRRRPT November 24, 2018, 6:49pm #2029

How about the HECU using proper radio discipline and jargon?

And on that note, MORE GRUNT RADIO CHATTER. It’d really work well with the atmosphere, especially in military-heavy areas such as On a Rail, Power Up, and We’ve Got Hostiles. Naturally becoming more and more panicked, as things start going wrong.

For examples, think of some of the chatter in Prototype for the sort of thing I’m talking about.

Such as, just to throw out another example, say you’ve just wiped out a squad of grunts and are progressing through the area, coming up on another group, you start hearing snippets of their conversation.

“Stiletto 2, Stiletto Actual. Contact lost with Stiletto 4 in sector two-alpha. How copy, over?”
“Solid copy, Stiletto Actual, proceeding to check the area. Out.”

Then of course, the second fireteam shows up/is ambushed by the player, and you have another fight on your hands.

Also, it might be worth it for the grunts to start radioing in contact with you to their superiors in addition to the standard. “SQUAD! GET FREEMAN!” that we’re all so familiar with. Just stuff like “Freeman engaged in sector yadda yadda.” Or “Confirmed contact! Open fire!”

It might also be interesting to hear acknowledgments over the radio when you’re calling down airstrikes/artillery in Surface Tension.
