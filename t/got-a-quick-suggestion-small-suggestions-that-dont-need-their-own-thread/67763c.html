Brother Shrike:

Time to start bitching. It’s not a silenced MP5, it’s a suppressed MP5. The correct term is suppressor, as silencers don’t actually exist. And the classic kinda smoothed, softened “silenced” sound (a la Counter-Strike) doesn’t happen, a suppressed gun still sounds quite staccato.

The term silencer and supressor are interchangable, although supressor is the correct term.

They work by muffling the sound of the expanding gases from a gun, thus lowered the noise and greatly reducing the muzzle flash. Howver, they do nothing to stop the “crack” of a bullet as it breaks the sound barrier. If using ammunition that travels less than that of the speed of sound it is possible to have a very quiet gun. MP5s with intergrated supressors and subsonic ammuntion give off no noise other than the bolt clicking back and forth.

HL1 used an MP5 with a supressor on it, but the actual firing sound isn’t silenced at all. Maybe Valve thought having one of the main game weapons silenced would be kind of dull.
