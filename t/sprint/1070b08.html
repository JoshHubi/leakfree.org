Flatline August 11, 2020, 4:50am #172
Dias:

In Half-Life 2, the players hull height is 72 units tall(& so is barney) because all of the human characters use a scale of 1 foot as being 12 units, or 1 unit equals 1 inch. (As opposed to the maps & props which use a scale of 1 foot = 16 units.)

From what I can find on the valve wiki, speed is given in map-units(i.e. inches) per second. To convert that to miles per hour, just multiply by about 0.056818. The player movement speeds according to the dev console (& cl_showpos for crawling speed) are:

HL2_normalspeed 190 (10.795455 mph)
HL2_sprintspeed 320 (18.181818 mph)
HL2_walkspeed 150 (8.522727 mph)
crawl-speed 63.33 (3.76875 mph)

There. That’s how I figured it out. I don’t need some “in-game-speedometer” to figure out what’s going on. In fact, it took me longer to explain than it did to figure out. Start up a treadmill, see if you can walk 8.5 miles an hour for more than a minute (without a HEV suit)

Or you could read this: https://developer.valvesoftware.com/wiki/Dimensions#Speed

Which is official and slightly different.
