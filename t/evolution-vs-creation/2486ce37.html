YARO:

I don’t like the idea that everyone who has worse/better brain capibilities is either sick or distorted. Scientists say our brain can contain a lot of informations (and I mean A LOT). Then why do we forget all we learned over the years (not when you’re getting old and your neurons are degenerating/dying, more like a few years after graduating or, in many cases, a few days after a test ;P).

It’s how the brain works. There are connections between neurons called synapses. When a signal comes through a synapse a threshold required to stimulate it decreases, so the next time the signal could be weaker to stimulate the cell. In time this threshold increases, so a signal must come through it from time to time to keep this connection “alive”. A large combinations of those connections form our memories. That’s why we forget things when we don’t refresh our memory.
