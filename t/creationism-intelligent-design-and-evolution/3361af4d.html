Just like to get the free radical business out of the way.

Free radicals are molecules or compounds which naturally have an odd number of valence electrons. This means that the molecule searches violently to get just one more electron to fill its outer shell. This means it is highly reactive with…almost anything. It can combine with another molecule, a metal, a single element, etc.

Therefore, if introduced into the body, they do have a high chance of bonding to other molecules, such as DNA.
