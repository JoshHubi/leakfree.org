Env_projectedtexture can only be used 1 at a time, IIRC.

Also Hezus, the effect he means IS actually block light. You lay the tool texture out in a grid, and bump up the lightmap scale in the area around it. It’s used in a few places in Black Mesa too, it’s probably better for that lighting effect than env_projectedtexture though it’s a bit more work. You could also use texturedshadows in lights.rad, but making a blocklight grid is probably just as simple and more flexible.

He is talking about using the block light texture to artificially force VRAD to bake lightmap shadows onto the walls, which you did not do. The examples that he gave all use this technique, in which the mapper used block light brushes in the shape of a grating to bake a high-quality shadow onto the walls.

Env_projectedtexture is a dynamic light with many flaws. You can only have one of them active at any given moment, and the resulting shadows are hideously blurry.
