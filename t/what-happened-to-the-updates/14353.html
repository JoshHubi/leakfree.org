TextFAMGUY1 November 25, 2018, 4:37am #4

Our next focus for updates is Content Update 2, which will be even larger than the first major one, and we are aiming to deploy it within a couple of months. I can’t give an exact timeframe on it yet as it is dependent on how quickly some of the required work gets done and how smoothly the new hires turn out.

Though I can’t be too specific, our plans for future updates - not necessarily all in the next one though - (prior to Xen), include - more new weapon models, bug fixes (of course!), more balance changes, new MP maps (Crossfire I can confirm), new gamemodes for MP, many MP gameplay changes and refinements, new graphical features, updates to singleplayer (Surface Tension is planned but not completely certain yet), menu and UI overhaul, workshop updates, and that’s just what I can remember off the top of my head.

There may be a small patch or two in between now and then but I don’t believe we have one officially planned. Our development is very fluid and dynamic. Development is extremely time consuming, guys.

One of the future updates will come with a complete 3rd person animation overhaul for MP. We currently use hacked/rerigged HL2 animations from Valve which have a lot of problems. Our animators have been working on creating an entirely custom movement/weapon set of animations, I believe they are quite far along. I think there are some cool features planned like proper jump animations (with a looping mid air animation like TF2), long jump animation, swim animations, etc.

MysticJ:

I still think it’s a bad decision to not test Xen with Early Access audience. The Earthly portion of the game benefited greatly from player feedback; without it, it would be mediocre. And if you don’t test Xen with us, then there’s a great risk it will be mediocre as well.

I don’t think many would argue the earthbound portion of the game turned out mediocre in the 2012 mod release, which hadn’t been publicly tested. Xen will not be mediocre. We know what we’re doing. We also have a pretty large internal testing team, anyway. It’s precisely because we know what we’re doing that it’s taking so long, because these things take a lot of time to get right.
