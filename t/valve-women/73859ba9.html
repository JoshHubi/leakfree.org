I found Alyx to be a phenomenally well developed character. A big reason of why Episode One was so great is that she was by the player’s side almost the whole time and never felt out of place or artificial. She was there by Gordon’s side, she shot things accurately, etc. like a good AI should, but what really gets her to stand out is how her character develops over the course of the three segments of the series so far. Half Life 2’s focus is more on developing the world, the universe, what happened after the Combine hit. Alyx didn’t get very much screen time compared to her role in the Episodes, but she set a good tone for the universe. She was there to discover things with the player, point out stuff in the environment to further enhance the setting, and dish out snark in little tidbits. I got really invested in the character- I felt like I could relate to her as a person rather than as just another NPC in the game.

Zoey in L4D1 is a fun character- She’s a strong lead like Alyx but tends to enjoy herself more, with much funnier dialog. (“I call zombie bullshit!”)

Rochelle I haven’t logged enough hours in L4D2 to comment on her one way or the other, but I seem to recall Jessica Cannon (Sin Episodes: Emergence, not by Valve but on Source regardless) being a better player companion than her.

Chell is just the player’s avatar, like Gordon. So we can’t really comment on her character because she has no characterization.
