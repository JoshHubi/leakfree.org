Jeannotvb November 24, 2018, 11:26pm #66
CatzEyes93:

now it seems they are thinking they were more scavengers rather than hunters.

Most scientists agree that T. rex was both a scavenger and a predator.
There is evidence to suggest T. rex scavenged, but there’s also evidence in the form of healed T. rex bitemarks on Triceratops and Hadrosaur fossils that suggest it was a predator.

Also don’t forget that T. rex had forward facing eyes (binocular vision), this is not common among dinosaurs and usually seen in predators. If T. rex was a scavenger then surely it wouldn’t have evolved binocular vision.
The idea of it being a 100% scavenger is ridiculous anyway, there’s only one animal that does that today which is a Vulture.

Those who use the “its arms were too small” argument don’t understand how evolution works. T. rex survived for more than 2 million years, so clearly it didn’t need arms. It had one of the most powerful jaws in the history of life, just like sharks and crocodiles it used brute force to kill prey without ever needing hands (not to mention it was the largest predator at that time and its prey were all big slow herbivores).
