speaking from experience, changing thermal paste works a hell of a lot better, and you will get an opportunity to clean out the dust that might have accumulated on the copper heatsink of your laptop

if you don’t feel comfortable opening the laptop because it’s still under warranty, call Dell and tell them that you need their permission to change the thermal paste, if you tell them you are a pro at this they won’t void your warranty

after changing the thermal paste on my laptop the turbo boost went from 1.6Ghz to 2.8Ghz and the GPU was crashing at stock 500Mhz now it runs 24/7 stable at 700Mhz B)
