Malidictus November 25, 2018, 4:22am #886

There are a lot of instances in the game where people just refuse to interact with you, sometimes in really strange ways. You know they “accept” your use button since their heads turn to look at you momentarily, but they don’t say anything. The original Half-Life had filler lines for this, hence the “Catch me later. I’ll buy you a beer.” line that made it all the way to Half-Life 2. Example - the lady in the cold storage area, which I saved from headcrab zombies. She ran into a corner and then just… Stood there. I tried speaking with her and she’d just look at me, but wouldn’t talk or do anything. OK, lady. If you don’t want armed guy in power armour to help you, you get yourself to safety.

Granted, the same was true of Half-Life, but Black Mesa generally does things a lot better. Previously, NPCs would follow you until you reached an obstacle they couldn’t navigate and they’d simply stop. Black Mesa actually has scripted events where NPCs will stop following you and assume specific positions, usually giving you verbal reasons why they can’t go on. It makes the game feel far more immersive when the guy says “Wow! I can’t make that jump!” vs. when you just have to leave him with nary a word. Trouble is that those sections are so good, it makes sections where this isn’t accounted for feel… Awkward. Hey, I just saved a scientist from a soldier. Can I speak with him? “I do believe he was trying to kill me!” Good on you, Dr. WhatsYourName, but do you want to come with me? Maybe hide somewhere? Do you have a reason why you don’t want to ride the elevator up? He just stands there looking at me. OK… Good luck with that.

Finally, a request: Can we get the “move order” command from Half-Life 2? You know, the one which causes followers to stop following you and go to a specific location for a while? I replayed Half-Life 2 recently, and found this incredibly useful for getting my companions out of trouble, so they don’t get killed. For instance, I knew there were going to be many enemies incoming, so I ordered them inside a building while I fought the Combine soldiers off, thus saving their lives. Or is that too big of a departure from Half-Life?

As far as multiplayer game modes go, I’d honestly like to see a sort of DOTA-like NPC-based mode. Have you guys played the old Future Cop: LAPD game? That one gave you a base and some limited resources with which to buy tanks/helicopters to slowly move towards the enemy, as well as turrets which could be captured along the map. Indeed, Future Cop predates DOTA as far as I’m aware, and it’s been copied in games like Super Monday Night Combat. The basic flow of the game would run along the lines of…

Remember the defence of White Forest from Half-Life 2: Episode 2’s chapter “Our Mutual Friend?” I’d imagine a map kind of like that, with a bunch of non-linear paths going from one starting location to another with supply caches/spawn points along the way. One team would play as “Black Mesa” and the other as early Xen alien collaborators (to explain why everyone has human weapons), so one team could spawn soldiers and tanks while the other could spawn the various Xenians, with a Gargantua in place of a tank.

The map, indeed maps, don’t have to be an open field, either. You could also do this along enclosed corridors deep inside Black Mesa. You can even borrow a page from UT2004 by having each control point be linked along a grid such that you need to have a “route” to it, avoiding the Battlefield problem of one solitary douchebag capping a point deep behind your lines.

I know this is outlandish and probably not going to happen, but it’s really the only game mode I can think of that’s not basically a rename of a Quake 3/Unreal Tournament game mode.
