I for one would advocate Half Life 2: Episode One as the best paced of the Half Life games. It always threw something new at you in very quick succession. Underground, directing Alyx’s fire with a flashlight. Fixing fuse boxes yourself in seconds which originally would have required Alyx to do it and would have bogged down the game’s pace. The classic elevator standoff with a swarm of zombies, destined to be repeated ad infinitum by Left 4 Dead and other following shooters. Combine door breaches. A room full of laser tripmines. Blasting a gunship THROUGH a large roof atrium. A epic fight through a hospital with some of the world’s most badass music. A multi stage faceoff against rappeling combine, snipers, and other hazards while escorting citizens to a train. Fighting a massive strider inside a railroad building. And the whole city blowing up at the end.

And that’s all the stuff from off the top of my head. Episode Two was also very good, with the added bonus of narrative punch and a non-linear penultimate fight against a veritable company of striders and hunters.
