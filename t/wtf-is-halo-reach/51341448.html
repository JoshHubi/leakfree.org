I found racing in the forge maps to be rather frustrating, actually. I liked Reach’s multiplayer because it was better balanced. Namely, the arsenal was varied and more creative. Besides, I prefer engagements in the game to be from extreme long range with the game’s sniper rifles. Sprint or Jetpack let me get to good perches quickly or make an escape when I’m being flanked.

In Halo 3 by contrast, I’d get stabbed point blank with an energy sword before I even got halfway through my first magazine. It seemed less strategic and more like senseless slaughter.

I think I mentioned earlier that ODST was the best game in the series by far due to the single player campaign’s massive improvement in story and gameplay over Halo 3. Nonlinearity was the soul of the game’s hub-world, and the variance in gameplay styles is indeed a particular strength of the game.

However, I find Half Life 2 to be a much better game. The shooter gameplay keeps you on your toes. The arsenal, while not as large as Halo’s, is better balanced and versatile. But the most important part of my Halo critique was that the story simply wasn’t as emotionally engaging. Why did I find HL2 Episode 2 to be so much better than ODST, despite the gameplay style of ODST to be great in a different way? Because I reacted emotionally. The end of Episode 2 almost reduced me to tears. I couldn’t believe what I was seeing. Few games manage to do that, one of which was The Darkness (FPS by Starbreeze, pick it up in the local bargain bin, the single player is a crime epic). Even FFXIII got more emotional reaction out of me, and the pace of the gameplay is MUCH slower. (Compare- semi-nonlinear FPS to strategic turn based combat where you must swap your tactics more than your actual character moves)

Also, for the record, the Half Life 2 original Xbox port was not a bad game by any means. It was actually rather impressive that they crammed the game onto the system with little sacrifice in the gameplay department. Occasional framerate dips were annoying, but the game itself was rather pleasant to control, particularly during the driving sequences.
