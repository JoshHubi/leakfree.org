Sitten_duck November 24, 2018, 8:31pm #166

When this thread was first started, I was trying to find a photoshop creation that I had made to post on here, but lost the file somewhere and couldn’t post it when this thread was alive. I found it recently and if I can find it again I’ll post it up here. I’m kinda glad that this thread was resurrected so I can finally post it. The only reason I am not posting it now is because the only copy I have of it is on a disc back at school, and I am currently at home for spring break. I wont be back at school to post it until Sunday.

The picture was a part of a project that my friend and I were going to do toward the end of my senior year in highschool last year. I had just gotten my hands on Photoshop, most of the other adobe programs and a DSLR with a good range of lenses through a photojournalism class I took at school. I really wanted to see what I could do with it all. At the time a good friend of mine was into graphic novels and anything zombie related. During a moment of inspiration I proposed we make a graphic novel using the DSLR to take pictures, photoshop to edit them and other adobe programs to create the graphic novel layouts and make a pdf copy. I don’t quite care about zombies or graphic novels; I just wanted to see if I could do it. I took a test shot to see if I could manipulate it right, and we were quite pleased with the results. The picture that I will post I showed to several people and got them amped up and willing to volunteer for the project. When I told my photojournalism teacher about my plans and showed her the image I made, she offered to suspend all my other assignments for the rest of the year to let me work on it as long as I brought her whatever I made on a regular basis. Sadly, we started the project late in the year and classes got in the way and nothing became of it. I hold the picture to be some of my best photoshop work. It probably won’t be much to seasoned photoshop users, and I make no claim that it is something really special, but I am curious to see what others think of it. I haven’t been able to use photoshop much since I graduated from highschool, so its a pretty good representation of where I am in skill level in photoshop. I’d like to hear what more experienced users think of it and critiques in general.
