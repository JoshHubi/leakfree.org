Orpheon November 25, 2018, 4:12am #4

Over on Tom’s Hardware they have a lot of great guides for building computers, and how to get the best for your money. Also, I’ve personally never gone wrong trusting the reviews on newegg. Just go with anything rated 4 or 5 eggs, and if you really want to be sure, read at least the most helpful reviews to find out anything else you might need to know about the part before you buy it.

What I’d suggest:

CPU: Intel Core i5-4690K

Motherboard: The brands I trust the most are Gigabyte, ASUS, and ASRock. Just make sure it’s reviewed well and has everything you’ll need - if you’re gonna do Crossfire or SLI make sure it’s got support for that, make sure it’s got enough SATA and USB connections, etc.

GPU: I don’t know if you have any particular brand loyalty, but the best bang for your buck right seems to be AMD cards if you’re gonna go single card - try an R9 280X or an R9 290. If you’re gonna SLI/Crossfire, check out how the games you want to play do with multi-GPU setups. Not all games benefit all that much from SLI/Crossfire AFAIK.

Power supply: Corsair, Antec, and Cooler Master are good brands, not too sure about anything else. Again, newegg reviews can help here. Additionally, make sure the wattage is high enough to handle all your components, that you have enough amps on the 12v rails (in order to power your GPU), and that you have enough connectors to power all your devices.

Storage: I’ve gone with Western Digital, Samsung, and Seagate. All drives seem to perform well. I’ve had the most failures with Seagate, but most of the drives I work with are Seagate, so that may not mean anything. Also consider an SSD for your OS and games. It really makes a huge difference in boot and load times. Good brands are OCZ, Intel, Samsung, ADATA, Crucial, Sandisk.

RAM: Get at least 8GB, and make sure you get the right speed for your motherboard. G.Skill, Crucial, Mushkin, and Corsair are good brands, even ADATA or Kingston might do.

Monitors: ASUS and Acer make good monitors. You might notice some light bleed on some of the cheaper ones though.

Keyboard/Mouse: I’m a Logitech fanboy. The G9/G9x is still my favorite mouse ever.

Hopefully some of that helps.
