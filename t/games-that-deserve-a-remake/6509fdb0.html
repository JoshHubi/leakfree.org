Someonerandm:

What’s your point?

My point was that movies never stop being watchable, you just copy the from storage device they were on to a newer one. Games can be unplayable, because of changes in hardware & software, sometimes porting a game without emulation (which could cause legal trouble) & could be less cost-effective than recreating the game from scratch. At that point, making it with current generation sound/graphic/physics standards can be more profitable than keeping it the same as the original. Even enough to make a real sequel.

I’m not saying that I think there aren’t enough remakes out there or anything. It’s just that many people who seem revolted by the idea of remakes are only really thinking of games from the past five or so years. This is about what games deserve a remake. Fan-made or otherwise.
