[color=palegreen]Canadian GP: McLaren One-Two in Montreal Thriller as Red Bull strategy fails[/SIZE]

In today’s 70 laps of many overtakes, Brittish chances looked bland as they started on already worn out softer tyres (and that is in Montreal where tyres get killed pretty fucking fast). They were beaten by Red Bulls as latter were on harder compound: Vettel overtook Hamilton, Webber (who, despite gearbox penalty, had a great start and made it up to 5th) - Button, which led both brits to stop for harder compound. And later in pits themselves, Alonso passed Hamilton which in turn led Brits to drop down to 6th and 7th, behind Red Bull’s one-two.

Hovever, since on the right compound, McLarens started to fly like concordes, making up position after position. Red Bull, however failed with Vettel’s uncalled decision to get softs in the middle of the race. And Webber’s attempt to open the gap in font led him only to drastically slow down on worn primes as Hamilton, overtaking Alonso by the way, closed the gap and passed the Aussie. That all gave Red Bull only 4th and 5th, as the struggled to keep up with both McLarens’ and Alonso’s pace.
Button - Vettel “battle” for 4th in the middle of the race has shown that with absolutely the same tires, McLaren was HEAPS better, considering the fact that Button was saving tyres for some time, then suddenly opened a huge gap in a couple of laps, which left Vettel crying like a little baby:

Sebastian Vettel:

How am I going to pass him if I’m getting slower and slower?

Speaking of Alonso, he put up a good fight from 3rd, but was eventually passed during the race first by Hamilton, then by ever so “silky-smooth” Button, who, once again, phenomenally kept his tyres in mint condition throughout the whole race.

So it was all clear for McLaren’s double in the end. Congratulations to both Brits who showed everyone who their daddy is. Funny thing is that Toro Rosso’s Buemi LED THE RACE briefly as leaders pitted. He eventually finished in points, scoring 4 on 8th place.

Also, Schumi is back in business, braking Massa’s front wing on lap 65 and cutting corners. But anyway he was passed by Sutil which left him with only one championship point.

So Mclaren now leads WCC by 22 points with 215 against Red Bull’s 193.
Hamilton is now WDC leader with 109 points over Button’s 106 and Webber’s 103.
