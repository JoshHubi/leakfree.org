Once I made this Flash platformer game. It started out as a simple little project with like one bigger level. Then it soon became something like 24 levels, and I kept adding more and more features to it. Then I felt like it was finished and that it was awesome. All this took me like 1 month. Then I decided to add a new feature, and later I added 3 difficulty settings. This all took 3 more months. Then for 1 month I felt that I don’t know what else to add to it but that it’s not really finished yet. Then after like 2 months of not knowing what to do, I added a couple of things and I decided it was probably finished. I never felt like it was finished, I just got to releasing it eventually because I ran out of ideas to add. When you’re programming, or creating something as complicated as a computer game, stuff can get so damn unexpectedly delayed that it’s hard to imagine! It’s not like a building, when you say “oh yeah we need to add some doors and windows and paint everything, should take 6 weeks and we’re done.”, it’s more like “we have no freaking idea how far we are into this right now!”. I don’t know about the developers here, but I’m sure they feel like they don’t really know what “finished” really means and when you can say that something is “finished”. It’s difficult to oversee a project of such complexity. I imagine that the mod is in pretty good shape now, probably many of us would say “oh that’s fine I could play that, just release it already!”, but after so many years of work, the developers want to make sure it’s totally perfect. There’s no finish line or mark that tells you something is at 100%.
