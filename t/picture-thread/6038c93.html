Starfish Brother Soup

And yes, I did saturate the colour - Pretty much had to to try and get some definition out of the shot…

They are slightly fuzzy owing to my being a newbie at the time. I would have done better to tripod the camera (a Canon 400D) and up the f stop to around 8 or so. (And the shutter for one more stop as well) I was using a 75-300 kit lense, which really is not so wonderful of a lense. I really wish I could retake the shot using either my new 70-200mm f4 L series, or my 100-400mm f4 L - Both have image stabalizeation and are TACK sharp. Coupled with my new Canon 7D, and it wouold have been SO much better. (Sigh)

All these images are from my my old system (Which I still have and use here or there - Except for a few of the Zoo shots - those were taken with a Canon G-10 Powershot. Amazing little camera)

Learning all the time… On thing I can say for certain, is that Pro series lenses ARE worth every penny if a person wants to up their game in photopraghy. I will post some of those later, but for now I am just goign through the older ones.

This first shot would have been better with a higher F stop. In this case, the shot is tight enough that blurring the backround really was rahter here or there. So once again, the definition took a hit for no reason. (Again, it was that silly 75-300mm 5.6 kit lense which I don’t use anymore. - More of a frustration than anything. - though, I have certainly took some reasonable pictuires with it, nothing really … amazing. I went and bought that new 55-250 Zoom with image stabalization - the auto focus is retardedly fast - A clearer lens, but still rather lacking compared to the L series lenses. Had I known the differences at the time, I would have bought fewer lenses and simply with the L series. The kit lenses take a “softer” picture which contributes to more blur - Bleh.
