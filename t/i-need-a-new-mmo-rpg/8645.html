Yeah Xenoblade Chronicles has been given rave reviews even by people who are normally pretty cynical. It’s supposed to be a real game-changer for JRPGs. I don’t even have a Wii and I might just pick one up for this game, the reception has just been phenomenal.

As for me, I’d recommend Tales of Symphonia. It’s one of my favorite JRPGs. But the thing is, once you’ve played one Tales game you’ve kinda played them all, so I might just be biased towards this particular one cause that’s the one I played first. However Symphonia in particular does have a bit of a cult following, and it was the only Tales game to get a direct sequel, mediocre though it was, so I feel pretty safe saying it’s the best one.

Chrono Trigger also if you haven’t played it, don’t think I need to say more about it.

The Mass Effect series, Elder Scrolls series, and Deus Ex 1 + 3 are great Western RPGs.

As for MMOs…well, I just gotta say that most F2P MMO’s are junk. Even the ones that start out pretty good usually end up being pay-to-win games with focus on their cash shops. Especially the Asian ones. That said, I had a lot of fun with Global Agenda for a while. It’s a F2P third person shooter MMO. It has a pretty good amount of PVE content now, but its main focus is still on PVP and raids. You really do benefit if you buy the one time premium account as it gives you a lot more exp, and after that if you want to level even faster you can buy a time-based exp booster.

Also, the reason Aion has that polish is that it was originally a subscription based MMO, but went F2P later, as did Everquest and LOTRO, so you can check those out too if you want a big-name game. WoW is also F2P up to 20, but seeing as how it’s kind of the mold for almost every other MMO it might not interest you.

Tried Tales of Symphonia, and with HLE the sound kept cutting in and out. But LLE is just too frickin slow to use. I hope somebody figures out a way to improve it soon.
