To go off what you say, Stabsley, I tend to think the focus should be less on how to reel in the MP5 and more about what makes the other weapons not fun to use in comparison. I find that, like you, I rarely used the Shotgun because it seems woefully inaccurate and weak against enemies more than about 64 units away. Another aspect that I feel is overlooked is reload time, in conjunction with how speedy the enemies are. In this game, to reload is to die, particularly against the impossibly zippy HECU units or Houndeyes who glide effortlessly through the levels. And reloading takes a very long time, especially with a nearly-empty Shotgun, which further diminishes its usefulness. To put it bluntly, the weapon isn’t there when you need it to be. You need double-shots for it to be useful at all, so you deplete your ammo at the most inopportune times. The big magazine of the MP5 means you’ll spend more time firing than reloading, so it’s naturally the thing you’re going to reach for.

To put it another way, if you were playing Borderlands, the MP5’s reload rating would be a 3. The Shotgun would be a 6, and its lack of damage and accuracy would make it inferior in nearly every case.

I have played the first few chapters of the EA release and I was blown away (again). Black Mesa just looks and plays so good (it also makes me wish I had a better computer so I could play BM in its full glory). Out of the lots of things I liked there are a few things that I disliked.

-The shotgun. It just didnt feel like it should feel. I think the Shotgun in Half-Life 2 and its Episodes and the one in the old Version of Black Mesa is perfect. Its one of the strongest weapons in the game - yes - but thats because its supposed to be. its a friggin shotgun. Its limited range and relatively small magazine makes me still use the other weapons. Now it just feels unsatisfying and lame.
I also dislike that you now get the shotgun before the surface battle in WGH. i always thought it was so awesome that you see the shotgun at the beginning of the level and ask yourself “hm how the hell am I going to get this?” and then, after barely escaping marines and helicopters and shit you get into that office and get the shotgun you almost forgot about and finally get to use that (once) awesome gun.

-The scientist before the resonance cascade. Yeah hes the Stanley Parable narrator and thats pretty awesome but I just think the old voice fitted a LOT better. (I know this is nitpicking but it just breaks the immersion for me) I would prefer him as a multiplayer announcer or as one of the scientists of the Lambda team right before the teleporter (iirc there is one scientist sitting away from the others. he could have some interesting dialogue). Rather some optional easteregg instead of “Hey look its the stanley parable guy!!!” (I know its not meant like this but it feels like this)

-The new glock. Although I think the new alt fire mechanic is interesting, it simply makes the gun a lot less useful in my opinion. After getting the mp5 I mainly used the glock to kill barnacles, headcrabs and clear mines and tripmines. In other words: do shit thats not worth the ammo of other guns. All this now takes significantly longer because the glock has such a huge delay (yes it is huge when you try to kill 10 barnacles. it used to take ~2 seconds to kill a barnacle, now its ~6). I could use the alt fire but thats not as accurate and wastes ammo. Then I could simply use the MP5 to clear all of this, it wouldnt make a difference

-I also hope for the VOX-Lines in OC and LC to return, I still dont know if theyre supposed to be absent, but they just make the world of the game seem more rich and detailed.

but remember, these are just a few things I disliked apart from the hundreds of things i liked. keep up the good work

[Edit:] One last thing I think was offputting is the desktop icon. We used to have this nice highres HL2-Style icon, now we have a strange lowres, pixely icon that looks very bad on white backgrounds https://puu.sh/i0rbN/4cda57567d.png

Feasible from a real-world perspective, but a bad idea from a gameplay perspective. By giving them glocks in the early game, you train the player to expect them to carry a glock. This would be frustrating and unintuitive, as you’d have changed the established gameplay rules out from underneath the player.

sourcegamer101 November 25, 2018, 4:33am #139

Sorry for dishing out so many fucking bugs, but this one is pretty game breaking if you use it in the right way. It seems to be left over from HL2. If you press your crouch key, then release it before your character gets back up to full standing height, about 88% to standing height as a rough estimate, then quickly hold crouch, you will be walking at crouch speed, but your view will be above what it should be, almost appearing as if you were slowly walking. You have to time it correctly so you are taller than crouch height, but still shorter than standing height. It is tricky, but really possible. What makes it gamebreaking in both singleplayer and multiplayer if you do this next to a vent or a confined place that requires you to crawl, then go into it, your view will clip into the world and you can see the contents of the whole map, and probably even other players before they can even anticipate you, because your player box is in crouch height to fit in the vent or hole, but your view is higher than that. I haven’t had much time to experiment in multiplayer, but does definitely work in both modes.
