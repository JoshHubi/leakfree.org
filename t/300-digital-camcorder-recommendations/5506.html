$300 Digital Camcorder Recommendations

Technology and Gaming Help and Support
BaTz281 November 24, 2018, 10:34pm #1

Hello all,

Looking to get a digital camcorder in a few weeks, priced in/around $300 US. I don’t really care how portable it is, but I am concerned about the following:

  • Best video quality possible
  • Decent amount of integrated memory (few GBs, at least)
  • SD card expansion (no DVD recorders, although I could live with a hard drive)
  • Decent microphone (able to record live concerts without too much distortion)
  • High Def 1080p would be nice, but not mandatory
  • Decent optical zoom, with optical stabilization

I’ve done a bit of research, but most of the reviews I’ve seen for cameras I’m looking at say things such as, “The video quality is what you expect for a $300 camera.” Well…I don’t know what to expect from a $300 camera . I plan on going to a brick-and-mortar to check out the display models, but that doesn’t always tell you everything about a camera (especially for stuff like moving objects, low-light conditions, audio interference, etc) so I’d appreciate some input, if anyone here has experience with or recommendations for a decent camera.

sersoft November 24, 2018, 10:34pm #2

You shouldn’t care about onboard memory, it’s all about SD cards these days so be prepared to buy/steal a good class 6 SD card. And for the camera, any popular manufacturer will do. Just don’t expect it to take full HD video and have 30x optical zoom, at that price range you get a shitty looking camera with average specs or a good looking camera with shitty ass specs.

Just read lots of reviews and buy the one with the most favorable opinion.

FireTime November 24, 2018, 10:34pm #4

Personally if you want good quality you will not get many of those other option for that price.

Your best bet is to get the best possible camera for $300 that has support for external mics (be it XLR or 1/8in). This way you can use a proper microphone and achieve better quality than you would with most built in ones. Also I second what sersoft said about memory. None of the proper cameras have internal memory anymore. The last film shoot I was on the cameras only shoot to SD speed class 10 cards. When the card was removed the camera can only stream to fire wire, no internal recording.
