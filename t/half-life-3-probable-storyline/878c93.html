Dyer November 24, 2018, 6:16pm #247

As somebody already said, it’s impossible to speculate with any degree of confidence until after Episode 3. Even then, I doubt anyone will come close to determining the real storyline. Look at the ending of HL. From that, would any of you have thought “I’ll bet the next game takes place twenty years in the future after a previously unheard of alien empire conquers the planet and Gordon is brought in by the G-Man to fight them for reasons unknown”? No.

While guessing about the storyline is completely futile, we can think about some of the mechanics/conventions that may come back, such as stasis. For example, Gordon may wake up from stasis unexpectedly this time. Maybe the G-Man is getting his shit fucked by some new enemy (or the Combine or the Xenians) and , in said shit fucking, the enemy accidentally wakes Gordon up. How about what “revolution” will be featured in HL3? You could argue that HL pioneered scripted events and HL2 added an emphasis on in-game physics. In HL3 it could be some radical new control method, like a webcam being used for interacting with other characters. That’s a shit idea, but the point is something new will be introduced, and at this stage I doubt that Valve even knows what that is.

While Ep3 wraps up the HL2 storyline, there’s no reason for Alyx not to return. Kleiner and Eli returned from HL, although you could argue that they were retconned into HL after HL2. I would say that this was due to the lack of real characters in HL. In HL2, a huge emphasis was placed on these characters, and now that Valve has them introduced they can continue to use them without having to insert new characters into an already existing continuity at the start of HL3.

Wow, wall of text.

TL;DR: We don’t know enough to speculate about HL3’s story, but it will probably feature a new revolutionary technology and new takes on series conventions. Since HL2’s story arc is wrapped up, the Combine may not come back for HL3 but there is no reason for the characters not to.
