higgsboson November 25, 2018, 2:11am #24

Yes, play Half-Life from beginning to end before you play Black Mesa. You don’t have to, but it will add something that’s an important part of Black Mesa: nostalgia and a sense of seeing something again, but differently…

I have rarely encountered save corruptions in Half-Life, if ever, so I’d say just go play it and don’t worry about this, it’s very unlikely to happen again. Or just back up the SAVE folder after each time you play, copy it somewhere to be safe. But really, this shouldn’t be an issue!

Oh, and most people who played Half-Life: Source suggest that the original, non-source version is better, so I’d say if you’re going to buy something, buy the original, not the Source version. After all, Black Mesa is a remake of the original, not the HL: Source version, even though the difference is minor.
