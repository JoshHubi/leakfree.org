JeffMOD August 11, 2020, 11:25am #4

HL2’s models are based off of real people, but that was only for reference - technically speaking, an actual person isn’t required.
Also, most of Black Mesa’s NPCs are based off a single base head with face flexors for deformation - similar to the way character creation in Mass Effect or The Elder Scrolls is handled. They were maybe influenced by real people, but each head itself by definition cannot be ‘modeled’ after a real person, because it’s a generic base mesh with modifications.

That being said, it would be child’s play for a character artist to take the source files for the scientist (either from the team files or decompiling one of the existing models), make a unique Einstein head variation using the HL1 model, concept art, or someone who looks similar to the model as reference, and assign it to a specific named scientist NPC using the character manifest file.
