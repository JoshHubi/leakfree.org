Somewhat correct.

I create the initial “transitional area” at 1:1 scale, and then shrink it down.

In this instance, I consider the “transitional area,” to be the part of the skybox which visually connects to the actual main mapping area. I can’t think of a better way to explain this. Just think of it as the start of the skybox. I map it in full scale so the displacements actually properly connect, and so there are no visual seams between the map area and the 3D skybox

Once that’s done, it gets shrunk down to 1/16th scale, and then I map out the rest of the skybox at the 1/16th scale. It’s a good system. Mapping at 1/16th is understandably a LOT easier when you’re dealing with huge unwieldy chunks of landscape.

It’s coming along well, but don’t expect anything soon. The view should be great though.
