Okay, frankly, I have no idea what you’re trying to say (other than the fact that it sounds somewhat rude), but let me put it this way.

In Half-Life’s story, Earth is a planet. Xena is a planet. Xen - the one involved in the Black Mesa Incident - is not a planet, it’s what’s referred to as a “border-world”. It does not exist in the same physical space that Earth does (that is, the universe). It’s implied to be the “space between dimensions”.

Xena is not a name inspiration for Half-Life’s Xen - HL1 was released in 1998, while Xena/Eris was discovered in 2005. There is no connection, in real-life or in the Half-Life universe, between the dwarf planet Xena and the border-world of Xen.

Therefore, there is no reason to change Xen’s name, as it is completely independent of the dwarf planet in our universe.
