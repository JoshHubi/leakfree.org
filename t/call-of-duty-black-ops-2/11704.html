Mented November 25, 2018, 2:55am #8

He’s right, the single player is terrible. Not even hilariously so, just terrible.

I quite liked the storyline of Black Ops 1. It was over the top and historically inaccurate all over but it was fun and held a strong narrative. Black Ops 2 however is absolutely shit, Seemingly written by a group of teenagers for a high school writing class. I’d go into it but it isn’t worth the bother.

The gameplay itself however is. The scripting is awful by standards set 10 years ago, let alone todays. Friendlies will completely ignore enemies should the script tell them to move forward. Animations are even worse. Enemy helicopters, or any such vehicle will instantly explode into an ugly mess once its hit points are depleted. Enemies still have dying, peaking and general moving animations reminiscent of the original medal of honors.

Treyarchs attempt at RTS/FPS hybrid in the form of ‘strike force missions’ are, to put it bluntly, an absolute abomination. Unresponsive commands due to both poor input and having to control said terrible A.I would be the first and major problem / reason for not attempting such a thing. The layout of these ‘missions’ is also terrible, pitting you against endless supplies of enemies in which your outnumbered 20-1, in maps with very little cover as is. A clear indication the developers at Treyarch have simply not played an RTS game in the past 20 years.

The multiplayers problems consist not of bad level design, as they are actually quite well done. It’s the gun mechanics that let it down. Within the first two days the game has devolved into nothing but the highest rate of fire weapons being used exclusively, on account of there being practically zero recoil. Changes such as the pick 10 system are good, however it doesn’t offer up the kind of variety it could due to the rather absurdly long process to unlock everything on account of the target audience.

In game you have the endless UAVs, leaving you near enough constantly on the enemies radar, which in conjunction with the small, yet open maps leave you consistently unable to form any kind of tactical route or style of play. This again has seemingly been reinforced by Treyarch, who have added in stun mine gadgets, 360 proximity mines ( an upgrade from directional claymores ), ‘slow down beam’ scorestreaks and other such things. Which imo shows a lack of understanding when considering what challenges players whilst still being fun.

Added to that the community, whos idea of a tactics is to jump around every corner shooting or simply going prone the second they see someone, adds to the annoyance and feeling the game simply isn’t designed well, trying to be a tactical game whilst not overcoming the major problems that plague its attempts to do so.

I’l be playing it a bit more, but it’s more than likely going back. Trade it in now whilst I can still get something decent for it and put it towards GTA.
