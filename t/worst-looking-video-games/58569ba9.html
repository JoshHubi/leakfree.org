Jeannotvb November 24, 2018, 10:53pm #43

Call of Duty: Black Ops

It still uses the same engine from the first Modern Warfare, the shadow density is very low. They still use BSP to create most of the level geometry instead of meshes. Most textures are low-res 512x512. The models are very low poly. No physics on models with proper collision, static model collision is made using BSP. Dynamic lighting with high quality shadows is also very limited from what I remember in Modern Warfare.

I don’t know why they do it, either time is not on their side and they can’t afford a proper upgrade or they don’t bother changing the engine seeing how it’s used to create very fast paced games and nobody will really stand still and look at something close-up for long. Even so, it’s far behind other game engines like the Unreal engine.
