Yes, taking down Megaupload should have been illegal. There are plenty of legit and not legit uses for the tech, and it’s very useful for high bandwidth file transfers that you just can’t do reliably through an email or instant messaging client.

First up: Fansubbers inhabit a grey area of legality where they translate Japanese anime programs to english and release “fansubs” (portmanteau of Fan and Subtitle, which is Exactly What’s On The Tin). They use “Fair Use” to release the subs, as being “for educational purposes.” Admittedly this is nebulously legal at best, but some anime production companies actually use fansub communities as a barometer of how likely a studio-done localization would be to turn a profit. It also acts as free publicity, as having an established fanbase of the associated work would allow them to have a market for a studio translation. Megaupload and other fileshare sites are utilized to upload such fansubs fairly often, but to MU’s credit, they take them down when people serve takedown notices on them.

The other big thing is the fact that people can in fact use fileshare sites for legitimate traffic. I for one use 4shared to transfer in-progress cuts between myself and Eric for the purposes of a collaborative workflow. If I’d been using MU instead, I’d be up shit creek because suddenly my fileshare would be gone. Email attachments can only hold so much data, and cuts of an in progress film regularly hit hundreds of MB, and in HD a full cut would easily hit the 10GB range.

Taking down a whole fileshare site because some people utilize it to pirate copyrighted material is very much unconstitutional and uncalled for, and undermines the rights of people utilizing it’s paid service lawfully.
