MarshmallowRadiation November 25, 2018, 12:11am #1

I was wondering about Wheatley’s place in the storyline. When was he activated? What did he do to get him detached from GLaDOS and listed as “corrupted?” So I came up with this:

1: Wheatley is responsible for the infamous BYDTWD.
When activated, GLaDOS had Wheatley attached to her, and Wheatley (who is programmed to make bad decisions) promptly floods the enrichment center with neurotoxin. I mean seriously, why would GLaDOS waste all the lives of those valuable test subjects?

2: Wheatley is responsible for the crap turrets.
He said he almost had a job in the turret factory, but the Foreman decided not to give him the job. I’m guessing Wheatley might have had the job for a few seconds, but in that time screwed up the production system so royally that the best ratio of good turrets to crap turrets they could possibly get is 1:1. (even with Aperture’s odd way of going about business it seems odd that FIFTY PERCENT of their turrets are blind, bulletless, armorless, laserless, broken wrecks.) Instead, Wheatley was placed in charge of keeping the test subjects healthy, which is automated by the Announcer anyway and who cares about the test subjects’ well-being?

3: The Announcer isn’t really prerecorded messages, but an actual AI.
The Announcer seems to have limited AI already (able to sense and respond to people on the turret redemption lines, able to pick up and interpret vague answers during a stalemate, etc.) but I mean actual AI. In Wheatley’s “almost got a job” story, he said the Foreman made a duplicate of himself run the factory. Guess who is running the factory? The Announcer. Ta-daa.

4: Chell is the adopted daughter of Caroline.
Yeah, I already touched on this. No more need…

EDIT 5: Chell isn’t out of Aperture Science yet.
If I remember right, I saw a promotion for Portal 2 that showed Aperture was planning on building a farm for giant chickens. The farm would contain a blue sky, fluffy white clouds, AND A WHEAT FIELD.

So, that’s all I have. Feel free to comment on these theories and/or leave theories of your own here.

danielsangeo November 25, 2018, 12:11am #2

In regards to Wheatley, according to the chronology, after Chell defeats GLaDOS the first time and is put into stasis/motel room by the Party Escort Associate, the multitude of cores come online (you see them coming online at the end of the Still Alive video).

For a time, not much happens. The cores maintain the facility and begin to get personalities. However, the cores are not quite able to do the job and begin to either malfunction and shirk their duties in maintaining the facilities causing it to fall into a state of disrepair, or without GLaDOS around, the cores are unable to maintain the facility and it begins to cause the facility to fall into a state of disrepair, causing the cores to malfunction.

Either way, the cores malfunction, have personalities (such as one wanting to go into space), and the facility falls into disrepair.

One core, Wheatley, was tasked with monitoring the humans (as he tells Chell as they’re going through the facility). As such, he begins to grow a small dislike for humans and perhaps somewhat of an envy for them. Perhaps. Finally, he awakens Chell because he has the bright idea that Chell could get him out of the facility. He’s unable to do it on his own so asks Chell to help. They get to what they believe to be the escape pod but end up reactivating GLaDOS instead. Later, Wheatley becomes in charge of the facility (good job breaking it, hero) and his small amount of animosity suddenly mushrooms when he realizes how much power he has.

So, there it is. I don’t believe that Wheatley is responsible for the crap turrets or the BYDTWD debacle. I think that the BYDTWD debacle was GLaDOS’ doing and the crap turrets are just a result of the state of the facility.

I don’t believe in the theory that Chell is an android, but I also doubt the theory that she was adopted by Charline. Rosie’s theory seems pretty reliable, except from the fact that GLaDOS was created after BYDTWD.

Wheatley may have been the intelligence core which didn’t get incinerated (GLaDOS may have reacted that the core was out of reach from her like the “string” that came between her and the cores when they were unattached) and connected itself to a rail. His story before he got attached to GLaDOS is that he was made by aperture and almost got a job he wanted (there is no clue of what job, so the theory about him working with the turrets remains a conspiracy), but he instead got in charge of the test subjects. After a while he got attached to GLaDOS and makes her kill everyone but Chell and Ratman. If he didn’t kill Chell because she might help him out of the facility you can figure out.

Ratman, like all humans, can’t live 100 years, he even couldn’t survive 70 considering the dangers and medical disadvantages you can get from being too long in a test chamber. If he could have gone the wrong way and instead of leaving the enrichment center, he could have gone into stasis or in a relaxation vault, but Ratman knew too much about Aperture. He drew arrows pointing where to go to get to GLaDOS and escape, if he could move the panels, he could have made every single place in the walls to his dens. My theory is that Ratman either escaped or died before portal 1 and another human got into the enchantment center while Chell was in the relaxation vault before portal 2. GLaDOS said she saw some humans outside the facility and if they are outside it, it is a high chance of one just walking right in there.

But to be honest Ratman is like Gman. He is everywhere and lives forever, so they both have to be Valves versions of Jesus.

The personality Cores in general can’t just be a random AI. They have to be Apertures failed experiment to put a brain into a machine, they either failed and could only know one thing at the time or got corrupted/their information decayed over the time nobody could maintain them. I have already seen some episodes of History channels “Life after people” so I have a basic knowledge of the topic. The thing that I began wondering about was that the relaxation vaults looked so maintained and almost new even after some hundred years.

Shortened, my theories are:

  1. The intelligence core was active before GLaDOS and became Wheatley.
  2. There were 2 “Ratmen”, one before Portal 1 and one between Portal 1 and 2.
  3. Ratman is basically the same concept like Gman.
  4. The personality cores are either a failed or successful experiment on AI mapping that decayed over time.
