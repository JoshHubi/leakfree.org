DoctorWeeTodd November 24, 2018, 5:47pm #23

I had a similar idea.

Instead of the G-Man though, it was for the Nihilanth.During the teleportation/dream scene I pictured an extreme closeup of his face, that panned to one of his eyes. It showed you the final threat but left a lot more to be seen.

I always felt exposition was poorly handled in the original Half-Life. There was absolutely no connection between the beginning and end; no conflict between the final antagonist and protagonist. It left a game where I felt no sense of purpose and didn’t care at all for a final boss that I didn’t know anything about if not for a single line from a scientist.

The games later improved on narrative. In Half-Life 2, the Citadel was visible from the very beginning and provided a clear goal. Dr. Breen and a Combine Advisor was seen in a teleportation mishap, which foreshadowed the end solution and the Advisor appearances expanded on a looming threat. In the original Half-Life, I had no idea why I was doing what I was doing for the most part, and had absolutely no idea why I was fighting a giant alien baby.
