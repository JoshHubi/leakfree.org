Sage_J_Fox November 25, 2018, 4:36am #4

All hard choices for this one!

Favorite Area: Questionable Ethics - I feel like this area received one of the best revisions yet in all of the facility, everything ties together beautifully while still being very faithful to the original. Also helps it’s where you pick up my
Favorite Weapon: Snarks - Adorable little buggers, and effective both for the hurtings and for playing Minesweeper. Black Mesa’s models for the snarks themselves and the nests are lovely recreations of the originals, the view model animations for them especially.

Least Favorite Area: Residue Processing - Not the fault of Crowbar Collective, but, this one does not enjoy trudging through sewage attempting to dodge more fire, ooze, and spinning blades of death than Rasputin had to deal with.

Least Favorite Weapon: Tripmine - Have yet to successfully lure one enemy into one, they just do not wish to follow. Also the bane of my existence in HL1DM (have yet to have a problem with them in BMDM. Yet.)

Honorable Mentions:
Hivehand - Another lovely recreation, love the effects with shooting. Also, its pickup has to be one of the iconic moments of Black Mesa. However, it’s currently pulling a Gordon on the devs and not showing up for work, and only sees limited use in these hands.
Anomalous Materials - So many memorable lines and references, so little time. Sets up caring for your co-workers fairly well, but missed out on the chance to pass by a retirement party.
Tau Cannon - My pretty baby, why must you have so little ammunition? Game balance is a fickle maiden.
Office Complex - Classic Half-Life here, redone beautifully. Don’t really have a negative, just love Questionable Ethics more.

Agent6 November 25, 2018, 4:36am #15

Okay, here we go:

Favorite level: Questionable Ethics, mainly due to the design, experiments, and combat. Pretty much the most interesting chapter found in BM. As for HL1, I’d actually say that Xen is my fav. chapter there. The whole world is just very intriguing to me, and, unlike some people, who complain about the platforming parts, they’re not a problem for me, and I didn’t dislike them either at all, nor found them to be out of place or anything like that.

Favorite weapon: Oh boy, this one is pretty hard for me.

I’d choose either the .357 or the shotgun. I’ve never been a fan of SMGs, otherwise I would’ve chosen the MP5. It does indeed feel much better and fun to use in the actual game than the mod, although I, personally, liked the design of the MP5 from the 2012 release much more than the current one. Sadly, the reload animations in the mod were hilariously bad from my point of view, which screwed it in the end. Also I love the iron sights on the .357, I think it’s a welcomed feature.

Least favorite level: Another hard choice, basically because I just love both BM and HL and think that they’re pretty well polished. I think I’ll go for On a Rail. I prefer the original chapter from HL than the one in BM, and not because it’s bad per see, but it’s just much, much shorter. That’s why I liked the one from the original game a lot more than the current one, because I like to explore all areas in a game, which sadly, can’t really happen in BM.

Least favorite weapon: Both the Snarks and the Tripmines. The Snarks are not so useful in both BM and HL SP, but I wouldn’t go as far as to say that they’re useless. They’re just… not very well implemented in neither BM nor HL, I think their design was rushed or not well thought in HL. They can sometimes be useful to distract the enemies. As or the Tripmines, the player has literally no point to use them, in their case I’d say that yes, they’re actually useless for the SP. But, despite this, they can be very, very useful in MP if you know where to place them, but in SP, they’re worthless. Never used them in any of my playthroughs.

Agent6 November 25, 2018, 4:36am #18

Ok, my thoughts on the NPCs:

Favorite NPC(s): Vortigaunts, Gangantua, Alien Controller, Alien Grunt. Their behavior remained pretty much unchanged from HL, and it’s exactly how it should be. I’m a bit disappointed though about the vorts, I’ve been hoping for new sounds, but, they’re the same from HL2. They’re not bad as enemies et all, but could’ve been slightly better.

Also the Houndeyes seem to have a faster sound attack, compared to the HL one, which is great because it took them so long in the original game to use their attack that they rarely ever hit me, with the exception of group attacks, but even then, they wouldn’t hit me more than once. They’re much better in BM.

Favorite boss: Gargantua. Again, its behavior remained the same from HL, and it’s also looking much better. The Apache fights can also take this place (as fav.) since they’re (at least from my experience) much more challenging than in the original HL. And the assassins, they’re just as tough as in HL, or maybe even tougher, it’s somewhat hard for me to compare the BM ones with the HL ones. Some may find them very annoying due to their skill, but that’s exactly what I want. A challenge, so I have a much bigger satisfaction when I defeat them. An easy boss is not a boss after all.

Favorite Human NPC(s): Barney. Can’t really say anything here.

Least Favorite Boss: Actually I don’t have such a thing, I like all the bosses in BM, some more than others, but there aren’t any bosses that I don’t like, or at least I’m not inclined in that direction. Yes, there may be some ,ok, bosses, but I don’t dislike any of them.

Least Favorite Alien: Snarks. They’re weak and pretty much useless against enemies. Yes, you can distract the enemies with them, but they’re killed very fast, and the player needs to throw quite a lot of snarks at the enemies to actually kill someone, which from my experience, rarely occurred.

Least Favorite Human: The Marines. I honestly don’t understand why so many people complained about the voice acting in the mod version. It’s simply incomprehensible for my mind. They sounded like professionals with voice filters on, but they were just perfect imo. Now they sound like a bunch of under 20yrs old idiots. They’re by far the worst human enemies I’ve ever encountered from my point of view. Don’t misunderstand, the AI seems to be pretty good, but the sounds… are just terrible.
