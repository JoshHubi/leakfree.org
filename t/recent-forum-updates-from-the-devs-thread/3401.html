Reposted from the Updates thread:

raw_bean:

I’ve been thinking for a long while about the merits of a thread summarising recent mod-related posts by the devs who contribute to the forums, whether that be in a separate thread, in here, or on a wiki page. I think even those of us who do keep up with the forums regularly would be surprised to see how much Ram and James and the others do actually say about the mod (for example in answers in the Got a Question thread) when it’s all gathered in one place.

It wouldn’t be that hard to do, just by going dev by dev through their recent posting history for relevant posts and maybe a quick hop into the originating thread if the original context isn’t clear. The question is what would be the best way to present it? In here, in another thread, on the wiki, some combination? “Recent Forum Updates from the devs”?

EDIT: How about this, I’ll create a closed sticky and a wiki page with all the interesting dev posts I can find for the last few months, then update them both once a month. At the same time, I’ll stick the same info in a post in here for discussion, and a new post in here with the new info once a month for further discussion. Good idea?

Further EDIT: I think doing this would also help feed me or anyone else who wants to help out with ideas for FAQ updates, both on the wiki and the forums.

Final EDIT: And in an effort to please everyone (despite my earlier assertion about it being impossible) when I post up the dev postings and each time I update them after that, I could push to get a dev with twitter access to tweet a link. I should have plenty of time this week so I think I’ve talked myself into doing it like this, unless anyone has any alternative suggestions or comments.

Questions/comments? I’m gonna start compiling posts over the next few days.
