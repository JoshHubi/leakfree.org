Serza:

Wait. You solo’d fucking GOLD/PLATINUM? Fuck. I can barely solo Silver, sometimes I have trouble with Bronze (mostly tried with Asari Justicar, though, not my favorite Quarian Infiltrator)

Yup. Just gold though, not platinum, I can’t even imagine that, its brutal even with 4 people. I did my solo runs with the Turian Infiltrator and the Alliance Infiltration Unit. They are great for soloing because they are quick, can cloak, and have wonderful shield regen powers that use grenades, plus they still do lots of damage. When soloing, speed and being able to hide trump any amount of shields or damage output though, so spec for longer lasting cloak (helps a lot for running to cover and doing objectives), and use an adrenaline module. Use a mod that expands the capacity of your grenades, and use the regen power to save yourself when you start taking damage. Since it’s just you, you’ll always have grenades to pick up, and you won’t have to rely on your limited ops packs. It will take a long time to finish, no question about it though, and it may take some luck of the draw with the objectives. Hopefully you don’t get 4 device or hack objectives, those are the worst because they force you to stay in one spot.

There is a way to game the system though, if you’re feeling up to it and have one or more friends willing to help you. I did it once or twice just to speed it up, since I felt I had already proven myself with my other solo runs. The way ME3 counts games towards the achievement is just checking if you extract by yourself. That means you can have up to 4 people playing up until round 11. As long as they quit before the extraction, you will extract alone and get the achievement, so you can just run around and hide the whole round until it’s time to extract. Now my memory is a little hazy, but what I did is I let my friend host, then at round 11 he quit, which restarted the round with me as host, then I finished and it counted towards my achievement. I don’t know for sure if it is important you do it that way or not, but I think it might be. You can try googling it if you want.
