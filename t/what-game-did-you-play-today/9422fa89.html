Bloodshot November 25, 2018, 4:15am #3396

The Operative: No One Lives Forever

I swear this fucking game makes the biggest budget most well received AAA games look like a monotonous copy pasted travesty.

It has 40+ missions (feels longer than Half-Life), and every single one of these missions has goals that differ from the last so you never really do the same thing.

On top of that, every single level of this game has its own texture set, textures are only really ever reused if its the same type of building (like a warehouse) which I’ve only seen like 2 or 3 times. You go to aztec ruins around swamps, space stations, the swiss alps, space, forests, underground bases, morocco, sunken underwater ships (with their own gameplay mechanics that don’t suck, and even face off against sharks using harpoon guns), nightclubs, fall out of a plane, and more.

The production values are really high for it too, and the attention to detail is astounding. There are so many humorous or informative conversations you can overhear as you sneak around that really rewards you for opting to do so instead of running and gunning, and the game has several vehicle sections on motorcycles and snowmobiles that are comparable to HL2, albeit more interesting.

It just blows my mind how they accomplished so much in 2000, and did it so well. The cartoony style of the games graphics just helps it to hold up more today than other games do.
