Level:

Also, what are your thoughts on steroids? You’re in competitions so I assume you don’t, but do you know anyone who does? Do they test you for steroids and stuff at competitions?

I don’t judge people who use them but I do have an opinion on those who use them as a way to get bigger faster without reaching their natural potential. I believe if you’re going to use AAS to get bigger, you need to reach your body’s natural limits first. If you’ve been eating more, lifting harder, doing everything you can for 6 months and you’ve gained not much more than a pound, and you are huge, I can see it. But when you’ve got these 16, 17, 18 year old kids who weigh like 160-170 pounds trying to get hormones and even legitimate AAS, it just astonishes me. Steroids and hormones are not going to do anything but fuck you up pretty good if you don’t research them thoroughly before using them, have a proper PCT, and stay strict with your cycle. Steroids are not a magic substance, you still have to work hard, keep a strict diet, and maintain a good lifestyle to get bigger. You don’t just inject some D-Bol and then sit on your ass all day expecting to grow. Steroids don’t take any hard work away from anyone that uses them simply because they cannot naturally grow anymore, but they do make an ass out of the kids looking for a shortcut to get big, and those are the ones you hear about who get injured or die from injections.

I am completely natural, the class I compete in in my comps are natural classes, but the guy who placed first last year ahead of me was on some serious HGH. They do testing, but people time their cycles up right so that by the time they are finished, all of the steroids or hormones have left their system by the time they start doing bloodwork and piss tests for the competition. I personally will probably never use steroids. I’m doing this to push my body to the limits of its own potential, not shorten my lifespan.

Collision November 24, 2018, 7:37pm #48

Yes, Parkour (Freerunning for those who do not know proper terms), requires a limbre, balanced and fit physique.

You need to constantly move fast and not lose speed, be able to jump a very high height for an average person, I have not tested myself, but I’m sure a big bulked up body would very easily conflict with maneuverability.

Plus adding extra body weight would make it a lot more harder on your legs (knees especially) when landing from high jumps or falls.

Getting into a gym and toning up is by no means a bad idea for the sport, but bulking up is.

Parkour requires a very large amount of strength to do, for instance, lifing your own body weight with one arm I can imagine be hard to do when you weigh 100+ kilo’s from muscle mass.

A good weight for parkour is about 60-80KG depending on your height, I am 175cm and weigh 60KG, a good balance for the sport.
