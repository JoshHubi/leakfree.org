OldDirtyBastard November 24, 2018, 8:58pm #223

Deload. Your performance = your fitness - your stored fatigue. As you train and lift heavy, you get more fit, but you also get more and more chronically tired. Let your fatigue dissipate by taking a week off or taking it easy on your weights.
Or actually try something different.
Try to rotate exercises (e.g. incline/decline), change rep/set range.
Your body adapts to the same routine very quickly. Your goal is to keep it guessing what next, be it more/less weight every next week, or more/less reps/sets, or completely new routine.

If you do the same shit over and over again, your body has no reason to change.
Don’t forget to EAT and sleep well.

EDIT: Also Kaze, have you tried 10x10 German volume training? I heard it’s some pretty rough ‘n’ tough shit. Not that I’m considering to switch to it anytime soon though.
