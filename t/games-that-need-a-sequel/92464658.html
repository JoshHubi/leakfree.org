k3nny1550 November 25, 2018, 2:05am #31
OldDirtyBastard:

and?

Wasn’t it intentional?

Yes, it was very much intentional. Basically the entire point of MGS2 was that Hideo Kojima hated fans who idolized Solid Snake, hated his publisher for making him milk the franchise, and hated the fans even more for continuing to buy into his games despite his obvious attempt at brutalizing them through the story.

Heck, I’m one of the few people who actually liked the ending, because it brought up commentary on mass surveillance and censorship before people even thought twice about it, despite the story being penned mostly before 9/11. The game is even used to teach meme theory in some college courses. I’m not saying MGS2 is a bad game.

I am, however, saying that MGS3 was the more polished product. It’s decontructive elements, such as the entire story arc of the Boss, served the story rather than merely brutalizing the player. It had greater gameplay variety, better controls, better camera- Everything about it was just better. A shame that only the first two acts of MGS4 were worthwhile to play through more than once. A tailing mission, a nostalgia chapter, and a map with all enemies instantly alerted to your presence does not a fun Metal Gear make, particularly when it’s billed on sneaking through battlefields in virtually every press kit about the game ever.

A game can be critical of itself, it’s medium, and it’s story tropes without leaning out of the screen and screaming at the player “YOU ARE AN ASSHOLE FOR BUYING THIS. FUCK OFF.”

ADDENDUM: The way to do it would probably be Peace Walker, in terms of deconstructing the player’s involvement in the story. You’re basically making Big Boss relive his most painful memories of the end of MGS3, and helping him turn his unit of mercenaries into [COLOR=‘Black’]Outer Heaven, complete with it’s own Metal Gear. It’s amazingly subtle compared with MGS2. I sure felt like an asshole at the end of that game. Then I went back and started playing the Pooyan missions because this game is fucking addicting.
