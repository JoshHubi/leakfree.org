Not really a fan of achievements since they don’t give anything other than satisfaction for a completionist and bragging rights. Since I am not a completionist and prefer to brag using skill rather than my ability to carry a gnome across a game, I really have no use for achievements.
I actually have some console fan-boy friends who spend hours, sometimes days on a game that they grew tired of just so that they can get some achievement. And since after that they usually sell the game I really wonder why they bother.
Achievements detract from the whole point of video games. Games should be played for enjoyment, escapism, the ability to do amazing things you would otherwise be unable to do or all those things at once.
Not so that you can spend forever doing something you probably wouldn’t do otherwise just so that you can get a virtual “achievement” that will only really make you lose time out of your life that you could be spending on something else.
Believe me, if you stop trying to get achievements in games you will enjoy them a lot more and you will not have to spend hours for literally nothing.
