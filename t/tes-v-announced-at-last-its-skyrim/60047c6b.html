I think that’s the whole point of the factions in Skyrim - nothing is supposed to make you feel like you’re making 100% the right decision. The Stormcloaks want the sovereignty over Skyrim, but they will do just about anything to get their way, and don’t seem to care that breaking off from the Empire would make them weaker as well, and could soon lead to their defeat by the Thalmor. The Empire needs Skyrim to stay with them so they can have strength enough to make war against the Thalmor possible again someday, but of course, until then, they allow the Thalmor to operate as they please and aren’t entirely respectful of the Nords’ honor. Even the main quest is kinda like that. Sure, you need to defeat Alduin, but the Blade woman insists you kill Paarthurnax and turn your back on the Greybeards, while the Greybeards scorn the Blades for being too violent. The side guilds are a bit more straightforward, if I remember correctly, and don’t really offer much moral choice other than whether you want to join them or not, but overall I think one of the main themes of the game is that you can take sides, but no side is completely right.
