They just don’t know any better. The basic terrain data for the world is not very big, all it is is simple geometric data, which can be greatly compressed. What fills it, models, textures, sounds, scripts, etc, is what makes the bulk of the game size and that can also be compressed. Quests are simply scripts, which are very negligible in size and they all consist of telling the game what should the player do and where to go, alongside specific conversation audio and animation that there might be, and other than that, it uses whatever assets are already in the game.

Open world games like The Elder Scrolls are often times surprisingly small in file size despite the amount of content in it.
