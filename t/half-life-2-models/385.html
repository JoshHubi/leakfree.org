Mangled November 24, 2018, 5:31pm #7

You can’t just bung everything in your HL2 directory.

Get GCFScape (google it) it will allow you to explore your source engine files to see where they are in the directories.

For instance if it was a sound you would open source sounds.gcf and look for the sound you want to replace… do not replace the file in the gcf but simply use the same directory that the file uses in the gcf for where you put the replacement in your hl2 directory.

ie: if it was shotgun sounds you wanted to replace, you could look around source sounds.gcf and find this directory: hl2\sound\weapons\shotgun

then you would know to place the mod sounds in C:\Program Files\Steam\steamapps\username\half-life 2\hl2\sound\weapons\shotgun

The same concept applies to skins, textures and models which are located in other .gcfs

But really… any skins/sounds/models should come with information on where to install them to.
