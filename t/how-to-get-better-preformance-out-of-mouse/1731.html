While I would (heavily) recommend upgrading to an optical/laser mouse, I do have some tips.

As stated by Kaveros, you should do a good cleaning of your mouse. First, open the ball cover on the underside. There are likely 3 wheels inside. Two of them are for the X and Y axis. The third is simply to give support to let the ball move freely. Remove the ball, and take a Q-Tip or toothpick and pick off the dirt and gunk that has accumulated on the rollers. If you need to, hold them still with a finger while using the other hand for the cleaning tool. Once you have the rollers clean, make sure you also clean the ball (as stated above) to stop more debris from accumulating in the mouse, and also clean the feet of the mouse. There are probably 3 or 4 on your mouse, and you want them to be clean so that there is less friction between your mousepad/desk and the mouse itself.
