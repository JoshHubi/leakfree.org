Jeannotvb November 24, 2018, 11:08pm #3368

In ZBrush you can use the UV Master plug-in. I usually unwrap models in 3ds max but unwrapping organic models takes much more time and is best done in programs like ZBrush. It’s still possible to unwrap organics in 3ds max by dividing the models into seperate parts and unwrapping each section using UVW modifiers. The ZBrush UV Master plug-in saves a lot of time though.

edit:

I’m changing the teeth on the model, they just feel too small and should be more D-shaped in cross section as seen in other Tyrannosaurids.

edit 2:

@ p0rt1r

Unwrapping is not done at the highest level of subdivision in ZBrush. Basically ZBrush saves more than just the location of each vertex, so you can just go back to the lowest subdivision mesh (the base mesh) that consists of only several thousand polygons. You then unwrap that model, then you switch back to the high poly subdivision level made up of 20.000.000+ verts (in my case) while retaining the UVW map and all the little details that you previously sculpted onto the model.
