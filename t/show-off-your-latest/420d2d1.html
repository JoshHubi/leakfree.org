Ramirezoid:

Actually, it would, the way I visualize things in my head is far better looking than what I produce.

I think that’s always the case with everybody except for those ultra-talented Mozart-like people, but we’re spiteful of them anyway. It takes practice and definitely trying new things, which I’m guessing you’ve done. Try working on cleaning it up after you’ve finished doing the lines, or maybe even after you’ve colored for something different. Go back over your squiggles and clean them up into single smooth lines. Keep practicing until you feel you’re somewhat competent, then go back and see how you can improve your “squiggle” habits, see if you can do away with it, or are still comfortable with it, and improve it.

But I’m sure most of that you already know if you’ve been drawing a long time. Just try to improve your skills so you CAN do more, you don’t want to be a one-trick pony. Remember, Picasso was actually a pretty skilled realist painter, it’s just that at some point he decided to do something different and found his new niche.
