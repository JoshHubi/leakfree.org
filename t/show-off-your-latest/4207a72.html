Wark August 11, 2020, 7:21pm #2214

About a week ago I saw the Dear Esther mod for the first time. Amazed, I decided to put my art skills into gear and attempt a short 1-2 hour mod and see just how far I could emulate it’s awesomeness. I typed up a short haunting, silent hill esque script and I’ve just begun modelling.

The player approaches the tilted remains of the table. Its legs are broken off, but the body remains atop a rock near the center, putting it at arm’s reach. The drawer is jarred open and inside is a flashlight, some extra batteries, and a few odd notes. One of them reads, “You’ll need this.” On pickup, the light shines as it’s flipped on.

I was amazed to see the kind of texturing you can get out of a Source game once I started using sprites and models as details (after stealing the idea from the Dear Esther blog). This is very early without the finer details and features an incomplete normal map and it already looks amazing in game.
