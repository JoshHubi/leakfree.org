Shaddy June 25, 2019, 1:41am #631

Wow, so seeing it in action I now have some thoughts.

  1. Love the HEV-armoured zombies and the exploding houndeyes. Great modifications to make existing enemies more of a threat.

  2. The long jump looks different? It seems to activate after the player jumps, which is weird since jumping from a crouched position it what normally does it. I dunno, maybe I’m stupid.

  3. The level design looks really open. I’m not sure how I feel exactly, because I’m used to HL being a thoroughly mostly-linear game. I guess I like Metroid Prime, that’s another shooter about exploring giant alien worlds and wrecking shit.

  4. The plant switches you have to blow up to move are weird. They’ve got cords coming off them, like they’re electric. Shooting the…seed dildo pod apparently makes them work but also changes the color of the light on the cord? Why would you not just have it die, other than that that cord leads you to the thing it affects. It kind of reminds me of Homestuck’s Alternian computers, where you basically had a sack of green slime and worms with wires in it, that somehow acted exactly like any Earth machine.

  5. Speaking of Metroid Prime, I hate to say this, but the atmosphere feels very…“Hollywood Alien World”? It doesn’t remind me of Xen in HL1, and I get that was the point, but that was from a gameplay perspective. Aesthetically it just doesn’t match? The lush forest and plants everywhere don’t cotton to my perception of Xen as a bunch of floating hemorrhoid shits with live bees shoved into them. It looks a bit more classic near the end and looking out into the big space vista, but it’s still very blue, and Xen is mostly pale green and yellow. Obviously this isn’t gonna change much before release and I don’t really mind that since it’s meant to be a different interpretation anyway, but I felt like it needed to be said. Maybe it’s just that neither Half-Life nor it’s many attempted remakes are monoliths that a consistent vision springs forth from, so if I’m playing a remake of Op4 or BlueShift (or a new Half-Life game, god forbid), the synchronicity of that vision is going to be at least a little awkward.

Maybe other remake devs will just steal assets from this game though, I guess that’s possible.

Regardless, it still looks great, I don’t want any criticism of this to overshadow my praise. I’m extremely excited and honestly can’t believe we actually made it to this point. I joined this forum in like sixth grade after the initial release, and here I am seven years later anticipating the same game all over again.
