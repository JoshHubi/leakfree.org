Lamsey:

Red and green are opposites in subtractive colour (i.e. pigment/ink colour, which is the colour wheel most people think of), so it could be argued that it’s a reasonable assertion. In additive (screen/light) colour, however, red does indeed become cyan when inverted.

Nitpicking of nitpick: It’s got nothing to do with additive versus subtractive, only the colour model. The standard additive primaries used for CRT displays and projectors are red, green and blue which have red and cyan as opposites. The standard subtractive primaries used for printing (check your colour printer cartridge) are the complements of the additive primaries: cyan, magenta and yellow. In this subtractive model, red and cyan are still opposite.
