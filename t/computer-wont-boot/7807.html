Ok, the problem is your bios isn´t posting (that´s why fans are on full, they dont regulate until post).

I doubt it´s the GPU, if it was, you would either hear a bip or it would post regardless, granted you wouldnt see it, but the fans would regulate.

I doubt it´s the ram, as again you´d hear a bip.

I think your best bet is, either PSU or, mobo is done for.

This. As someone else mentioned, you can also try removing/re-inserting the CMOS battery (or replacing it with a new one). But the bottom line is, something is preventing your BIOS from even loading, which is either faulty power (bad PSU, loose power connections - make sure the 20/24 pin and 4/8 pin cables are secure on your motherboard), faulty BIOS or motherboard - either of those cases requires a swap of the mobo.

afterwars November 25, 2018, 12:50am #12

I have had this problem in the past, generally it is caused by either the screen arsing about, or the cable going into the screen (E.g mines a DVI-D)

What you could try is either while the computer is starting up, wriggle the wires a bit, try a different screen for a conclusive result, or after turning on the computer, turn it off again, unplug it and leave it five minutes, this will let the RAM clear it’s cache. Failing that, open it up and reset the bios via the bios reset switch. (if worst comes worst, reinstall windows…)

NOTE: It can easily be caused by a loose wire (either in the pc or to the screen, check the wires…)

Hope this helps!
