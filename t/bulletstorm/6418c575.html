k3nny1550 November 24, 2018, 11:43pm #106

Bulletstorm isn’t necessarily innovative. But then again, what in the FPS genre has been truly innovative? Deus Ex was probably the last time anyone really shook up the genre.

I’ve played through the campaign once and a half, and I may be in the minority here, but I enjoyed myself. The variety came from the various ways to kill the enemies in game- kicking them into spikes, rotating fans, helicopter blades, using a laser whip to bring them towards you and then kick them off a ledge “300” style, sticking them with a grenade and then kicking them into a group of their friends before blowing him up, kicking off pieces of their armor to allow yourself to subsequently mag dump up their asshole, the list goes on.

Is it crass? Yes, it’s got dialog written like a 13 year old trying to rewrite “The Departed” with none of the sophistication and no Jack Nicholson strap-on ad-libs.

Is the main protagonist indefensible? In a way, yes. He mindlessly followed a very obviously evil general and killed some people he shouldn’t have, and also put his crew in danger to get revenge. He regrets it by game’s end in a half-hearted attempt at character development, but Grayson Hunt is still one of gaming’s biggest assholes next to Kratos of God of War fame.

Is it fun? Hell yes. It’s the perfect “got home after a bad day, time to blow shit up” game. It was worth a rent.
