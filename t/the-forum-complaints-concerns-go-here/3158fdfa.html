SkyBlue November 24, 2018, 8:14pm #148

So, seeing as I’ve lurked here since before the big media release in 2007, I feel I ought to add my opinion. I think that there does seem to be a bias against newer members and those with poor English, however I don’t think that can be helped due to the nature of the Internet. However, there are number of issues that other people have raised and I want to agree with:

  1. READ THE FAQ - this gets extremely annoying to read, as well as being unhelpful to people who have just discovered Black Mesa. I can’t remember who suggested it, but if forum members could provide answer and THEN direct people to the FAQ, that would be useful. Seeing as most of the threads like “Got a question? Ask the developers” are over 30 pages long. If there’s only 10 posts per page, that would still be 300 posts to dig through. Can you really expect new people to do that?

  2. Mini-modding - I agree with Catz about Raminator, Ram is a core part of the community and his particular ‘brand’ of humour is part of what makes the boards so enjoyable to read. However, Ram is also a developer and hard at work on the mod. I don’t see why members who are only on the boards should be allowed to get away with acting like a mod.

  3. Flaming/Trolling and Old/New Members - I’ve never seen any forum on the internet where there is such a difference in the treatment of members by how long they’ve been there. Acting against people who troll and/or flame is a must for any forum. However, here it seems like the older members can get away with anything. At the time of writing, Shasta seems to have been banned, but the fact that it took that many posts just on this thread for him to be banned is quite worrying. Another example would be this from Raminator (apologies for not quoting properly, my Firefox doesn’t work properly):
    [COLOR=‘Red’]I have no idea what that’s about, but it’s not restricted to people who’ve posted in this thread. I think it’s based on recent posting activity at a certain time last night when a developer seemed to ban a whole load of people for one day. I’m un-banning them now. Done.
    If one moderator has to un-ban a load of people who shouldn’t have been banned, then the developer who unfairly banned a lot of people should have their modding rights removed.

  4. Release dates and consequent problems - having a release date to aim for is a good means of motivation, and I think that this was a good idea by the development team. However, the video updates are concerning. If you are going to post alpha videos, you should expect people to query what the latest thing is going to look like. Old(er) forum members replying that things will have changed, so don’t complain, is stupid and insulting to the rest of the community. Also, some news on what you are currently doing would make life easier for newer members, even if it was a locked sticky thread simply saying something like:
    “We are currently finishing models, levels and animation. We are also play-testing and removing bugs. The mod will be released when it is ready”.
    (:fffuuu: Although that’s just me guessing at what the progress is!)

Apologies for the wall of text!
