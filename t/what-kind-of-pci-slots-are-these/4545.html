They are indeed regular PCI slots, and I’ve never seen that little connector haha, what make/model of computer is that? There’s another option here that may grant you more performance-for-dollar for your situation - get a USB wireless adapter. The next computer you buy is likely to NOT have PCI slots (as the technology is on its way out the door) but USB 2.0 (which is what you’ll get in a wireless adapter) is compatible with USB 3.0 (which is currently being released in new computers).

In other words, a USB 2.0 wireless adapter will have more longevity than a PCI-slot wireless adapter. If you’re just getting it with the expectation of it lasting for 2 months, then buy whatever you want. In any event, I recommend Linksys or D-link (although most people will recommend getting an adapter that matches your wireless router).
