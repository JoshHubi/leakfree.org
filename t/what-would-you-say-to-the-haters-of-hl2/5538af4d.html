MysticJ November 24, 2018, 11:02pm #98

If someone told me they don’t like Half-Life, I wouldn’t argue with them. I don’t like Tetris, Civilization nor flight simulators, simply because they are not my cup of tea. Even within 1 genre some games fit you more and some fit you less. Doom 3 – Return to Castle Wolfenstein – Half-Life 2 – Far Cry : 4 games, all FPS, but each quite different. Where one goes for the easy scare, the other goes for a stealth and mission objectives, the third one goes for post-apo, human-rich storyline of resistance, and the last I’ve never got to the first objective anyway, so I can’t judge that one

I find a big chunk of wisdom in what the developers say in the commentary to the very first playable part of Episode 2: “Originally, the players had to jump from ledge to ledge on a cliff side. But after the playtesters complained that they died several times during the first 5 minutes, we changed it and went for perceived, rather than actual danger.”

I believe that many games make the fatal mistake of scaring the player (chasing them away) at the very beginning by one of the following:

  • too difficult first level(s)
  • too boring first level(s)

If you remember Warcraft 1 (1994), you may remember that the first mission was surprisingly difficult and had basically only 1 “survivable” play style --> going quickly for barracks, amassing an army before the 2500 gold from first mine was exhausted, and proceeding to second gold mine.
In Warcraft 2 (1996), the first mission is identical, but the first mine now has 10 000 gold, and you don’t need to go look for another mine at all.

That’s a visible “lessons learned” in game design.

Far Cry unfortunately, in my humble opinion, didn’t learn, and I just couldn’t survive the first fight. Maybe I was doing something wrong, but the game didn’t tell me what I should do, and I kept diing, so I quit and uninstalled.

Maybe something similar happened to those who hate Half-life or Half-life 2 – after being cut by laser after R.C., or after falling to their deaths when running away from CPs on the rooftops, or maybe because it took so long to get the first gun… they just quit.
