Flavrans November 25, 2018, 4:52am #3

If you re-enable Steam Cloud again later, it’s possible that Steam will restore the non-working config.cfg again.

Alternatively, try this:

Right click on Black Mesa in your Steam Library and click “Properties”. Then under “General” click “Set Launch Options” and add the following to the launch options: [tt]-autoconfig[/tt]

If the game launches successfully, then go into the Options menu -> Video and check the video settings and change/lower the advanced settings to what they were when the game worked (tips: set Cascade Shadow Map to a lower setting or disable it, or set Shadow Detail to medium).

If [tt]-autoconfig[/tt] doesn’t work, then add [tt]-dev[/tt] to the options and try again.

After you’re done changing the video settings, exit the game and remove [tt]-autoconfig[/tt] (and [tt]-dev[/tt]) from the launch options. Hopefully, the game should continue to work the next time you launch the game.
