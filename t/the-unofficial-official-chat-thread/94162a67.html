Would it hurt more to get attacked by a head crab or a “Face Hugger”? My friend just asked me that…I honestly have no clue. I say head crab probably hurts more.

danielsangeo November 25, 2018, 3:30am #4839

Headcrab, I think, would be worse. A “facehugger” works by gripping the host’s head and injecting its proboscis down the host’s throat to implant its larvae (that would be the correct word for it, right?). Once it’s done its task, then the facehugger detaches and dies, leaving the host implanted with the young but otherwise in full control of his or her body and mental faculties.

A headcrab latches onto the host’s head and the first thing it does it crack open the host’s skull. Then it injects its stuff (whatever it is) into the host’s body and causes the body to mutate including splitting its chest open, breaking all the ribs from the sternum to create a makeshift mouth, and elongating the fingers into claws for protection from attack. It appears, however, that the host is at least somewhat aware of what has happened and continues to be conscious, though not in control, of what is happening.

Therefore, I think it would hurt more for a headcrab to successfully attack you rather than a facehugger. True, the ‘birth’ of the larvae does kill the host painfully but relatively quickly, but if someone doesn’t put the zombies out of their misery, they could go on existing like this for possibly the entire life cycle of the headcrab or host (whichever is longer?).
