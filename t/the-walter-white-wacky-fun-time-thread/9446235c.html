Winged_One November 25, 2018, 3:41am #122

Right now, Walt might be the most tragic character of them all. He made deals with multiple devils, he turned INTO the devil, but now that he’s out his past mistakes still continue to haunt him. He’s tried desperately to move on in his last few months of life, but he keeps getting roped back in. It’s kind of like how Walt kept manipulating Jesse into sticking around despite how it would inevitably hurt him, except this is all Walt’s doing so the tragedy comes in the futility of him trying to do right but never seeming to escape his past rather than having a shitty lot in life and having nowhere else to turn.

At the start of this half of the season, I genuinely hated Walt. I hated how he manipulated others, I hated who he became during the first half of S5, and I hated him for whatever he might do in the future to bring ruin to those surrounding him. Now, seeing that he genuinely wanted out of the business (and even got out for a time) and really only put out a hit on Jesse in last ditch desperation after he kicked down his door and poured gasoline on the family rug (Even then, it took a myriad of continued threats for Walt to put the hit out to protect himself and his family, and even ended up calling it off (though, as I’ve said before, deals done with the devil are always end up being fulfilled)), I feel…well, I wouldn’t call it pity. I’m not sure what I would call it. But I know that he is learning his lesson and is about to be payed back in full with a heaping pile of tragedy.
