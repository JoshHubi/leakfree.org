FireTime November 24, 2018, 11:00pm #13

I strongly recommend against overclocking. The reason being that most laptop coolers are barley adequate for stock frequencies due to the inherent lemited space for proper cooling. The increased heat places strain on the whole system seriously reducing the life of the equipment.

While you have the thing apart don’t forget to clean it out and spend the money on proper thermal compound. A faster processor will run a little hotter (unless it happens t o be a smaller atrecture than your current chip then it may be similar). Just doing those simple things can drop a system 10º.
