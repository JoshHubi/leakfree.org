All the above advice is true. Western Digital generally makes great drives (in 14 years of building PCs, I’ve only ever used WD and Maxtor, and [knock on wood] haven’t had a single drive failure). And with magnetic 7200RPM drives, the cost per gigabyte is so cheap nowadays, as you noticed - you can add an extra 110GB for only $5-10.

I would warn against RAID0 unless you have a reliable and frequent backup system - even if the drives themselves don’t fail, RAID controllers, driver issues, etc. can all affect a RAID. I’m not saying they don’t perform well (used to run two separate RAID0 arrays in my machines, they are fast) but the failure consequences are high. RAID0+1 helps in this regard, but you’re only looking to get 1 drive so we can forget about that

Bottom line - definitely go with a WD, and then, definitely go for the Caviar Black with the highest cache available. Whether you get Sata 3.0 or Sata 6.0 is up to you and your motherboard specs, but capacity is up to you

This guy is a winner.
