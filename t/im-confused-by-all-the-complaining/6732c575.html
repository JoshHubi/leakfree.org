There are two things I have noticed. 1) There are people who defend the Devs and mods. 2) There are people who are impatient and will say anything they can think of to try to get Dev reaction.

There are over 7500 comments on the Steam page that go like this: Is it dead? No. When is it released? https://isblackmesareleased.com. Why is it taking so long? Devs have lives and are doing this for free. Aren’t we entitled to updates? No, shut up. The world will end in 2012 and we won’t get to play it. Again, shut up. Black Mesa Forever? It is not dead. Where can I find information? Emphatic defense of Black Mesa and devs. Random discussion about other mods and people posting fart jokes.

And it continues in this loop.

Frankly, I don’t understand why some of these people are just sitting around staring at the screen and waiting for information or a release date. There are plenty of new PC games and new mod releases every month. And with the sales on Steam, I’m surprised more people aren’t busier. I check back once every three weeks to look at any new files and the repeating comments (hoping the conversations might evolve). I have read the forums and it is good reading. But, I also play the other games I have. I go outside. I work. Life goes on.

I do not care if any information is posted or for the debate between fans. To me, there are a lot of fans that have absolutely killed the mood of this mod. It is like going on a date and having your friends call you every 5 seconds asking if you have kissed yet and why not. Then your other friends text your date and defend your stance on why you haven’t kissed yet. It’s killing the date and the suspense.

For me, the main problem is in trying not to play into the hands of either thought: ‘imminent release’ or ‘being dead’. So, perpetual limbo sets in. I say, do something, it might actually taper off those people who aren’t die hard fans or at least we can get a laugh at their expense for thinking it was coming out tomorrow. We will see them again when it finally is released. It would actually make the greatest April fools joke ever.

I miss the contests. That was the part that saddened me the most. When that stopped, people got antsy and impatient.
