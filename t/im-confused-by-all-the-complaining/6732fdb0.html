Personally, i think that the argument of the team being much smaller is a bit unacceptable and that it’s no excuse for exceeding an expected due day by nearly two years.

The reason i find the argument invalid, is that most developers have a severe cripple in comparison to that of team black mesa. When a developer creates a game, they focus on several things; new sound design, a totally new engine, creating a story/plot design, and level design. TBM suffer from none of the above to a full extent, as they have a predesigned engine (source) which came fully packed with sounds for every physic collision and what have you. They could probably get through making the entire mod without using a single original sound clip (not including voices).

They also had the pleasure of having every single event already planned out in front of them. They knew what the levels should look like, and they knew what scripted events to put where. They didn’t even have to produce their own story.

Some would argue that CALL OF DUTY uses the same engine, so how would i compare TBM to teams at Activision? Well, i wouldn’t, because activision releases a brand new game every single year.

There are faults with this argument, but this very argument is still a catalyst for anger for some people.

That said, i personally am not angry with anybody working on the project for taking so long. Instead, i am just excited. If anything, the long wait has built my interest.
