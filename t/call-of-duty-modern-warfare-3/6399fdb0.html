Do you understand what opinions are? No.

Some opinions are well grounded and some aren’t. An opinion can be wrong in that it can be completely illogical or rely on faulty logic. Contrary to popular belief, an opinion can be logically and intelligently argued; you can give the reasoning behind your opinion and compare it to the reasoning behind the other person’s opinion. If both people’s reasoning is sound and it comes down to personal preference, the argument ends in a stalemate. If one person’s reasoning is shown to be heavily flawed and illogical, the other person wins.

To give an example, someone might say “Coca Cola tastes like shit.” And you say “Why?” It can go two ways. The person may say “Because I don’t like the feel of carbonation in my mouth.” And then it has boiled down to personal preference and the argument is over. However, the person might say “I hate the taste because it has way too much pumpkin juice in it.” Coca Cola does not have any pumpkin juice in it, so although the original statement was nothing more than an opinion, the person is still wrong.
