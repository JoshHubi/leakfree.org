Langenscheiss November 24, 2018, 10:16pm #28

Quantum mechanics are formulated in an abstract Hilbert space that obeys to laws much like the ones know from the usual R^3. This includes the Schwarz inequality. Heisenberg’s uncertainty relation is a direct consequence of this inequality AND NOT A POSTULATE, but a consequence of 6 to 8 postulates of quantum mechanics. And according to this principle, every quantum system has a minimum kinetic energy greater than zero. This is called the zero point energy.

In quantum field theories, like the very succesful quantum electrodynamics, we try to explain the fundamental forces like electromagnetism by quantizing the fields known from the classical theories (like classical electrodynamics). But unlike electrodynamics, we still are not able to formulate a valid quantum field theory for gravity (which would imply a particle called the graviton).

In conclusion, I have no idea how a zero point energy manipulator could achieve tasks the gravity gun is able to perform, nor what a zero point energy field manipulator is. I’m not into quantum field theories (not yet ), but I doubt that the zero point energy is in any way related to controlling gravity (by emitting gravitons, postulated by a hypothetical quantum field theory for gravity).
