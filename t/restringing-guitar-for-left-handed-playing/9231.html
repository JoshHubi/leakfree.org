CPU November 25, 2018, 2:04am #2

You’ll need to replace the nut and buy new strings. (On some guitars you can simply flip the nut around.) Not all guitars can be made to play left handed because of the bridges.

Check the string height by tuning up the strings to pitch and press down on the third fret of each string. When the string is plucked open it should have no buzz in it. Adjust the height until it plays properly.

Loosen the strings and place a few drops of glue under the nut.

Tune the guitar and then set the intonation.

Well, I don’t really recommend this if you don’t know what you are doing but I have wired my guitar amp’s output with my soundboard’s microphone input (they are both 3.5 mm jacks in my case so a single connection is enough, otherwise make sure your transitions aren’t faulty). Be sure not to have too much amplification or you might burn out something on your computer. This is the only way I can keep the quality of the sound. The only problem is that you might get a lot of buzz in some cases, make sure you at least have good quality wires.

Edit: I sometimes have to remove noise and master the sound in Adobe Audition.
