The other interesting thing about hydrogen is that it not only has to be obtained in liquid form, but it has to be manufactured. On Earth, the only natural source of hydrogen gas in large quantities are erupting volcanoes. So hydrogen is, for the most part, manufactured by electrolysis of water. Exactly what it sounds like… running electricity through water to split it into oxygen gas and hydrogen gas. Problem is, it’s an energetically unfavorable reaction, and energy is lost in the process, so making hydrogen consumes more energy than the hydrogen gas can produce.
